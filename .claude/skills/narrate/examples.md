# Narration Examples

## Combat Turn — Human Register

**Payload**: Round 1, Mara uses Attack on the younger bandit. Physical damage 2. No status applied.

**Narration**:
> The younger one is already moving when Mara moves. She catches his wrist on the way down and turns into him and the hatchet goes past her ear. Her knee comes up into his ribs and something gives under the denim. He takes two steps back and sits down on a slab of broken concrete. He does not get up immediately. The older bandit has not fired. He is watching.

## Combat Turn — Eldritch Register

**Payload**: Round 3, Teo uses Power (displacement_short). Player exposed +2, mental damage 1.

**Narration**:
> Teo goes where the door had been and arrives in the corner behind the standing clock. The clock says seven-oh-four. He thinks he has been in this foyer for a minute and a half. He is not sure that is right. The thing in the coat has not turned to face him. His nose is bleeding. The clock says seven-oh-four.

## Scene Framing — Faction Seat

**Payload**: Location Mount Tremper, time dawn, tension uneasy.

**Narration**:
> The room was cold. Preston preferred it cold. The tributary representative stood in the center of the hall and recited the figures for the quarter's tribute. Grain, cattle, hides. The numbers were slightly lower than the previous quarter. Preston did not speak.

## Dialogue — Merchant Register

**Payload**: NPC Mariam Okonkwo, topic trade partnership, standing neutral.

**Narration**:
> The letter was from Mariam Okonkwo. Mariam was inquiring about the altered cattle and wondering if a partnership might be useful, with House Okonkwo providing research funding in exchange for first pricing on future production increases. Marsden read the letter twice. She folded it carefully.

## Key Principles in Practice

- The courier example uses specific distances ("two hundred meters") and physical details ("courier pouch across his back")
- The healer example costs something ("her hands were trembling")
- Violence is described through consequence, not spectacle
- Dialogue uses "said" and "asked" or replaces tags with action
- Weather and temperature are consequential, not decorative

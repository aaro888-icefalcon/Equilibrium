# Combat Reference

## Combat Verbs

Players choose one major action per round:

| Verb | What It Does | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Attack** | Standard physical/weapon attack. Roll vs defense. | Default damage option |
| **Power** | Use a manifestation power. Costs nothing but accumulates use marks. | When powers suit the situation |
| **Assess** | Study a target. Reveals weaknesses, builds momentum. | Information gathering |
| **Maneuver** | Reposition, set up advantage, change zones. Builds momentum. | Tactical positioning |
| **Parley** | Negotiate mid-combat. Only vs humans. Reduces heat. | De-escalation, human enemies |
| **Disengage** | Attempt to flee. Roll required. | When retreat is wise |
| **Finisher** | Devastating attack. Requires: target is Exposed AND 5+ momentum. | End the fight decisively |
| **Defend** | Full defense. Reduces incoming damage this round. | When survival matters more than offense |

## Status Effects

- **Exposed**: Vulnerable to Finisher. Built up through damage.
- **Marked**: Targeted by allies. +2 to hit from mark source.
- **Shaken**: -2 to social/mental actions. From intimidation or shock.
- **Stunned**: Skip next major action. From critical hits.
- **Bleeding**: Take 1 physical damage at end of turn. Stacks.
- **Burning**: Take 1-2 damage at end of turn. From fire/energy.
- **Corrupted**: Eldritch taint. Accumulates toward corruption scale.

## Combat Registers

- **Human**: Heat accrues per enemy. Witnesses multiply heat. Killing vs incapacitating matters.
- **Creature**: Ecological clock. Reinforcements at thresholds. Pack behavior.
- **Eldritch**: Attention clock. Corruption offers on certain verbs. Reality bends.

## Momentum

- Builds on successful actions (critical/full success)
- Spent for: conversion (turn partial into full), reroll, bonus damage
- At 5+ against an Exposed target: Finisher becomes available

## Presenting Combat Choices

When presenting a combat round to the player:
1. Show current round number
2. List enemies with visible state (injured, stunned, etc.)
3. Show player condition (tracks, statuses, momentum)
4. Present available verbs with brief context on what makes sense
5. Ask for verb + target selection

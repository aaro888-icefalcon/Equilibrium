# GEOGRAPHY OF THE MID-ATLANTIC AT T+1

## The Corridor at T+1

The mid-Atlantic at game-start is not the pre-Onset mid-Atlantic shrunk by two-thirds of its population. It is a different geography, and the difference is structural.

Pre-Onset, the region was defined by infrastructure: the I-95 corridor from DC to New York, an integrated rail network, the ports of Baltimore and Philadelphia and New York, the Bos-Wash megalopolis reaching north. Infrastructure bound the region into a single economic and political zone. Most of that infrastructure does not work now. I-95 is walkable; the Lincoln Tunnel is a ruin; the Amtrak trains stopped at 14:23 UTC on Onset Day and rusted where they stopped. The airports are grass. The ports are smaller than they were but survived better than the airports — water transport is the region's functional heavy-goods infrastructure.

What binds the region now are rivers and the people who travel beside them. The Delaware from Lehigh to Philadelphia to Wilmington. The Hudson from Yonkers to Bear-House to the Catskills. The Chesapeake from Delmarva across to Baltimore and its tributaries. The ocean connecting Delmarva to Philadelphia via the Delaware Bay. What does not bind are the highways — roads are dangerous and slow and tax-able.

Four zones dominate the region by population and political weight: **DC and environs**, with Fed Continuity claiming institutional legitimacy over 1.9 million; **the NYC metro in its fractured form**, with Tower Lords, Staten Citadel, Iron Crown, Queens Commonage, and the Species C and H clusters holding distinct pieces; **the Philadelphia-Central Jersey corridor**, the region's commercial center; and **the Delmarva peninsula**, the region's granary. Orbiting those four are the Hudson Valley under Catskill Throne suzerainty; the Lehigh Valley; the Chesapeake under Crabclaw; and the Pine Barrens, which is not a zone in the political sense but a growing non-zone — territory functionally lost to the Listening.

Sixteen million people, spread unevenly across four hundred thousand square kilometers, organized into two hundred and ninety factions of which twenty-two are regionally significant. Cities are smaller; countryside is thicker. The shape of settlement has inverted. The rural population, near-vanished pre-Onset, is the stable population now; the urban core is the ruin.

## Regions

### Catskills and Upper Hudson

*Character.* Cold, forested, defensible. The Catskills are mountains and passes, and Preston holds the passes. The upper Hudson below the Catskills is the Bear-House's reach, centered on Peekskill's reactor. The corridor is the most politically hierarchical in the region — tributary relationships flow upward toward Mount Tremper; the Throne's word is functionally law along its reach.

*Population.* ~530,000 in the Catskills and upper-Hudson combined (Catskills population increased 33% from pre-Onset baseline due to refugee absorption). Bear-House alone ~420,000.

*Factions.* [catskill-throne] (direct ~180k; tributary reach over Bear-House, Yonkers Compact, several smaller polities); [bear-house]; [yonkers-compact] (lower-Hudson, tributary); the Hollow Choir (eldritch; contested territory).

*Key locations.* [loc-mount-tremper], [loc-peekskill-reactor], [loc-yonkers-council-chamber], [loc-kingston-hv], [loc-rhinebeck], [loc-kaaterskill-falls], [loc-hollow-choir-range].

*Travel.* River travel along the Hudson is functional and taxed at Yonkers, Peekskill, and passing the Catskill pass tolls. Road travel is slower and patrolled; Catskill Throne retinue presence deters banditry in the upper reaches. Kaaterskill Falls is a day's travel from the passes. Cross-mountain travel westward is possible but slow.

*Resources.* Hudson River transport; Catskill timber; Peekskill reactor electricity (~10,000 household equivalents); Yonkers biokinetic agriculture surplus; mountain agriculture (modest); bear-house matter-manipulator fabrication.

*Features.* The Catskill passes (defensible choke points); Peekskill reactor (unique); the Hollow Choir range (avoided); Mount Tremper command site; seasonal weather more severe than the corridor average.

*Encounters.* Wretch packs on the Catskill perimeter; Hunter packs deeper in; occasional Aberrants at the eastern valleys' edge near Hollow Choir territory. Warped: Grey Walker wanders the southern Catskills; Grey-on-Grey and the Five Sisters hold packs in the interior; the Drowned Mother is a T10 rumor in the eastern Catskills.

### NYC Metro and Long Island

*Character.* Ruin and archipelago. Manhattan is a salvage-frontier at its north and a closed anomalous zone at its south. Brooklyn is five to six Tower Lord districts that shift alliances perpetually. Queens is divided between Commonage and Cluster (Species C) and Tower Lord reach. Staten Island is Chen's fortified harbor. Long Island further east is scattered small communities, some integrated into one polity or another, some independent.

*Population.* ~4.89M in the outer boroughs + Long Island; ~250k in Manhattan core (84% loss, the region's highest); ~480k Queens Commonage + ~178k Flushing-Edison Cluster + ~1.1M Tower Lord districts + ~260k Staten.

*Factions.* [tower-lords-brooklyn]; [queens-commonage]; [staten-citadel]; [flushing-edison-cluster] (Species C); minor Species H enclaves in outer Queens; various small polities and independents (Coney Island holdout; LaGuardia cult); the Echo (anomalous, Lower Manhattan).

*Key locations.* [loc-manhattan-midtown], [loc-financial-district], [loc-central-park-wildland], [loc-brooklyn-tower-districts], [loc-flushing-workshop], [loc-queens-commonage], [loc-staten-citadel], [loc-jamaica-bay-fishery], [loc-coney-island-holdout], [loc-cross-queens-line], [loc-laguardia-ruin].

*Travel.* Harbor travel is Staten Citadel-controlled and taxed. Bridge travel is fragmentary (several functional bridges; several collapsed). Cross-borough movement requires knowledge of which Tower Lord holds which block and what customs apply at which checkpoints. Subway tunnels are mostly impassable (water intrusion; collapse).

*Resources.* Manhattan salvage (declining yields); harbor trade (Staten toll); fine crafts (Cluster); urban agriculture (Commonage); Jamaica Bay fishery (contested).

*Features.* Kinetic corruption zones in Tower Lord districts; Central Park Wildland; Financial District anomalous zone; Cross-Queens Line fortifications.

*Encounters.* Altered fauna (Central Park; Glass-spined hogs in Bronx periphery); Shades in Lower East Side ruins; the Reaper's raider band in Cross-Bronx; occasional Wretch packs in less-patrolled outer Queens; Aberrant contact near Financial District border.

### Newark / Jersey City

*Character.* Port and warlord-seat. Iron Crown territory runs from Port Newark south through Elizabeth and east to Jersey City waterfront. The harbor is functional and heavily garrisoned. Pre-Onset industrial infrastructure is largely intact though operating at fractional capacity.

*Population.* ~740,000.

*Factions.* [iron-crown]; minor Species H enclaves (Paterson, Passaic — not under Iron Crown control, hostile relationship after T+4-month incident).

*Key locations.* [loc-port-newark-compound], [loc-jersey-city-waterfront], [loc-elizabeth-salvage-yards], [loc-liberty-state-park-staging].

*Travel.* Road travel within Iron Crown territory is patrolled (safety contingent on Crown toleration). Harbor travel requires Iron Crown or Staten negotiation. North-south travel along the corridor toward Paterson is officially Iron Crown-permitted but in practice fraught with Species H network interception.

*Resources.* Port Newark deep-water harbor; industrial salvage; some biokinetic agriculture in the outskirts.

*Features.* Heavy kinetic corruption in Volk's immediate territory; dread-field zone extends from Volk wherever he is (fortunately, usually at the port).

*Encounters.* Iron Crown patrols are more dangerous than wildlife. Crown captains enforce territory. Species H couriers appear briefly along routes. Night-walker raccoons are a nuisance in abandoned suburbs.

### Central NJ Corridor

*Character.* Federated townships; agricultural; academic. The CJL runs from Princeton to Brick, fifteen member townships. Princeton University functions as an intellectual and archival center. Rutgers runs the regional biokinetic training program. Edison is a Species C sub-cluster.

*Population.* ~1.69M total; ~780k in CJL member townships.

*Factions.* [central-jersey-league]; [flushing-edison-cluster] (Edison as Accord member); [lehigh-jersey-gs] (Princeton-New Brunswick corridor concentration).

*Key locations.* [loc-princeton-accord-hall], [loc-rutgers-nb], [loc-princeton-horse-lands], [loc-edison-township], [loc-toms-river], [loc-trenton].

*Travel.* Road travel within CJL is well-organized; Accord Council maintains inter-township postal service. North-south travel into Philadelphia (via Trenton) is reliable. Travel toward Pine Barrens perimeter is increasingly dangerous south of Toms River.

*Resources.* Biokinetic agriculture (among region's most efficient); Princeton Library; Rutgers training; Edison Species C craft; cattle and dairy.

*Features.* Princeton Horse Lands (Warped treaty territory); altered biokinetic fields visibly greener than baseline.

*Encounters.* Wretches at southern CJL perimeter (Toms River vicinity); occasional Hunter packs in Pine Barrens buffer zone; Warped (White Horse of Princeton + associates; Corvid Council of Princeton).

### Philadelphia and Environs

*Character.* Commercial center. The Bourse's copper defines the region's economy. Fairmount Park is a biokinetic-altered wildland containing the Wissahickon Green Hive colony. Camden across the river is Species I Herrera-kin concentration plus House Pereira salvage operation. South Philly Holding garrisons the Navy Yard and wharves on Bourse contract.

*Population.* ~2.52M metro; ~180k Camden.

*Factions.* [philadelphia-bourse]; [south-philly-holding]; [species-i-diaspora] (Herrera-kin + Marquez-kin); [the-broker] (eldritch, Camden-side salvage sites).

*Key locations.* [loc-old-market-philly], [loc-bourse-library], [loc-delaware-wharves], [loc-philadelphia-navy-yard], [loc-fairmount-park], [loc-camden-nj].

*Travel.* Delaware River traffic; road approaches from Central NJ and Delmarva; Camden bridges (functional). Philadelphia is the best-connected transit hub in the region.

*Resources.* Bourse copper and commercial arbitrage; Navy Yard salvage; Species I labor and healer networks; Fairmount biokinetic-altered flora (licensed).

*Features.* Bourse printing press and library; Wissahickon Green containment operations; Camden's divided character; Doctor Pale's hidden operation (Camden); The Broker's salvage-site manifestations.

*Encounters.* Urban crime; occasional Hive incidents at Fairmount perimeter; Bourse enforcement by South Philly Holding; in Camden, the full range from civil commerce to Doctor Pale's predations.

### Lehigh Valley and Pocono Approaches

*Character.* Coal and principalities. The Lehigh Valley is twelve minor principalities with ~120k population centered on Allentown. The only functional coal production in the region runs here, biokinetic-supplemented. Species G integration is deepest here of any regional species.

*Population.* ~990,000.

*Factions.* [lehigh-principalities]; [lehigh-jersey-gs] (integrated); Old Badger (Warped, treaty); Susquehanna Crown (Hive, northern front).

*Key locations.* [loc-allentown], [loc-bethlehem-steelworks-ruin], [loc-carbon-county-mine], [loc-jim-thorpe], [loc-susquehanna-crown-border], [loc-lehigh-valley-interior].

*Travel.* Road-based. The Delaware River corridor reaches the Valley via the Lehigh River confluence. Travel northward into the Poconos is seasonal.

*Resources.* Coal (primary export); pre-Onset industrial metal salvage (Bethlehem); Species G agricultural work; seed development.

*Features.* Active coal mining; biokinetic soil rehabilitation on strip mine tailings; Old Badger territory (treaty-protected); Susquehanna Crown Hive front (active).

*Encounters.* Hive incidents on the Susquehanna border; occasional Wretch pack in deep Poconos; Old Badger if treaty crossed; general rural hazard.

### Crabclaw / Baltimore / Chesapeake North

*Character.* Three-warlord confederation at Baltimore; fisheries the dominant economic driver. Defended against Fed Continuity northward pressure; commercial toward Delmarva and Bourse.

*Population.* ~1.08M (Chesapeake North + Baltimore metro).

*Factions.* [crabclaw-confederation] (Inner Harbor + Dundalk + Towson lords); Grandfather Silt (eldritch, Nanticoke River mouth, no-go zone); the Salt Judge (Warped, bay-scale territorial).

*Key locations.* [loc-inner-harbor-baltimore], [loc-dundalk], [loc-fort-mchenry-ruin], [loc-chesapeake-bay-north].

*Travel.* Chesapeake Bay traffic; overland to Philadelphia (via Delaware); hostile border with Fed Continuity southward.

*Resources.* Biokinetic-altered fishery (feeds a quarter of the region); industrial salvage (Dundalk); harbor commerce.

*Features.* The Inner Harbor promenade (converted to trading quay); Fort McHenry ruin (Crabclaw defensive line + neutral meeting ground); the Nanticoke no-go zone.

*Encounters.* Crabclaw enforcement; Salt Judge territorial enforcement against netting violations; Ironmouth crocodilians (tributaries); occasional eldritch incident at the no-go perimeter.

### DC / Fed Core

*Character.* The largest coherent polity by population; institutional rather than warlord; eroding. Fed Continuity holds the Pentagon-Andrews-Quantico triangle plus scattered outer districts. Reeve's Potomac Irregulars hold Virginia suburbs.

*Population.* ~4.80M in the DC core + scattered districts.

*Factions.* [fed-continuity]; [potomac-irregulars] (nominally subordinate; practically independent); [species-i-diaspora] (Diaz-kin Anacostia, Vasquez-kin Anacostia/PG County, Flores-kin PG County).

*Key locations.* [loc-pentagon], [loc-andrews-afb], [loc-quantico-periphery], [loc-georgetown-library], [loc-dc-mall], [loc-arlington-cemetery], [loc-anacostia], [loc-pg-county-md], [loc-dc-potomac-district].

*Travel.* Road-based; Fed Continuity maintains regular patrol on main approaches. Small-arms violence possible along Irregular-Species I borders.

*Resources.* Pre-Onset federal archives; conventional small arms; scrip printing; Species I healer networks (covertly being negotiated for levy).

*Features.* The National Mall's partially restored civic spaces; the Pentagon's inner rings (partially operational); Arlington Cemetery (maintained symbolically); Anacostia kin-neighborhoods.

*Encounters.* Fed Continuity patrols; occasional Irregular interference at borders; rare eldritch (the DC area has the lowest eldritch density in the region).

### Delmarva Peninsula

*Character.* The granary. Six major Harvest Lords plus ~12 minor; no unified command. Agricultural production among the region's most efficient. Lowest mortality (12% Y1) of any regional zone. Relatively safe from eldritch pressure.

*Population.* ~1.15M.

*Factions.* [delmarva-harvest-lords] (Marsden, Chen-Samuel, Archer, Velasquez, Finch, Brother Tam, plus minor lords); [species-i-diaspora] (Velasquez cross-tie).

*Key locations.* [loc-easton-md], [loc-denton-md], [loc-chestertown-md], [loc-salisbury-md], [loc-rehoboth-beach-de], [loc-monastery-north-dover].

*Travel.* Road-based within the peninsula; harbor travel along Chesapeake and Delaware bays; good commercial infrastructure by regional standards.

*Resources.* Grain, dairy, vegetables, fisheries, altered cattle (Marsden), seed bank (Chen-Samuel), biokinetic-altered crops; the region's primary food export.

*Features.* Fields visibly greener than pre-Onset; Archer's demesne noticeably calm (Auratic zone); Brother Tam's monastery.

*Encounters.* Minimal by regional standards. Rare Wretch contact. Chesapeake Bog Hive incidents along the western shore. Salt Judge enforcement of fishery zones.

### Pine Barrens

*Character.* Not a zone in the political sense but a growing non-zone. The Listening holds the interior; the Hive occupies the Batsto core (50,000 tons, 2,400 km²); the border is the Black Pines Line moving slowly outward.

*Population.* ~70,000 humans total; ~35,000 cult-members plus uncounted transformed.

*Factions.* [the-listening]; the Batsto Cluster (eldritch); the Deep-Root (Hive); The Lost Thing (T10 warped/eldritch anomaly); occasional Warped (Nine-Tail, others on perimeter).

*Key locations.* [loc-batsto-village], [loc-wharton-forest-reserve], [loc-ongs-hat], [loc-black-pines-line], [loc-pine-barrens-perimeter], [loc-pine-barrens-mobile].

*Travel.* Dangerous. Border settlements maintain scouts; beyond the Black Pines Line, travel is risk-limited. Listening messengers appear at the perimeter periodically.

*Resources.* None accessible to non-cultists.

*Features.* The Sprawl/Deep-Root; One Who Waits's ceremonial grounds; The Lost Thing's unpredictable movement; Ong's Hat anomalous events.

*Encounters.* Wretch packs at the perimeter; Hunters deeper; Aberrants in core territory; Listening messengers; the One Who Waits (rare, significant); the Lost Thing (rare, unpredictable).

## Travel and Trade

Long-distance travel in the mid-Atlantic is slow and consequential. A courier can cover 40–60 km per day on foot; a horse-courier 60–100 km per day; a river boat 40–80 km per day depending on current. The regional courier network (Species H formal courier service; Bourse-contracted couriers; independents) charges roughly 50 copper for a 100-km guaranteed delivery. Escort services charge 15 copper per day.

The primary trade flows follow rivers and bay traffic:

**The Delaware Corridor.** Philadelphia to Lehigh to upstream sources. The region's primary industrial artery. Coal flows south; Bourse-goods flow north; grain moves through both directions depending on season.

**The Chesapeake Bay Trade.** Delmarva to Crabclaw Baltimore; Crabclaw to Philadelphia (via ocean and Delaware). The longest sustainable water route. Takes a week in good weather.

**The Route 95 Fragments.** Fed Continuity Baltimore-DC corridor plus parts of the NJ Turnpike plus I-95 into Philadelphia. Usable with armed escort and horse transport. Dangerous without both. Fed Continuity patrols the southern portion; the northern portion is bandit-prone.

**The Hudson Valley Route.** Yonkers to Bear-House to Catskill Throne. River-based, tributed, relatively safe. Catskill Throne tolls at the passes.

**The North Jersey Courier Network.** Species H-operated. Not a commercial route per se; it functions as an information network that commercial operators pay for.

The eight top internal trade flows by gravity model (arbitrary units):

1. Philadelphia Bourse ↔ South Philly Holding (72.5)
2. Fed Continuity ↔ Potomac Irregulars (68.1, diminishing)
3. Staten Citadel ↔ Tower Lords Brooklyn (39.4)
4. Iron Crown ↔ Tower Lords Brooklyn (24.0)
5. Crabclaw ↔ Fed Continuity (7.2, reduced due to border hostility)
6. Philadelphia Bourse ↔ Lehigh Principalities (5.7)
7. Philadelphia Bourse ↔ Central Jersey League (5.5)
8. Yonkers Compact ↔ Bear-House (4.0)

Primary commodities and their price behavior: grain is the regional standard and trades tightly at Bourse prices; coal peaks in late autumn as winter demand rises; pre-Onset medicine commands extreme premiums and fluctuates wildly; biokinetic healing services are contracted, not priced in coin; small-matter crafts (Cluster goods) trade at high per-unit prices with long production lead-times; horses are always expensive because transport matters.

## Danger Geography

The regional danger map is not uniform. Certain zones are effectively safe; others are effectively lost; most exist on a gradient.

**Low-danger zones.** Delmarva agricultural interior; CJL township cores; Yonkers Compact core; DC/Pentagon/Andrews triangle; Bourse-garrisoned commercial centers; Bear-House demesne; Mount Tremper approach route. Travel safety in these zones approaches pre-Onset norms.

**Moderate-danger zones.** Most of the NJ Turnpike corridor; inter-Principality routes in Lehigh; outer Yonkers Compact; Delaware Bay shipping lanes; Chesapeake main channels; CJL southern townships. Armed escort recommended for valuable goods; single travelers usually safe.

**High-danger zones.** Manhattan salvage corridors (varies by district); Brooklyn inter-Tower transitions; Iron Crown-Staten contested waters; Cross-Bronx corridor; Pine Barrens outer perimeter; Crabclaw-Fed Continuity border; southern Catskill valleys. Armed escort necessary; single travelers expect trouble.

**Extreme-danger zones.** Lower Manhattan (Financial District); Pine Barrens interior; Hollow Choir valleys; Grandfather Silt's Chesapeake range; Batsto Sprawl; Susquehanna Crown front; the Lost Thing's movement. Entry for non-specialists is generally a fatal error.

Regional beast-encounter rates, per bible Part XXVII sensitivity analysis: Delmarva agricultural ~1 per 30 travel-days (fatal ~1 per 1,000). CJL township corridors ~1 per 14 (fatal ~1 per 500). Catskill patrolled roads ~1 per 10 (fatal ~1 per 300). Catskill wilderness ~1 per 3 (fatal ~1 per 80). Pine Barrens perimeter ~1 per 2 (fatal ~1 per 40). Pine Barrens interior ~1 per day (fatal ~1 per 15). Manhattan ruins ~1 per 2 (fatal ~1 per 60).

## The Vertical Slice: Central NJ as Starting Region

For campaigns starting in the Central NJ corridor, the sub-regional geography is worth resolving at higher detail.

**Princeton.** The CJL Accord Hall is here, in the converted Nassau Hall on the former Princeton University campus. The university itself is functional — faculty live in former academic housing, students attend lectures, the library is open. The horse lands of the White Horse of Princeton are south of town, a day's walk, bordered by stone markers the treaty specifies. Dr. Okafor's offices and private quarters are adjacent to the Accord Hall. Her working days begin before sunrise. Quarterly Accord Council meetings fill the town with representatives from all fifteen member townships; the inns are full; commerce briefly spikes.

**New Brunswick.** The Rutgers-New Brunswick biokinetic training center. Five hundred residents and more rotating through. Fields that were athletic fields are now instruction plots. Students come from Philadelphia, from Lehigh, occasionally from Delmarva. The Ethnography Department under Professor Afolabi keeps offices here; the Warped-contact correspondence (Sparrowhand, other T8 fox) runs through his office.

**Edison.** The Species C sub-cluster township. Full CJL Accord member. Roughly 80,000 residents, 15% Species C, organized under Twelve Hands sub-council authority. Edison's workshops produce for Bourse commercial contract. Queens Commonage maintains close trade connection. The sub-council chamber is above Edison's central market, mirroring Master Kuan's Flushing arrangement.

**Perth Amboy.** Northern CJL township. Port access on the Raritan. Fisheries; some salvage from pre-Onset industrial sites. Less academically developed than Princeton; more commercially active than New Brunswick.

**Old Bridge and East Brunswick.** Inner CJL townships. Agricultural. Most residents never travel outside Middlesex County. Mid-range Bourse copper economy; township meetings weekly; biokinetic agriculture fully integrated into local life.

**Freehold and Red Bank.** Middle-distance from Princeton. Mixed agricultural and small-manufacturing. Red Bank's position on the Navesink gives it minor coastal trade access. Freehold is the pre-Onset county seat and retains some civic infrastructure.

**Toms River.** Southern CJL anchor. Population ~35,000. Proximate to the Pine Barrens perimeter (less than a day's ride to the Black Pines Line). Active militia training here specifically against Listening expansion. The atmosphere is more tense than in the northern CJL townships — residents know what the Pine Barrens is and what is in it.

**Brick.** Southeasternmost CJL member. Small (~18,000). Beach-and-bay access. Fishing and salvage economy; vulnerable to Listening border raids (one attempted; repelled with losses).

**Trenton.** Western anchor. Population ~22,000. The Mapmaker of Trenton operates from here, anonymously; commissions through intermediaries. The pre-Onset state capitol is partially functional as a township meeting space. Trenton's position at the confluence of Delaware and CJL road networks makes it a travel hub disproportionate to its population.

**Cranbury.** Small township, not a major Accord member. Located strategically between Princeton and Edison; acts as a rest stop for travelers on the Princeton-New Brunswick route.

Beyond the CJL townships proper, the central NJ area includes Species H enclave concentrations in northern New Jersey (Paterson, Passaic, Union — outside CJL but commercially linked); Species G Princeton-corridor integration; Quaker and pre-Onset religious communities in outlying areas; and small independent farming settlements that have not affiliated with any larger polity.

The starting region for a campaign rooted here offers: commerce with Bourse orientation; CJL civic politics; Species I diplomacy (Herrera-kin Camden is ~100 km southwest); biokinetic training access; Warped diplomacy at Princeton; growing concern about Pine Barrens Expansion; and easy narrative transit to Philadelphia (1-2 days south), Newark/Iron Crown (1-2 days north, hostile), Bourse country proper (1-2 days south), and the Pine Barrens border (1 day southeast). The region is safe enough that newcomers can establish themselves; close enough to multiple dangerous frontiers that escalating stakes are plausible within the first arcs.

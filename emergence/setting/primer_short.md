# Emergence — Short Primer

Four years ago, on a Thursday afternoon in May 2024, something happened.
Every electronic device in the mid-Atlantic United States stopped working
at once. Roughly ninety-five percent of people in the region felt
something change inside them. Over the following days, most of them
discovered they could do things they could not do before — push objects
without touching them, heal wounds with their hands, hear thoughts, cast
zones of trust or dread, teleport, see in the dark, reshape metal, read
intent.

Nobody knows what caused it. No theory has evidence. It is called the
Onset.

By the end of the first year, about thirty-four million people were dead —
two-thirds of the region's pre-Onset population. The sixteen million who
lived now hold a region stitched together from about twenty-two major
factions and three hundred smaller polities. Some factions are remnants
of the federal government. Some are merchant consortia. Some are warlord
retinues. Some are metahuman cluster-communities whose members share a
heritable post-Onset transformation.

About one in ten survivors did not only manifest powers but changed into
something species-distinct — ten transformations in total, four of them
concentrated in the mid-Atlantic. One in a hundred changed further, into
things that are no longer people. Some of those haunt empty zones. Some
of them speak.

Your character is a survivor. You have at least one supernatural ability.
You are embedded in a job that uses your skills or your powers or both.
You have allies, rivals, debts, and obligations. You will take contracts
that involve real physical danger. You will make choices. The engine
resolves outcomes; the narrator describes them; you decide what your
character tries.

The world has not settled. It may change again.

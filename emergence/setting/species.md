# THE TEN METAHUMAN SPECIES

Each of the ten species is the product of Onset transformation acting on approximately 1% of pre-Onset humanity per species. Primary cluster regions are the areas of densest population and fullest cultural coherence; outside the primaries, each species exists as diaspora of varying depth. The mid-Atlantic has one diaspora concentration (Species I) and three secondary clusters (C, H, G); the other six species are minimal presences here. Species are listed alphabetically rather than by regional importance.

---

## SPECIES A — "The Hollow-Boned"

**Overview.** A East Asian primary-cluster species — Yangtze basin and Korean peninsula — with reduced bone density, extended limbs, and a species-typical temporal-spatial sensitivity that manifests as micro-precognition of physical harm. Global population pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 46%. Mid-Atlantic presence is minimal: roughly 46,000 scattered across the region with no coherent settlement concentration.

**Physical form.** Taller and leaner than baseline humans. An adult weighs 60–70% of a baseline human of equivalent height. Limbs are slightly extended; the torso is narrower. Bones break easier; falls do more damage. Movement efficiency is notably higher — an A can run longer on less food, require less sleep, and tolerate thin air that baseline humans struggle in. Skin tone and facial features vary within East Asian norms.

**Abilities.** The species-typical capacity is micro-precognition — fractions of a second of physical-threat foresight, enough to flinch out of the way of thrown or swung objects they could not see. In individual manifestation this baseline is augmented to various Perceptual and Temporal-Spatial profiles; A manifesters often develop into high-Perceptual or Temporal specialists because the species-typical substrate favors it. Combat-manifested As tend to be evasion-focused rather than damage-focused.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset population distribution concentrated in Yangtze basin with extension up the Korean peninsula. Primary-cluster A communities emerged organically as survivors coordinated.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: East Asia (Yangtze + Korea). Minor diasporas: South Asian coastal cities; scattered North American enclaves.

**Population.** Mid-Atlantic: ~46,000. Distributed through pre-Onset East Asian immigrant-descended communities without coherent settlement concentration. Most As in the mid-Atlantic are individuals or small family groups within larger mixed populations.

**Cultural patterns.** Primary-cluster A communities organize around clan structures based on Onset-survivor lineage — which Onset Day a family's ancestors survived is a marker of internal status. Diaspora pockets tend toward integrated-trade rather than warlord roles; they are seldom military sovereigns and often merchants, specialists, or diplomats. Mid-Atlantic presence is too thin for distinctive cultural practice at community scale.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Integrated and largely unnoticed. Because the species-typical signature is internal (metabolism and sub-second precognition), a casual observer cannot tell a baseline human from an A. Cohabitation is routine.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Baseline-A pairings produce baseline offspring approximately 80% of the time and A offspring approximately 20%. A-A pairings (rare outside primary cluster) produce A offspring reliably. Cross-species pairings (A x another metahuman species) almost always fail; the 95% cross-species reproductive failure rate applies.

**Internal factions.** Not applicable at mid-Atlantic scale. In primary-cluster East Asia, A polities include merchant-republic traditions and several clan-federations; these are not reproduced in the mid-Atlantic's small population.

**Current political situation.** Politically insignificant at regional scale. Individual As are occasionally found in specialist roles (merchants, scouts, couriers) within other polities.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic A individuals.]

**Narrative register.** An A in a mid-Atlantic scene is unmarked physically at first glance. What you notice is the slightness of their motion, the way they react to thrown objects a beat before baseline humans, the lean quickness. They do not announce themselves. They are where they are because their movement efficiency and risk-avoidance let them be.

## SPECIES B — "The Deep-Voiced"

**Overview.** A South Asian primary-cluster species — Gangetic plain — with expanded chest cavity, enlarged vocal apparatus, and the species-typical capacity to produce sounds (voice and sub-vocal infrasonic) baseline humans cannot. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 36% — the second-hardest-hit species, reflecting collapse conditions in the primary cluster. Mid-Atlantic presence minimal: ~44,000 in metropolitan immigrant-descended communities.

**Physical form.** Broader-framed than baseline humans, particularly through the chest and upper torso. The enlarged vocal apparatus is visible externally as a more prominent throat structure. Voices are lower on average than baseline; speech carries further; song is strikingly resonant.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: voice carries 3–5× baseline distance, sub-vocal infrasonic production capability (deliberate subsonic effects for disorientation or long-range signaling), and enhanced hearing. Speech carries auratic weight — Bs are disproportionately effective at persuasion and command. Individual manifestation usually augments these into higher-Auratic, Perceptual (auditory), or broadband communication profiles.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in the Gangetic plain. Collapse conditions in the primary cluster were particularly severe — infrastructure dependencies that elsewhere survived (water, food distribution) failed more completely in northern India, leading to high Y7 mortality.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: South Asia (Gangetic plain; extensions into the Deccan). Minor diasporas: East Africa; parts of the Arabian peninsula; scattered global enclaves including US East Coast.

**Population.** Mid-Atlantic: ~44,000. Concentrated in pre-Onset South Asian immigrant-descended communities — parts of outer Queens, Jersey City (before Iron Crown consolidation; many fled), suburbs of Philadelphia, small enclaves in DC.

**Cultural patterns.** Heavy cultural investment in oral tradition. Primary-cluster B communities maintain elaborate song-and-recitation practices; regional great powers in post-Onset India are often B-dominated polities with remarkable internal coordination (leaders can command at distance without radio). The mid-Atlantic B community preserves sung-prayer practices and is known locally as "the singers" — recognizable at a district scale even when individuals are not.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Culturally marked but not separated. The distinctive vocal register is recognizable; most baseline humans who have lived near B communities are accustomed to it. B orators occasionally find themselves in cross-faction mediation roles because their voices hold rooms.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Same as Species A (80% baseline, 20% metahuman from mixed; ~5% cross-species success).

**Internal factions.** Not politically significant in mid-Atlantic. Primary-cluster B politics include several song-lineage federations, which do not transfer to diaspora context.

**Current political situation.** No polity. Individual Bs occasionally in mediator, priest, or orator roles.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic B individuals.]

**Narrative register.** A B in a scene speaks in a register a baseline human notices. The voice carries in ways that don't match the speaker's posture; the pitch settles lower than expected. In a song or a sermon the effect is amplified — rooms quiet when a B sings, not because the song commands silence but because the sound fills the space in a way baseline voices do not.

## SPECIES C — "The Silver-Hand"

**Overview.** An East Asian primary-cluster species — Japan, Korea, coastal China — with characteristic metallic sheen on palmar skin and innate matter-manipulation capability expressed through the hands. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 53%. **Mid-Atlantic SECONDARY CLUSTER** (~178,000, 1.1% of regional population), concentrated in Flushing, Sunset Park, Edison.

**Physical form.** Visually near-human with one distinctive feature — a silvery or platinum metallic sheen on palmar skin, extending from fingertips to inner wrist. The sheen intensifies with extended matter-manipulation use; elder C artisans have hands silvered well past the wrist. Otherwise facial features and build vary within East Asian norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: hands can reshape non-living material through contact — metal, stone, wood, glass. The capacity does not scale to combat (a C cannot throw bolts of manipulated matter), but in craft production it is unmatched. A C working metal can smooth surfaces, true edges, fine-tune tolerances that no baseline craftsman could match. Individual manifestation augments to higher Matter/Energy tiers, usually with Perceptual secondary (sensing material properties).

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in Japan, Korea, and coastal China. The secondary mid-Atlantic cluster drew on pre-Onset immigrant depth in Queens and Brooklyn and on a smaller New Jersey population.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: East Asia. Mid-Atlantic: Flushing (Queens), Sunset Park (Brooklyn), Edison township (NJ).

**Population.** ~178,000 in mid-Atlantic, distributed across the three settlement districts. Coherent cross-district coordination.

**Cultural patterns.** Organized around the **Twelve Hands** — a rotating council of master-artisans drawn from workshops across the three districts. The Twelve Hands bind all Cluster members on trade and external relations; internal workshop matters are left to individual masters. Apprenticeship is the defining life-structure — a young C enters a workshop in adolescence and works through a multi-decade training. Craft lineages are tracked; a watch signed by a specific master carries the endorsement of that master's lineage.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Commercial and cooperative. The Cluster is integrated into the regional economy as the premier source of fine craft and restoration. Bourse houses pay premium prices for Cluster work; wealthy clients across factions commission it. Cross-species tensions are low because the Cluster has not sought territorial or political expansion — it has remained focused on craft.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard metahuman profile. In the Cluster, C-C pairings are common (the community is large enough to support endogamy where desired); C offspring rates are accordingly higher than for scattered diaspora species.

**Internal factions.** Flushing (most traditional; Master Kuan's seat), Sunset Park (commercial; trade-oriented), Edison (most integrated with baseline society; CJL-member township). Differences are stylistic rather than oppositional; the Twelve Hands bridge them.

**Current political situation.** Stable. The Cluster has declined Fed Continuity's approach for weapons-production capability. The Cluster maintains formal alliance with Queens Commonage and informal participation in CJL (through Edison). Commercially paramount in the region for craft.

**Notable individuals.** npc-iwo-kuan (Flushing master, Twelve Hands senior, T5); npc-tanaka (Bourse-associated master, T5); npc-chen-samuel (Delmarva Harvest Lord; Species C integration into agricultural nobility).

**Narrative register.** A C in a scene is recognizable by the silver at the wrist. Masters keep their hands covered when not working. Workshop smell is characteristic — hot metal, stone dust, the faint mineral smell of mineralized skin. A C master works slowly by appearance; a watch restoration takes days. The precision does not look like manifestation from the outside. It looks like craft. Close observation shows that tolerances are being met that would require machine tools a baseline craftsman could not replicate with hands alone.

## SPECIES D — "The Pale-Eyed"

**Overview.** An Eastern European primary-cluster species — Russian plain, Baltic coast — with unusually pale eyes showing a tapetum-lucidum-like reflective effect in low light, and species-typical perceptual capacity including enhanced low-light vision and auratic presence. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 53%. Mid-Atlantic presence minimal (~96,000) in pre-Onset Eastern European immigrant communities.

**Physical form.** Pale-skinned and light-eyed beyond expectation from genetic heritage — a D person with dark-haired parents will still have eyes that are pale grey, ice-blue, or silver. The eyes reflect light in darkness (subtle but noticeable). Otherwise facial features vary within European norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: low-light vision at baseline-exceptional levels (near-darkness becomes clearly visible), ability to read others' immediate intent by subtle physical cue, and a weak but persistent Auratic presence — Ds tend to be trusted or feared by baseline humans in ways the observers cannot explain. Individual manifestation augments Perceptual (most common) or Auratic (secondary common).

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration on the Russian plain and Baltic coast. Primary-cluster D polities have emerged as significant regional powers in post-Onset Eastern Europe.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: Eastern Europe. Minor diasporas: Central European cities, North American cities with pre-Onset Eastern European immigration.

**Population.** ~96,000 in mid-Atlantic. A notable enclave in outer Brooklyn (~3,000) operates as a recognizable community within Tower Lords' territory. Small Philadelphia and North Jersey concentrations.

**Cultural patterns.** Primary-cluster D communities maintain strong folk-religious traditions and clan-lineage tracking. Diaspora is mostly assimilated; the outer Brooklyn enclave retains distinctive Orthodox Christian practice and a partial Russian-language Sunday school.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Integrated but with faint social marking. The reflective eyes are noticeable; baseline humans meeting a D in dim light react subtly (startle, trust shift, or distrust shift depending on context).

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard.

**Internal factions.** Not politically significant in mid-Atlantic.

**Current political situation.** No polity. Outer Brooklyn enclave operates as a small bloc within one Tower Lord's district.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic D individuals.]

**Narrative register.** A D in a lit room is unremarkable. A D at dusk or in a dim interior has eyes that catch light in ways baseline eyes do not. The effect is subtle but unmistakable when seen close. In conversation the auratic presence is a faint pressure toward trust or distrust; the direction depends on the D's intent and baseline mood rather than any species factor.

## SPECIES E — "The Slow-Breath"

**Overview.** A South American primary-cluster species — Amazon basin, Andean Chile and Peru — with substantially reduced respiratory and metabolic rates, high toxin tolerance, and the capacity for prolonged conscious torpor. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 49%. Mid-Atlantic presence minimal (~56,000, scattered).

**Physical form.** Not visibly distinct from baseline at casual observation. Body temperature runs slightly lower; pulse and respiration are noticeably slow at rest (6–8 breaths per minute). Endurance in low-oxygen conditions is superior; heat tolerance is lower than baseline.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: reduced metabolic rate (caloric requirement ~60% of baseline), breath-holding 6–10 minutes, conscious torpor state extending days-to-weeks with minimal intake, toxin resistance, thin-air tolerance. Individual manifestation augments Biological (self-regulating) capacities or develops into hardiness-specialist profiles.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in the Amazon basin and Andean South America. Primary-cluster E communities are effectively dominant in post-Onset high-altitude and deep-jungle environments where baseline humans struggle.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: South America (Amazon + Andes). Minor diasporas: scattered through the Americas.

**Population.** ~56,000 in mid-Atlantic, genuinely scattered. Small family groups without concentration.

**Cultural patterns.** Primary-cluster E communities maintain strong indigenous-continuity religious and environmental practices. Mid-Atlantic diaspora is mostly assimilated; cultural continuity is maintained at family rather than community scale.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Integrated. The species-typical traits are not visible in casual contact. E individuals are occasionally found as hardy frontier travelers, scouts, or specialists in conditions (thin air, polluted water, extreme heat/cold) baseline humans find challenging.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard.

**Internal factions.** Not politically significant in mid-Atlantic.

**Current political situation.** No polity. Individuals in specialist roles.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic E individuals.]

**Narrative register.** An E in a scene is unremarked unless the scene involves hardship. In ordinary company their stillness and slow breathing might be mistaken for meditation. In extreme conditions — fire, thin air, toxic water — the E is the person still functional when others have collapsed.

## SPECIES F — "The Broad-Shouldered"

**Overview.** A Western European primary-cluster species — France, Iberia, Italy — 15–25% larger-framed than baseline with proportionally increased muscle mass and bone density. The species-typical capacity is kinetic-adjacent: enhanced strength, endurance, and damage resistance. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 47%. Mid-Atlantic presence minimal (~71,000).

**Physical form.** Visually imposing. Average F adult is 185–200 cm (6'1–6'7) and 90–115 kg. Bone density elevated; muscle mass above baseline even without training. Other features vary within Southern European norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: enhanced strength, endurance, damage resistance. F children often show T1–T3 kinetic tendencies without formal manifestation. Individual manifestation tends strongly Physical/kinetic.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in Southern Europe. Primary-cluster F polities in post-Onset France, Iberia, Italy have developed warrior-aristocracy character.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: Western Europe (Romance-language). Minor diasporas: North America, South America.

**Population.** ~71,000 in mid-Atlantic. Often integrated into baseline-human warlord retinues as guardsmen and shock troops.

**Cultural patterns.** Primary-cluster F communities lean into the visual impression — ceremonial armor, public combat ritual, warrior-aristocracy tradition. Diaspora Fs often serve as mercenaries or guards in baseline-human factions; the value is obvious and the F is paid accordingly.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Valued as physical specialists. Size is immediately visible; Fs are recognized at a glance. No particular social friction; their role in the regional economy is established.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard.

**Internal factions.** Not politically significant in mid-Atlantic.

**Current political situation.** No polity. Individuals in retinue service.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic F individuals.]

**Narrative register.** An F in a scene is physically obvious. In a room with baseline humans the F is immediately the largest person, sometimes by a substantial margin. In combat the F is a shock trooper — they close ranges faster than baseline humans can adjust, they absorb hits that would incapacitate, they carry weight. They are valued and paid accordingly.

## SPECIES G — "The Sun-Worn"

**Overview.** A Western European primary-cluster species — British Isles and Germanic North — with subtle skin-weathering, early greying, and species-typical biokinetic-adjacent plant affinity. Highest Y7 survival rate of the ten species (95%), due to the primary cluster managing collapse conditions unusually well. Global pre-Onset ~80M. **Mid-Atlantic SECONDARY CLUSTER** (~168,000, 1.1% of regional population) in central NJ and the Lehigh Valley.

**Physical form.** Subtle. A faint weathering or ruddiness of skin even in indoor-kept individuals; hair greys early relative to baseline humans (noticeable greying by age 25–30). Otherwise features vary within Northern European norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: innate connection to plant life and soil. Gs can sense plant health at distance, are disproportionately successful at agricultural work, and their children often show T1–T2 biokinetic tendencies. Individual manifestation augments Biological (agricultural specialization is common) and Perceptual (agricultural diagnosis).

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in British Isles and Germanic North. The unusually high Y7 survival is attributed in some accounts to species-typical biokinetic sensitivity supporting community agriculture through the first winter; other accounts cite favorable geographic and political conditions in the primary region.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: Western Europe (Germanic/British). Mid-Atlantic: central NJ (Princeton–New Brunswick) and Lehigh Valley. Other minor North American enclaves.

**Population.** ~168,000 in mid-Atlantic. The most *institutionally integrated* of the three secondary-cluster species. Does not form a distinct polity; embeds within the Lehigh Principalities and Central Jersey League.

**Cultural patterns.** Agricultural vocation as cultural center. G communities tend toward rural or semi-rural settlement even when originating urban; they practice ritualized seasonal observances. A subset practices heathen-revival religion (Odinic and related traditions), not publicly acknowledged by the community at large but known internally. Brother Tam's Delmarva monastic community, though not Species G, follows a parallel contemplative-agricultural pattern.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Integrated. G councilors advise the Lehigh Principalities formally; Species G biokinetic specialists teach at Rutgers-Princeton. The integration is reciprocal — Principality coal operations depend on G biokinetic soil-rehabilitation for tailings reclamation.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard. G-G pairings are common in the cluster but not dominant.

**Internal factions.** Lehigh (Jim Thorpe concentration; more traditional heathen-revival practice, discreet) and central NJ (Princeton-adjacent; more integrated with academic baseline). Differences are stylistic.

**Current political situation.** Not a separate polity. Embedded advisor/specialist roles in Lehigh Principalities and Central Jersey League.

**Notable individuals.** npc-veldt (Lehigh Principalities chief agricultural advisor, T3); npc-halbrecht (Lehigh Species G coordinator, T2, Jim Thorpe).

**Narrative register.** A G in a scene is visible by the weathering of the face — even younger Gs look slightly older than their years. Greying starts early and is visible. In agricultural work they are distinctive: they kneel to soil, handle seeds with a sensitivity that is not performance, know names of plants in languages that are not standard taxonomy. In the Lehigh they are part of the landscape in a way diaspora species usually are not.

## SPECIES H — "The Quick-Blooded"

**Overview.** A South American primary-cluster species — Brazilian coast, River Plate basin — with accelerated metabolism, rapid wound healing, and strong infectious-disease resistance. High Y7 survival (93%). Global pre-Onset ~80M. **Mid-Atlantic SECONDARY CLUSTER** (~174,000, 1.1% of regional population), concentrated in North Jersey (Paterson, Passaic, Union), Bucks County PA, outer Queens.

**Physical form.** Slightly smaller than baseline humans. Elevated body temperature; rapid pulse even at rest. Wounds close in hours rather than days; skin heals without scarring when it closes quickly enough. Features vary within Latin American norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: accelerated metabolism, rapid regeneration, disease resistance — they clear infections that would be fatal in baseline humans. Species-ability is biokinetic-internal rather than external (a H cannot heal others the way a biokinetic healer does). H children often show T1–T2 biokinetic tendencies oriented toward self rather than others.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in Brazilian coast and River Plate basin. The high primary-cluster Y7 survival reflects both favorable geographic conditions and species-typical metabolism aiding rapid recovery from infection.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: South America (southern/eastern). Mid-Atlantic: North Jersey, Bucks County PA, outer Queens.

**Population.** ~174,000. **Dispersed rather than territorial** — 20–30 recognizable township-level concentrations across North Jersey, Bucks County, and outer Queens, bound together by the **cross-enclave courier network**.

**Cultural patterns.** The courier network is the defining institution. Teenage and young-adult couriers run physical-mail routes between enclaves weekly. The network functions as distributed governance: major decisions are discussed across enclaves and resolved by rough consensus, enforced by the fact that any enclave defying consensus loses access to the courier network. The Hs are quietly well-armed; the T+4-month Iron Crown expropriation incident produced a coordinated response (loss of Iron Crown's access to medical services for six months) that demonstrated deterrence.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Cautious-neighborly. The Hs are integrated into local commerce (their couriers are premium service for the Bourse and others) and defend their enclaves quietly. They are not exclusionary but are not assimilating either.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard.

**Internal factions.** Northern network (Paterson-centered) and southern network (Bucks County). Coordinated via couriers.

**Current political situation.** No polity but effective distributed governance. Hostile with Iron Crown; friendly with Bourse, CJL, Species I.

**Notable individuals.** npc-cortese ("the Sparrow," T2, Paterson senior coordinator); npc-montes (Bucks County coordinator, T4). The anomalous case is npc-razorgrin — a Species H in Iron Crown service, a community embarrassment whose reasons for being there are not known outside her personal circle.

**Narrative register.** An H in a scene is smaller than baseline humans and faster-moving. Couriers are notable — they cover distances baseline runners cannot. In conversation Hs are friendly but observant; they remember. A courier coming in from a route has usually picked up three conversations along the way. The network knows what the network knows.

## SPECIES I — "The Wide-Sighted"

**Overview.** A Central American primary-cluster species — Mexico, Guatemala, the Yucatán — with significantly expanded irises, enhanced vision across a wider spectrum than baseline, and species-typical ability to recognize other Is at extraordinary distance. Global pre-Onset ~80M; Y7 survival 54%. **Mid-Atlantic DIASPORA** of ~2.17M — the only metahuman group large enough to influence regional politics.

**Physical form.** Visually notable. Eyes have significantly expanded irises often with unusual coloring (amber, orange, violet). Vision extends into near-UV and somewhat into near-IR; low-light vision is strongly enhanced. Otherwise features vary within Central American norms.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: enhanced vision in general, clear sight in near-total darkness, and long-range species-recognition (an I can pick out another I in a crowded room at 500 meters). Individual manifestation augments Perceptual strongly, sometimes with Auratic secondary (political presence).

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration in Central America. The mid-Atlantic diaspora depth draws on decades of pre-Onset immigration.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: Central America (Mexico/Guatemala/Yucatán). Mid-Atlantic diaspora is the largest outside primary-cluster region globally.

**Population.** ~2.17M in mid-Atlantic, concentrated in western Philadelphia suburbs and the DC/PG County corridor. Six major kin networks: Flores-kin (470k), Marquez-kin (380k), Herrera-kin (320k), Diaz-kin (280k), Vasquez-kin (240k), Reyes-kin (180k), plus smaller family networks.

**Cultural patterns.** Kin-governance. Extended family networks make collective decisions through communal discussion and maintain shared resources. Inter-kin coordination is quarterly; Species I does not currently claim territory in a political sense, but specific neighborhoods and townships are kin-network *concentrations*. The long-range species-recognition is a working tool — kin members coordinate at urban scale without infrastructure.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** The defining regional political question. Courted by Fed Continuity (needs healers, translators); covertly backed by Philadelphia Bourse (Beltran is Herrera-kin); friendly-neutral with Central Jersey League; hostile with Iron Crown and Potomac Irregulars. Cross-faction violence along the DC/PG border has killed dozens.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard; within the diaspora's scale, I-I pairings are common and produce reliably I offspring.

**Internal factions.** The major division is consolidation versus informal kin-governance: Elder Flores's conservative coalition (primarily older generation) vs. Tomás Reyes's pro-consolidation faction (primarily reproductive-age, currently at ~55% kin-polling support). The Species I Consolidation clock (2/8 at game start) measures progress toward formal polity declaration.

**Current political situation.** The single largest uncertainty in mid-Atlantic regional politics. Clock firing produces polity declaration, territorial claims, alliance realignment across factions.

**Notable individuals.** npc-flores (Flores-kin elder, T4); npc-reyes (Reyes-kin leader, T6, consolidation driver); npc-herrera (Herrera-kin elder, T3); npc-diaz (Diaz-kin elder, T4); npc-velasquez (Delmarva Harvest Lord, T7 — only Species I Lord); npc-beltran (Bourse Factor-General, T6, Herrera-kin); npc-ngo (Fed Continuity Captain, T5).

**Narrative register.** An I in a scene is marked by the eyes — large, often colored in ways baseline eyes are not. In low light their eyes catch ambient light. In a crowd an I scans and a baseline human can watch them scan — the gaze moves across faces with speed and precision. Two Is in the same room will have noticed each other within seconds of entering; their recognition is not performance.

## SPECIES J — "The Stone-Silent"

**Overview.** A Sub-Saharan African primary-cluster species — West African coast, Sahel — taller than baseline with elevated bone and muscle density, characteristic stillness of demeanor, and species-typical auratic-adjacent zone of steadiness. Lowest Y7 survival of the ten species (32%); the primary cluster was acutely vulnerable to collapse conditions. Global pre-Onset ~80M. Mid-Atlantic presence minimal (~33,000).

**Physical form.** Taller than baseline humans with unusual density — both bone density and muscle mass are elevated. A J of average male build is 190–205 cm and 95–110 kg. Visually marked by typical skin coloration of the primary population plus the species-typical **stillness of demeanor** — Js naturally hold physical position with remarkable precision. In a group, they are the person who is not fidgeting.

**Abilities.** Species-typical: project a zone of steadiness, perhaps 3–5 m radius, in which nearby baseline humans find it harder to feel fear, easier to make careful decisions, and less prone to panic. The effect is subtle but reliable. Individual manifestation augments Auratic (most common) and occasionally Perceptual.

**Origin.** Pre-Onset concentration on the West African coast and Sahel. Primary-cluster J communities have struggled through extreme collapse conditions; post-Onset polities in West Africa are fragmented.

**Geographic cluster.** Primary: Sub-Saharan Africa (West). Minor diasporas: Caribbean, North America, Europe.

**Population.** ~33,000 in mid-Atlantic, quietly respected as advisors, mediators, and crisis-response workers.

**Cultural patterns.** Primary-cluster J communities maintain strong oral tradition and age-hierarchy governance. Diaspora Js often serve in mediator and crisis-coordinator roles — their steadiness-aura is specifically useful in those contexts.

**Relationship with baseline humans.** Integrated and valued. Their crisis utility is known across factions; Js are occasionally retained for specific negotiations or for disaster coordination.

**Reproductive dynamics.** Standard.

**Internal factions.** Not politically significant in mid-Atlantic.

**Current political situation.** No polity. Individuals in advisory roles across factions.

**Notable individuals.** [UNSPECIFIED — bible names no specific mid-Atlantic J individuals.]

**Narrative register.** A J in a scene is immediately large and immediately still. In a tense room the tension noticeably eases around them; in a panicked crowd they are the calm point. Their speech is usually measured. When they move, the motion is complete rather than hesitant. The steadiness-aura is not the same as Auratic trust-field — it does not incline listeners to trust what is said; it inclines them to think clearly before responding.

## Cross-species similarity note

Two pairs of species risk feeling similar without deliberate differentiation in play. **Species C (Silver-Hand, matter-manipulation craft) and Species G (Sun-Worn, biokinetic agricultural) are both "specialist integration" species** — they embed in baseline polities as skilled practitioners rather than forming separate polities. The distinction in practice: C's integration is commercial and institutional (Twelve Hands council, Bourse contracts), G's is advisory and agricultural (councilor roles, Rutgers training). C is urban-workshop; G is rural-estate. **Species A (Hollow-Boned) and Species E (Slow-Breath) are both "hardy specialist" species** — physically unmarked but metabolically distinct. The distinction: A is evasive-efficient (fast, alert, falls apart under blunt damage), E is endurance-resistant (slow, toxin-tolerant, survives conditions that kill baseline humans). Scenes featuring either should lean into these specific utilities rather than generic "metahuman hardiness."

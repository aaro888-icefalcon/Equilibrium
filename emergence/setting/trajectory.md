# TRAJECTORY — Lordly Equilibrium, T+1 through T+30

This document traces the projected shape of the mid-Atlantic through three decades, as derived from simulation output. The canonical population trajectory is 15.94M (Y1) → 15.65M (Y7) → 15.13M (Y15) → 12.42M (Y30). Net decline across three decades. The world does not rebuild. It settles into a slower shape.

## The First Era: T+1 to T+5

The opening window is defined by two things: clocks that are about to fire, and the first post-Onset-born children who have not yet manifested. Everything happens in the shadow of both.

The Bourse Currency Stability clock completes early, probably within the first two years. Copper becomes formally what it already informally is. Continuity scrip devalues in measurable steps; merchants stop accepting it at posted rates; Fed Continuity finds its reach shrinking further because its paper no longer travels. The Biokinetic Agriculture Saturation clock completes within three years, around T+3 to T+4. Regional food surplus becomes permanent in the settled zones. Nobody in Delmarva, Central Jersey, or the Yonkers-Bear-House corridor will starve again through ordinary weather.

Open war begins in two places in this window. Iron Crown and Staten Citadel fight over Manhattan salvage, somewhere between T+3 and T+5. Volk probably loses — Staten's temporal-spatial capacity is not something he can match — but he may survive as a diminished power. If he dies, Iron Crown fragments into captain-fiefs and northern New Jersey becomes a contested ruin. Crabclaw and Fed Continuity also enter open conflict along the Chesapeake line, somewhere between T+3 and T+7. Fed Continuity will not win; the Baltimore failure was the predictor. Alcott will likely cede Baltimore formally rather than continue fighting.

The Catskill Throne advances its tribute-to-vassalage clock. Yonkers Compact faces its first existential decision — whether to accept formal subordination. [SPECULATIVE: the Three Judges may attempt to negotiate a special-status arrangement; Preston may accept because Yonkers' biokinetic services are more valuable cooperative than coerced.]

The Species I Consolidation clock crawls upward, one segment per year or two. Tomás Reyes wins some assemblies, loses others. By T+5 he is not the marginal voice he was at T+1, but he is not yet the majority. Elder Flores is visibly aging.

First post-Onset children begin manifesting around T+6 to T+8 (the earliest puberty arrivals). The first cohort is small, but they are the first humans to have never known the Before. Their manifestation rates are noticeably higher than the Onset generation — nearly 99%. Parental manifestation heritability is real but weak.

## The Second Era: T+5 to T+10

By T+10 the population has barely declined from the T+1 baseline — roughly 15M. Fertility has recovered partially (TFR around 2.5). Attrition from faction violence, eldritch pressure, and disease roughly balances births. The demography looks less like a refugee crisis and more like a pre-industrial steady state.

Fed Continuity Erosion completes sometime in this window, probably around T+8 to T+11. Colonel Reeve formalizes Potomac Irregular independence. Alcott is either deposed, assassinated, or reduced to a ceremonial DC-Mall authority. Fed Continuity scrip collapses catastrophically for its holders. Several Virginia districts consolidate under Reeve. The largest regional polity by population becomes a medium one.

The Tower Lords of Brooklyn undergo multiple succession events. The 38% five-year leadership-change probability compounds; by T+10 probably three of the original five-to-six Tower Lords have been replaced, some violently. The borough's territorial lines rearrange but the salvage economy continues.

Central Jersey League grows through the collapse of Fed Continuity's northern reach. Several border townships petition for Accord membership. The Accord Council processes applications cautiously. [SPECULATIVE: Dr. Okafor's health becomes a regional concern around T+8 to T+10; her successor is not evident.]

The Delmarva Harvest Lords remain stubbornly unconsolidated. The Unification clock barely moves — maybe one more segment in this decade. But their collective importance grows: as Fed Continuity fails and inter-faction trade concentrates on the Bourse, Delmarva's food becomes the most reliable currency in the region.

Species I Consolidation has not yet fired but is visibly closer. If Fed Continuity falls to Reeve, Species I in the DC corridor faces a direct threat (Irregulars are hostile), and the clock advances faster. Tomás Reyes begins drawing the majority.

Pine Barrens Expansion advances slowly — maybe 5/12 by T+10. The Listening grows by a few thousand each season. Sprawl territorial creep continues. Nobody mounts a coordinated response. The Catskill Throne lacks southern reach; Philadelphia Bourse lacks military capacity; Central Jersey League is focused on internal stabilization. [SPECULATIVE: the first Listening attack into a populated township may occur in this window, provoking emergency response.]

Manhattan Eldritch Breach crawls. An anomalous entity other than the Echo may appear in the salvage zones. The Tower Lords' wealthiest captains begin quietly investing outside Manhattan.

## The Third Era: T+10 to T+20

Population begins declining more noticeably: 15M → ~13M across this decade. Net attrition overtakes birth rate as the post-Onset-born mature into working-age cohorts but the surviving Onset-generation ages and dies at pre-industrial mortality rates.

Species I Consolidation fires somewhere in this window, most likely around T+15 to T+20. Tomás Reyes (by now in his late fifties) declares formal polity. Territorial claim encompasses the western Philadelphia corridor, PG County, and scattered kin-network concentrations. Baseline-human factions respond variably: Bourse recognizes formally; Central Jersey League accepts; Iron Crown (if still extant) rejects; Reeve's Irregulars declare hostility. The declaration triggers a regional political reset — alliance structures reorganize around the new player.

The Catskill Throne's tribute clock is complete. Hudson Valley polities are formal vassals. Preston is in his sixties. The question of his succession starts dominating regional speculation. His retinue is loyal but structurally ambiguous; no heir, no named successor. Marshal Whelan is the most obvious claimant but does not have Preston's capacity. [SPECULATIVE: Preston may designate a successor in this window, or may deliberately leave ambiguity; either choice has consequences.]

Delmarva consolidates or doesn't. If external pressure rises — a Reeve regime pushing north, a Crabclaw alliance turning east, or a Species I polity making resource claims — the Harvest Lords may federate under a single Council. If external pressure stays low, they remain six-plus lords in comfortable rivalry. The simulation sees this as genuinely uncertain; probability mass is split roughly 40/60 between formation and non-formation.

Manhattan Eldritch Breach is likely to advance to 5/8 or 6/8 by T+20. The Echo has not moved but a second anomalous entity has probably appeared. Tower Lords react with reduced salvage operations; the Manhattan economy shrinks. Some Tower Lord districts fail and their territory passes to neighbors, to abandonment, or to the anomalous.

Pine Barrens Expansion has not yet fired. The Listening has doubled or tripled in size. The Sprawl has expanded but not breached the Delaware line. Central Jersey League invests heavily in southern defense; the Species I polity (now declared) takes direct interest because Herrera-kin in Camden is proximate. [SPECULATIVE: the first faction-led coordinated strike against Listening territory may be attempted in this window, probably unsuccessfully.]

The post-Onset-born cohort now dominates the young adult population. Their values, memories, and political framework are entirely different from the Onset generation. They do not mourn what was lost. They regard manifestation as a birthright, faction loyalty as a choice, and the Before as myth.

## The Fourth Era: T+20 to T+30

Population declines from ~13M to the canonical 12.42M. Further attrition concentrates in the fringes: Manhattan salvage zones, Pine Barrens perimeter, contested Crabclaw-Continuity borderlands. Settled zones (Delmarva, CJL, Yonkers-Bear-House corridor, Species I polity, Catskill core) remain stable.

The regional great powers at T+30, per canonical projection:

1. **The Catskill Throne** — still present as a hegemon in upper Hudson, though likely with a successor to Preston or an internal succession struggle resolved. [SPECULATIVE: Preston's death circa T+25 to T+30 is the single most likely disruptive event of this era; the Throne may survive as a continuing institution or fragment.]
2. **The Central Jersey League** — expanded by absorption of collapsed smaller polities; the region's most stable federation.
3. **The Species I Polity** — formalized for a decade or more; a regional player with territorial claims in Pennsylvania and Maryland.
4. **The Delmarva Harvest Lords, unified or not** — the region's food-supply dominant; military capacity limited but strategic leverage enormous.
5. **Fed Continuity remnant** — reduced to DC Mall and Pentagon enclave but still a recognized authority in diplomatic fictions. Reeve's Irregulars are a separate polity occupying what was Virginia District.

The Tower Lords are almost certainly reduced or dissolved. The Iron Crown is either gone or a shadow. The Bourse remains as a commercial institution but has probably professionalized and extended reach — it is recognized across all surviving polities as a neutral arbiter and currency issuer.

The eldritch frontier has moved. The Listening is larger. The Sprawl has more territory. Manhattan has more anomalous entities. [LOW confidence: whether any of these have breached into populated faction territory by T+30.] The mid-Atlantic has a permanent eldritch backdrop; the question is only how encroaching it has become.

The population at T+30 is younger than the T+1 population, significantly less literate, more fertile, more manifested, and less institutionally organized in many respects. It is also more stable in the sense that it has lived with the post-Onset condition for thirty years and no longer experiences it as a crisis. What the Onset generation regarded as catastrophe, the post-Onset-born regard as the world.

## Dominant Dynamics Across the Arc

Three recurring patterns shape the trajectory:

**Slow decline within stable bounds.** The canonical "stagnant" trajectory does not mean collapse. It means that every winter is hard, every border skirmish takes lives, every eldritch breach claims a settlement, and net fertility does not quite outpace net attrition. The region shrinks by an average 0.6–0.9 percent per year over thirty years. That is not a death spiral. It is a settled low equilibrium.

**Institutional fragility at high tier.** The most powerful individuals — Preston, Volk, possibly Kostas — are indispensable to their factions. Their mortality clocks dominate long-run uncertainty. The simulation projects at least one major faction leader change every 2–3 years on average; over 20 years, most factions have turned over, some multiple times. Succession crises are the recurring narrative.

**The eldritch is patient.** The Listening grows by 15–25% per year on SIS dynamics. The Sprawl creeps by 5% per year on logistic dynamics. Neither is a crisis in any given year. Both compound. Over thirty years both double or triple. The mid-Atlantic's eldritch problem at T+30 is worse than at T+1 because the regional response has been inadequate — not failed, just insufficient.

## Tone

The Lordly Equilibrium is neither utopia nor dystopia. It is a world that has absorbed its catastrophe and settled into living afterwards. Things that would have been unthinkable before the Onset are now routine; things that were routine before are now unimaginable. People marry, have children, bury their dead, trade goods, pay tribute, argue politics, and die at forty-two on average. A few individuals see their world transformed by a breakthrough or a meeting with a Warped bear in the Catskills. Most do not. The slow decline leaves a society thinner than it was at T+1 but more coherent than it was at T+3. What the inhabitants call "the new world" is, by T+30, simply the world.

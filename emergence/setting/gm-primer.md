# EMERGENCE
## The Lordly Equilibrium

*A Sourcebook for the Mid-Atlantic, One Year After the Onset*

---

## How to Read This Book

This is a setting reference for roleplaying games set in the mid-Atlantic United States, one year after the Onset — the day every machine stopped working and almost everyone who survived found they could do something they couldn't do before. The world on the other side of that day is what this book describes.

The book is organized the way you will use it at the table. The early chapters establish what kind of world this is and how a character fits into it. The middle chapters are reference: places, people, powers, threats. The late chapters are procedural: how to generate encounters, what the factions are doing while the players aren't watching, how a campaign resolves.

Read Chapters 1 through 3 straight through before running a session. Everything after that is reference — skim, bookmark, consult.

A brief note on confidence. Not everything in this book is settled fact in the fiction. The character of the warlord Preston is firm; what happens when you stand in Lower Manhattan for three hours is firm; what the anomalous eldritch entities *are* is deliberately unresolved and will stay that way. Where the book uses phrases like *it is believed*, *scholars disagree*, or *the cause is unknown*, those are live mysteries. Preserve them in play.

The setting is grim but not hopeless. People survived. Some of them built things worth defending. That tension — between the collapsed world and the one being rebuilt on top of it — is the engine the book is designed to drive.

# CHAPTER 1: THE WORLD

## The Onset

On a Thursday afternoon in late May, at twenty-three minutes past fourteen hundred Greenwich, every operating electronic device in the mid-Atlantic ceased to function. Cars stopped on bridges. Aircraft fell from the sky — three hundred and eighty of them, over the region alone, inside three hours. Elevators dropped between floors. Pacemakers stopped. Ventilators stopped. Traffic signals went dark and stayed dark.

At the same moment, every human being in the region experienced something neither science nor scripture had prepared them for. The fortunate felt a warmth in the chest, a rearrangement, a sense of *something coming online*. The unfortunate discovered their new abilities by accident — kinetic force released into a crowded sidewalk, fire where no fire had been lit, bodies that no longer held together in the shapes they had held that morning. Ninety-five percent of humanity manifested powers in those first hours. Of the remainder, one and a half percent transformed into something no longer recognizably human. The remaining three and a half percent simply remained as they had been, unchanged, a minority now.

This event is called the Onset. No one knows what caused it. The major theories — divine action, technological accident, intrusion from elsewhere, recurrence of an ancient cycle — all have adherents and none have evidence. The entities who claim to know (certain eldritch sovereigns, certain high-tier Warped) give contradictory accounts in languages and cosmologies that do not cohere.

The Onset's second effect, less immediate but more transformative, was the emergence of the ten metahuman species. Approximately one person in ten awoke no longer entirely human. Their bodies had restructured overnight along one of ten distinct patterns, each clustered geographically by ancestry. The species are real, coherent, and breeding true within their own kind; their children are of their species, and the diasporas they form are political facts on the ground one year later.

The third effect is still being discovered. Animals manifested too. Most at low tier, indistinguishable from being slightly larger or faster or smarter versions of their species. Some manifested to tier 6 and above, where cognition crosses a threshold into full sentience and beyond. These are the Warped. They speak, reason, negotiate, and claim — with disquieting consistency — to remember the world before humanity built its cities. Whether these claims are true is one of the central open questions of the present age.

And plants manifested. Not all of them, and not individually — but certain regions grew something that is not quite a forest and not quite an organism, something that moves when threatened, coordinates across kilometers, and does not want humans in its territory. This is the Hive.

One year has passed. Sixty-eight percent of the region's pre-Onset population is dead — to immediate accidents, to starvation, to cholera in the unpumped cities, to winter, to violence, to the creatures that came out of the forests and the things that came up from the bay. The sixteen million who remain have sorted themselves into some two hundred and ninety polities of varying coherence. The largest holds nearly two million people. The smallest is a family on a fortified farm. Three names among these polities dominate the strategic map: the Federal Continuity Command in Washington, the Iron Crown in Newark, and the Catskill Throne in the mountains above the Hudson. A dozen others matter.

This is the Lordly Equilibrium. It is called that because no single power dominates the region and none is collapsing; because the warlords have discovered they cannot eat each other whole; because the merchants of Philadelphia have found a currency people will accept; because the biokinetic agriculturalists have begun producing surplus. The worst is over. Something steady has begun. It will not last.

## The Shape of the Region

The mid-Atlantic the characters inhabit runs from the Catskills in the north to the Chesapeake in the south, from the Appalachians in the west to the Atlantic. It is twelve zones and roughly sixteen million people.

**The north.** The Catskills are forested mountains held by a single warlord of terrifying personal capacity. Below them the Hudson Valley is river-rich, defensible, and tributary. Yonkers is a commune. Peekskill is a demesne organized around a still-functioning nuclear reactor. The passes are tolled.

**The city.** New York is a ruin and a frontier. Manhattan proper holds perhaps a quarter-million people scattered across ruined streets; its Financial District is not safe to enter. The outer boroughs — Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx — are parceled among warlords, communes, and the salvage operators who trade through Staten Island's harbor. The city exports broken machines and imports food. It will not be rebuilt.

**The industrial corridor.** Newark is the Iron Crown's. Jersey City is its extension. The Port of Newark is the only deep-water harbor in warlord hands, and what arrives there is the most feared cargo in the region. South of Newark the central New Jersey corridor is stable federation territory — fifteen townships bound by agricultural pact, the largest working polity by population. South of that, the Pine Barrens.

**The Pine Barrens.** Not safe. The Hive's largest colony is there. An eldritch entity is there. A cult lives among both. Trade routes bend around the Barrens; travelers who cut through do not always return.

**The river cities.** Philadelphia is the region's market. It is neither warlord territory nor commune but a merchants' city, governed by consortium, issuing the currency everyone accepts. Across the river, Camden is contested. North of Philadelphia the Lehigh Valley produces coal and stone; its twelve small lords supply the cities.

**The south.** Baltimore belongs to a confederation of three warlords who distrust each other and distrust Washington more. Washington itself is the seat of the Federal Continuity Command, the largest organized polity in the region and the closest thing to a pre-Onset institution still operating. Its authority projects perhaps fifty kilometers from the Pentagon.

**The peninsula.** The Delmarva peninsula is the region's breadbasket — six major biokinetic lords and a dozen lesser ones, feeding everyone. They will not unify. They do not have to.

## What Has Changed

The scale of what was lost is hard to hold in the mind, so examples will have to do. There is no electricity except in one demesne. There is no radio, no telephone, no internet, no functioning aircraft. Cars do not run; their engines will not turn over and no one understands why. Pre-Onset firearms still work — ammunition is reloaded by matter-manipulators, carefully — but heavier weapons do not. There are no tanks, no artillery, no helicopters. Printed books still exist; new printing happens in Philadelphia, on a press maintained by a matter-manipulator, producing perhaps a thousand pages a day.

Antibiotics are running out. The stockpile from Before is now eighteen months old and increasingly unreliable. Surgery happens without anesthesia unless a biokinetic is present. Childbirth kills women at rates not seen in the region for a century. Cholera and typhoid are endemic in the cities. Measles has returned. Tuberculosis is spreading.

Against this: the biokinetic healer at work in a small-town clinic can close a wound that would have required three hundred thousand dollars of surgery in the Before. The fields of Delmarva produce nearly what they produced with industrial fertilizer, because the lords who own them can make plants grow. The merchant who can read emotions at a glance negotiates better deals than her competitors. The courier who can teleport short distances carries mail that no bandit can intercept. The warlord whose presence freezes the blood of his enemies does not need as many soldiers as he would otherwise.

This is the trade. The world is smaller and poorer and more violent and more superstitious than it was a year ago. But the humans who survived are more — individually more capable, more vivid, more dangerous. Every peasant is a small wizard. Every soldier has a trick. Every merchant sees a little further into your soul than you want her to.

## The Central Questions

Every campaign in this setting eventually confronts some version of these:

*What will the world settle into?* The equilibrium holds for now. The Federal Continuity Command is eroding. The Iron Crown is ambitious. The Catskill Throne is immortal but reclusive, and will not rule forever. Species I, two million strong in diaspora, has not yet decided to become a polity — but it is considering. Any of these could break in the next decade and reshape everything.

*What are the Warped?* They speak. They claim to have been here before us. Some of them are demonstrably older than they should be. They are willing to negotiate; some are willing to ally; some want us dead. No one knows whether their claims about prior cycles of the world are true, and the ones who might know are not telling.

*What are the eldritch?* A cult grows in the Pine Barrens around something that was not human a year ago and speaks languages no human has ever spoken. In Lower Manhattan a presence takes hours from people who wander into its territory. In the Chesapeake something sinks ships. The transformation that produced them is not understood and not reversed.

*How much of what we knew can we keep?* Literacy is at twenty-nine percent and falling. The pre-Onset generation is aging. Medicine, engineering, science — the institutional knowledge that sustained the Before — decays faster than it can be transmitted. Some faction, somewhere, is going to have to decide what to save. The Philadelphia Bourse has decided this is their work. The Federal Continuity Command has decided it is theirs. The Catskill Throne has a library of five thousand volumes and no particular interest in sharing them.

These questions are not answered in this book. They are the campaign.

# CHAPTER 2: POWERS

## What Manifestation Is

A manifested person can do something baseline humans cannot. That is the whole of the definition. The specifics vary enormously — a manifested farmer might coax wheat to germinate a day early and nothing else; a manifested warlord might level a city block with his fists. Both are manifested. The difference is tier.

Powers do not feel to their users like tools. They feel like parts of the self. The farmer does not *use* her biokinetic sense; she has it the way she has hands. The warlord does not *draw* his kinetic force; it is in him, in the way fear is in him. Manifestation is an extension of the person, not an appendage. This matters for fiction and it matters for how corruption works, which is discussed below.

Most people have more than one thing they can do. A little over half of the manifested have a profile rather than a single ability — one dominant capacity, one or two lesser ones, sometimes in the same category, sometimes across categories. The woman who throws kinetic force (primary) also sees at a distance better than human eyes should (secondary) and plants wilt around her when she is furious (tertiary). Her *tier* is a single number assessing her overall capability; her *profile* describes what she can actually do.

There are seven domains of manifestation, described below. They are descriptive labels for the phenomenology of what people can do, not metaphysical categories. Do not overthink the boundaries. An illusionist who produces false images in a target's mind is operating in the Perceptual domain; one who bends light so everyone can see the illusion is operating in Matter/Energy; the same person might be doing both and be categorized by whichever is more common. Categorization is soft.

## The Seven Domains

### Physical/Kinetic

Powers involving the body and applied force. The broadest and most common domain — roughly twenty-eight percent of the manifested have Physical as primary.

This is the domain of the classic super: the brick who lifts a car, the speedster who outruns a motorcycle, the flyer who leaves the ground, the brawler whose skin stops bullets. Also the reflexive kinetic — redirecting incoming force, catching a thrown knife, stopping a swung bat with a raised hand. At higher tiers, sustained flight, concussive blasts, the ability to walk through smallarms fire.

At the low end, a Physical T1 is visibly above-human. She can lift three hundred kilos for a few seconds. She does not get winded. She takes a punch and does not feel it. At T3, she can flip a car, sprint past a motorcycle for short bursts, take a knife to the ribs and keep fighting. At T5, she throws cars across parking lots and her skin does not reliably bruise. At T7, she levels buildings. At T9, she levels city blocks.

Preston, in the Catskills, is T9. He is not the highest-tier being in the region. He is the highest-tier person.

### Perceptual/Mental

Powers of mind, perception, and influence. About fifteen percent primary. The most socially dangerous domain because most of its manifestations are invisible.

This is where telepathy lives, and empathy, and precognition, and illusion (the mental kind), and mind control. Also the more mundane perceptual enhancements: seeing clearly in what humans call darkness, hearing whispered conversations across a market square, smelling a lie on someone's breath. At higher tiers, reshaping memory, implanting beliefs that stick, coordinating the actions of dozens of allies who cannot see each other.

Perceptual users are often not obvious. A T4 telepath in conversation with you is reading your surface thoughts and has been the whole time. A T5 suggester is adjusting how you feel about the deal as she speaks. A T7 mentalist is, possibly, the reason your faction fell apart six months ago; you might not be able to tell.

Defenses exist. Most Perceptual users can also shield themselves against other Perceptual users — the one capacity almost guarantees the other. Outside that caste, the only reliable defense is the presence of a stronger Perceptual ally. This shapes every court, every council, every merchants' hall in the region. If you are important enough to need a reader present, you are important enough to be read.

### Matter/Energy

Elemental control, transmutation, and energy projection. About fourteen percent primary.

This is the domain of the pyrokinetic, the metalshaper, the ice-maker, the lightning-thrower, the light-bender. The specific sub-affinities vary enormously — one matter user might work only with iron, another with stone, another with any material she can touch, another only with the air between her hands. The tier governs how much and how decisively; the affinity governs what.

Matter users do the visible work of the post-Onset world. The knife that does not dull is matter-forged. The wall that does not weather was touched by a matter-user on construction. The Philadelphia Bourse's printing press exists because a matter user maintains its type. The Peekskill reactor is contained because a matter user spent forty hours reshaping its failing vessel. The finest craftsmen of the Flushing-Edison Cluster can smooth metal with their palms.

At combat tiers, matter users are artillery — fireballs, lightning, hurled stone, walls that appear and collapse on command. At T7, a matter user can set fire to a city district and keep it burning. At T9, the landscape itself rearranges under sustained work.

### Biological/Vital

Powers affecting living tissue — human, animal, plant, the user's own body. About fourteen percent primary. The domain that feeds the region and heals its wounded.

Healing is what most people mean when they say biokinetic. A T5 healer closes a gut wound in forty minutes. A T7 regrows a limb over six months. There are perhaps eighty such healers in the region, and every one of them is rich, or could be, if she wanted to be. Most accept patronage from a faction in exchange for retainer.

But the domain is larger than healing. It includes the agricultural biokinetics who made the post-Onset food supply possible — the women and men who touch seedlings and make them yield. It includes the shapeshifters, the animal-communicators, the few known shapers of their own biology. It includes the rare practitioners who work against the body rather than for it: the Iron Crown's interrogator, who induces pain with a touch, is biokinetic.

Biokinetic work is visible. Plants thrive around agricultural users. Healers glow faintly with residual vitality to the eye of another biokinetic. Long-practiced users often look younger than their years, or older — depending on whether they have spent their surplus on themselves or on others.

### Temporal/Spatial

Powers affecting time, space, distance, and phase. About fourteen percent primary. The strangest domain.

Teleportation is here — short-range at low tier, progressive to line-of-sight at middle tier, to landmarks and eventually to places the user has not seen at high tier. Pocket-space inventories are here: the merchant who carries her whole warehouse in an inside pocket, the courier whose satchel holds more than its volume should. Intangibility. Portal-making. Brief time-dilation for reflexive purposes. At the highest tiers, retrocausal perception — seeing a few seconds into the future reliably enough to act on it.

The Staten Citadel's harbor operates on temporal/spatial work; its lord can teleport cargo across a harbor that no bridge crosses. The Federal Continuity Command's couriers are temporal/spatial specialists. The Catskill Throne's intelligence chief operates almost exclusively through short-range teleportation.

Temporal/spatial users pay in subjective time and disorientation. A teleporter who has worked hard wakes up having lost a day she cannot account for. A phase-user who has spent a year stepping through walls cannot always be sure which side of a wall she is on.

### Eldritch/Unnatural

The rarest domain. About four percent primary, and falling — the rate of new Eldritch manifestations is lower than baseline, and many who do manifest conceal it.

This is the domain that should not work. Curses that produce effects without observable mechanism. Reality alterations. Void effects that suppress other manifestations. Contact and channeling with entities that are not the user. Pacts. Rituals whose effects exceed the user's own capacity because the power does not come entirely from the user — something else is being invoked, or consulted, or bargained with.

Eldritch users at low tier can produce effects disproportionate to their tier, because their power is negotiated rather than personal. A T3 eldritch with a strong patron can do what a T6 of other domains cannot. Tier assessment for eldritch users is unreliable and tends to underestimate their real danger.

Eldritch manifestation produces visible corruption faster than any other domain. The user changes — physically, and then more than physically. The Listening's members are the archetype. Most societies treat eldritch capacity with fear. Some treat it with reverence. No faction in the region openly employs eldritch practitioners.

### Auratic/Presence

The continuous domain. About eleven percent primary.

Other domains are things the user does. Auratic is something the user is. The auratic warlord produces fear in a circle around him — not because he intends to, although he can, but because his presence shapes the air. The auratic merchant's word is trusted more than her competitors' because a trust-field surrounds her. The auratic healer's clinic is a calm place; the auratic general's camp is brave.

Auratic effects are persistent and often passive. They do not turn on and off. A T5 fear-field warlord frightens his own allies as well as his enemies, and the price of his rule is that no one can be comfortable near him for long. A T5 calm-field mediator finds that genuine disagreement rarely occurs in her presence, which is useful and dangerous — the absence of conflict is not the same as the resolution of it.

At the highest tiers, auratic effects attach to places the user has spent time. The Throne Hall of Preston is not only quieted by his presence; it remains quieted in his absence. The Philadelphia Bourse's great chamber is a place where honest trade is easier and dishonest trade harder, and this is the accumulated effect of three years of auratic work by its Factors-General.

## Profile and Tier

Tier is a single number from 1 to 10 and above, assessing a manifested person's overall capability. It is not a per-domain measurement. A narrow specialist at T6 and a three-domain generalist at T6 are equally dangerous in different ways, and a game-master rules on tier holistically, asking: what happens when this person has to fight for her life?

Most people are T1 to T3. A tenant farmer whose biokinetic sense lets her predict which cows will calve is T1. A town smith whose matter-affinity lets him forge knives that hold their edge is T2. A retinue soldier who can leap twenty feet and take a blow from a crowbar is T3. These are the majority of the manifested.

Tier 4 to 5 is skilled-specialist territory. A town's chief healer. A warlord's trusted captain. A merchant-house principal. Perhaps five percent of the manifested.

Tier 6 to 7 is faction-level. A major warlord's second. A Harvest Lord. A Bourse factor of consequence. Perhaps half a percent.

Tier 8 is regional. Kostas of Peekskill, Volk of Newark, Reeve of the Potomac Irregulars. Perhaps a hundred individuals in the entire mid-Atlantic.

Tier 9 is Preston. There are others — rumored in distant regions, certainly some among the high Warped — but Preston is the only human T9 anyone in the region has seen and returned from.

Tier 10 and above is mythic. No human in the mid-Atlantic is believed to be T10. Rumor assigns T10+ status to certain figures elsewhere in the world and to certain Warped entities present in the region. Rumor is not always wrong.

### The Profile

When a character is described mechanically, the profile has three parts:

**Dominant domain** — the one the character expresses most often and most powerfully.

**Secondary** — a capacity in another domain, meaningful but lesser. Fifty-five percent of manifested characters have at least one secondary.

**Tertiary** — rarer still, a third domain. Ten percent of characters.

A character might be *Physical T7 / Auratic T4 fear-field / Biological T3 durability*. That is Volk, the Iron Crown. The profile reads: dominant kinetic striker, whose presence freezes the enemies around him, whose body shrugs off what should kill him. His tier is T8 overall because the integration of the three makes him more dangerous than a narrow T7 striker alone.

Alternatively: *Biological T7 agricultural / Perceptual T4 emotional reading / Auratic T3 trust*. That is Okafor, of the Central Jersey League. Not a combatant; her tier is T7 because her three capacities integrate into a leader who feeds fifteen townships and holds them together by reading the room and keeping tempers cool. She could not fight Volk. She does not have to.

### Tiers and Breakthroughs

Tier advancement is rare and marked. Most manifested reach the upper end of their capacity through ten or twenty years of practice and stop there. Advancement to the next tier requires a breakthrough: an acute event that forces the character across a threshold.

Breakthroughs come in three shapes.

**Depth.** The character's existing capacities deepen. A T6 kinetic becomes a T7 kinetic, throwing further and harder. This is the narrow-specialist's route, and it is how Preston reached T9 — three breakthroughs in succession, each concentrating him further in a single domain.

**Breadth.** The character manifests a new domain that was previously absent. A T6 pure Physical develops a T3 Auratic fear-field and rises to T7 overall because the profile is now richer. This is rarer and tends to happen under specific conditions — exposure to corruption zones, sustained proximity to a higher-tier user of the new domain, life-threat of a particular kind.

**Integration.** The character's existing capacities begin to combine in ways they did not before. The kinetic striker who also has enhanced reflexes finds that her reflexes and her strikes are now one motion rather than two. Integration breakthroughs often happen in mentorship — a student who has studied under a higher-tier user for years suddenly has the pieces come together.

Every breakthrough leaves a mark. Something accumulates. Preston has lost the capacity for fear; he cannot feel it, which is both useful and isolating. Kostas of Peekskill has mineralized — his left side is visibly stone-flecked, and the process continues. Volk's dread-field is also his loneliness; no one is comfortable near him for long, not even the women he marries.

High-tier characters are visibly marked. A T7 or above who appears unmarked is either new to that tier, or hiding it with great care, or something other than what she appears.

## Costs

Every use of a manifestation costs the user something. The cost varies by domain and intensity. Light use — the farmer's daily touch on her seedlings, the merchant's read of a negotiating partner, the retinue soldier's enhanced leap across an alley — costs almost nothing. Heavy use costs a great deal.

**Metabolic.** Physical, Matter/Energy, and Biological users pay primarily in calories. A kinetic fighter who has thrown hard for an hour is ravenous for a day. A matter user who has held a large working for six hours may collapse. This is the most common cost and the easiest to manage — eat more, rest.

**Cognitive.** Perceptual and Temporal/Spatial users pay in attention and subjective coherence. A telepath who has coordinated a battle for four hours has a migraine that lasts two days. A teleporter who has moved herself repeatedly loses track of where she was yesterday, and occasionally of what she did there.

**Ambient.** Auratic users pay continuously. A warlord whose fear-field is always on is never in a room with people who are at ease. A healer whose calm-field operates full-time cannot have an angry conversation with her children. The cost is social, over years, cumulative.

**Attention.** Eldritch users pay in the attention of whatever they have contacted. The more they use their capacity, the more something else is aware of them, and wants things from them, and eventually asks for them. The price of late-career eldritch work is rarely paid by the eldritch user herself. It is paid by someone she loves.

### Burnout

Sustained overuse produces burnout — a collapse state varying by domain, lasting hours to days, occasionally permanent. A burned-out Physical user is incapacitated for the duration; her body has exhausted its capacity to supply the force she has demanded. A burned-out Perceptual cannot shield, cannot read, cannot think clearly for a period measured in days. A burned-out Biological healer cannot heal herself or others until she recovers, and often her next healing session is noticeably weaker.

Roughly five to eight percent of heavy-use T2–T4 workers experience a burnout event each year. Among T5+ retinue soldiers in active faction warfare, the rate approaches twenty percent annually. Among biokinetic healers working at capacity, ten to fifteen percent. Among T7+ individuals who routinely operate at the top of their capacity, thirty percent — which is why most high-tier users reserve themselves for the moments that matter.

### Corruption

Long-term heavy use leaves permanent changes. Unlike breakthrough-marks, which are discrete, corruption is gradual and accumulates across years.

**Physical corruption** is densification. The lifelong kinetic becomes literally denser-boned and heavier; her joints stiffen in old age; her skin roughens. Preston's stillness — the quality of standing too still, moving without apparent effort — is corruption.

**Matter/Energy corruption** varies by affinity. Pyrokinetics develop heat tolerance and eventually a characteristic dryness of skin; stone-shapers mineralize; metal-workers acquire electromagnetic sensitivities that make them uncomfortable in thunderstorms.

**Biological corruption** is ambient spillover. Lifelong healers find that plants thrive in their homes and that small animals seek them out; shapeshifters lose track of which form is native; animal-communicators acquire mannerisms from the species they bond with.

**Temporal/Spatial corruption** is position-uncertainty. The long-term teleporter is never entirely sure where she is. The long-term phase-user passes unconsciously through thin walls at night.

**Perceptual corruption** is loss of thought-privacy. The lifelong telepath cannot quite maintain the boundary between what she thought and what she heard thought, and over decades the distinction erodes.

**Eldritch corruption** is transformation. It is visible within a year of significant use and total within a decade. This is why eldritch practitioners are feared: the cost is written on them.

**Auratic corruption** is personality fixation. The fear-warlord becomes unable to feel anything but the emotional texture of his own aura. The calm-mediator eventually cannot be angry. The trust-auratic is betrayed repeatedly because she cannot feel what distrust would feel like.

All of this takes years. A character who has just manifested is unmarked. A character who has been working hard for five years shows subtle signs. A character who has been a retinue captain for a decade is visibly her work.

## Species and Powers

The ten metahuman species are described in Chapter 3. Each species has a characteristic biological enhancement that functions as a kind of baseline manifestation — even species members who are otherwise unmanifested have species-typical capacities. These are not tiered; they are the species itself.

Species members also manifest in the ordinary way, within the seven domains, and their species-typical enhancements count toward their profile. A Species I individual with enhanced vision as a species trait who also manifests a Perceptual telepathic capacity reads as Perceptual-primary with the species trait as her secondary. Species traits are not additional to manifestation; they are part of the same system.

## Children and Manifestation

Children born before the Onset and who were under puberty at its occurrence did not manifest immediately. They manifest at puberty, as their bodies mature. The first large cohort of such children is now reaching manifestation age, and the early returns suggest that the post-Onset-raised generation manifests at slightly higher rates and with slightly more multi-domain profiles than the Onset generation did.

Children born after the Onset — now reaching ages where manifestation can be predicted, though none have manifested yet — are universally expected to manifest. It has become the central event of adolescence in the post-Onset world. Families prepare; communities celebrate; the rare child who fails to manifest is a social problem that each community handles differently.

A child's likely domain can sometimes be predicted by a skilled biokinetic. The prediction is correct about sixty-five percent of the time. Wealthy families hire consultants. Poor families watch their children and guess.

# CHAPTER 3: THE TEN SPECIES

One in ten humans in the mid-Atlantic is no longer, strictly speaking, human. The Onset rewrote their biology along one of ten distinct patterns, each clustered geographically by ancestry, each coherent enough to breed true. A Species C couple has Species C children. A Species C and a Species I couple have, depending on ancestry, mostly baseline human children, with a significant minority matching one or the other parent's species.

The ten species are not ranked. They are different, not better or worse. Most species are cosmetically subtle — a careful observer can tell at a glance, but a crowd obscures them. A few (Species F and J particularly) are visibly striking at any distance.

The mid-Atlantic is a secondary region for all ten species. None has its primary global cluster here. Three species (C, G, H) have secondary clusters large enough to form coherent communities; one species (I) has a diaspora of over two million, large enough to constitute a regional political presence in its own right. The other six species are present only in minor numbers.

What follows is a working description of each species and how it fits into the region's life.

## Species I — The Wide-Sighted

Primary cluster: Central America, from Mexico through the Yucatán. In the mid-Atlantic: a diaspora of approximately two million one hundred thousand, concentrated in western Philadelphia, the Camden corridor, and the Prince George's County region south of Washington. The only species large enough to matter as a regional political actor.

Species I is visually marked by the eyes. The iris is expanded significantly — covering most of what would be the white — and often colored in ways baseline humans do not display: amber, copper, violet, a deep gold. In low light the eyes reflect with a subtle tapetal sheen. The species sees further than baseline humans, clearly in near-total darkness, and across a slightly wider spectrum that includes some ultraviolet. Most usefully, Species I can recognize each other at distances where baseline humans could not distinguish faces. A Species I in a crowd of a thousand will spot another Species I at five hundred meters.

The species is organized in the mid-Atlantic into six major kin-networks, each named for its eldest living elder or the family line from which that elder descends. The Flores-kin is largest, centered in Prince George's County, at roughly four hundred and seventy thousand. The Marquez-kin holds western Philadelphia. The Herrera-kin holds North Philadelphia and Camden. The Diaz-kin is in the inner districts of Washington. The Vasquez-kin is in Anacostia and Prince George's. The Reyes-kin, smallest of the major six, is scattered across Wilmington and the Delaware corridor.

Inter-kin governance happens through quarterly assemblies — not decision-making bodies but information-sharing, consensus-building. There is no Species I polity. There has not been a formal declaration that the diaspora is a nation. There are strong voices on both sides of whether there should be.

Elder Josefina Flores of Prince George's, eighty years old, a former public school principal, holds more influence than anyone over the question. She is conservative, in the literal sense — she wishes to conserve the present arrangement, the dense informal coordination, the absence of a single target for other factions to hate. Against her stands Tomás Reyes of Wilmington, mid-forties, T6 perceptual, the most powerful Species I individual in the region, an argumentative advocate of formal polity declaration. The vote is not set. It could come within a year or within two decades, depending on how much external pressure accumulates.

Every major faction in the region has a position on Species I. The Federal Continuity Command courts them — it needs their healers, translators, and community coordinators. The Philadelphia Bourse backs them covertly with capital and trade access. The Iron Crown of Newark distrusts them ideologically. The Potomac Irregulars, Reeve's force in northern Virginia, are actively hostile and have fought low-grade border violence with the Vasquez-kin for months. The Central Jersey League is friendly-neutral. The Delmarva Harvest Lords are cordial; Lord Inez Velasquez of southern Delmarva is herself Species I.

What Species I wants, collectively, is to survive as itself. The diaspora has held together through a year that killed everyone else's institutions. Whether it coheres into a formal polity or not, it is one of the central political facts of the region.

## Species C — The Silver-Hand

Primary cluster: East Asia, particularly Japan and the Korean peninsula. In the mid-Atlantic: a secondary cluster of approximately one hundred seventy-eight thousand, concentrated in Flushing and Sunset Park in the outer boroughs and in the Edison corridor in central New Jersey.

Species C is subtle. The skin of the palms and inner wrists takes on a metallic sheen — silver or platinum, depending on the individual — that is visible at close distance and invisible in a crowd. Their hands can subtly reshape non-living material through contact. A Species C potter smooths clay more finely than any human hand. A Species C metalworker polishes steel to tolerances industrial processes could not achieve. This is not a combat ability; a Species C cannot throw matter or sharpen it into projectiles. It is a craft ability, and in the post-Onset world, craft is wealth.

The Flushing-Edison Cluster is organized around the Twelve Hands — a rotating council of master-artisans drawn from workshops across the three settlement districts. The Twelve Hands rule binds all Cluster members on matters of external trade and inter-faction relations. Internal matters — disputes within a workshop, family disputes, apprenticeship disputes — are handled at the workshop level, rarely escalated.

Master Iwo Kuan is the current eldest on the Twelve Hands. Pre-Onset he was a watch repair specialist in Queens. Now he is the most skilled small-matter worker in the mid-Atlantic, and his workshop in Flushing produces pieces — mostly mechanical, some jewelry, a few surgical instruments — that cannot be reproduced elsewhere. He lives in a fortified workshop above Flushing's central market. He is sixty-four, patient, and deeply cautious about the Cluster's external exposure.

The Cluster trades primarily with the Philadelphia Bourse and the Queens Commonage. Its relationship with the Tower Lords of Brooklyn, who surround two of its three settlements, is transactional and wary. Tower Lords do not attack Species C directly — the Cluster's goods are too valuable — but they extract tolls on movement and occasionally seize workshops whose owners cannot pay.

Species C has no ambitions beyond the Cluster. It wants to be left alone to work. It has the commercial leverage to secure this most of the time.

## Species H — The Quick-Blooded

Primary cluster: South America, particularly the Brazilian coast and the River Plate basin. In the mid-Atlantic: a secondary cluster of approximately one hundred seventy-four thousand, distributed across North Jersey (Paterson, Passaic, Union), Bucks County in Pennsylvania, and outer Queens.

Species H is slightly smaller than baseline humans and runs hotter. Metabolism is accelerated; wounds close in hours rather than days; infection is rare. A Species H takes an injury that would kill a baseline human and walks it off with a week of hard sleep. This is not combat regeneration — in combat a Species H dies like anyone — but between injuries, Species H recovers. It is why they make the best couriers in the region.

The North Jersey Hs are not territorially consolidated. They maintain twenty to thirty township-level concentrations across three states, bound together by a cross-enclave courier network that functions as distributed governance. Teenagers and young adults run weekly routes between enclaves. Messages, decisions, and news move along these routes at speeds no other network in the region matches.

The courier network is also the enforcement mechanism. An enclave that defies the rough consensus of the other enclaves loses access to the network, which means it loses access to medical services, kin connections, and trade coordination — effective economic isolation. This has happened twice since the Onset. Both enclaves capitulated within three months.

The network's effectiveness was demonstrated in Month 4, when Iron Crown forces seized a Species H workshop in Newark. Within a week, Iron Crown had lost access to every Species H healer and craftsman in the region. The blockade lasted six months, by which point Iron Crown had paid reparations and issued formal apologies. Volk does not forget. The Species H network does not relax; they are quietly better armed than any warlord realizes.

Sofia Cortese — known as the Sparrow for her speed along the northern routes — is the senior courier coordinator in the Paterson enclave. Mid-thirties, pre-Onset a neonatal ICU nurse. She is not in charge, officially; there is no officially. But when a message from the network carries her mark, every enclave treats it as authoritative.

Species H is Muslim in plurality — perhaps sixty percent, with strong pockets of Catholicism particularly among older Brazilians-by-descent. Imams and priests are central figures in the enclaves.

## Species G — The Sun-Worn

Primary cluster: Western Europe, particularly the British Isles and the Germanic north. In the mid-Atlantic: a secondary cluster of approximately one hundred sixty-eight thousand, concentrated in the Princeton-New Brunswick corridor and parts of the Lehigh Valley.

Species G is the most subtle in appearance. The skin develops a faint weathering or ruddiness even in individuals kept indoors; hair greys twenty years earlier than baseline. The species ability is biokinetic: a species-typical connection to plant life and soil, expressed as unusual agricultural success even in individuals who have never manifested an ordinary Biological capacity. Species G children often show T1 or T2 biokinetic tendencies before puberty, a pattern not seen in other species.

Species G is the most institutionally integrated of the cluster species. Rather than forming a distinct community structure, Species G has embedded itself within the existing Central Jersey League and Lehigh Principalities as agricultural specialists, councilors, and technical advisors. Several Lehigh Principalities have Species G councilors with real advisory authority. The Central Jersey League's biokinetic agriculture program has a Species G faction within it running seed development.

Councilor Anna Veldt, T3 biokinetic, serves as chief agricultural advisor to the Allentown Principality and manages inter-Principality seed exchange. She is respected across faction lines; the Philadelphia Bourse occasionally consults her on crop-futures valuations. She does not hold formal power and does not seek it. Her influence flows through expertise.

Species G's survival rate in its primary region was unusually high — ninety-five percent of the pre-Onset population survived the first year — for reasons that remain unclear. The species is small in the mid-Atlantic, large in its home region, and apparently thriving.

## Species A — The Hollow-Boned

Primary cluster: East Asia, specifically the Yangtze basin and the Korean peninsula. In the mid-Atlantic: minimal presence, approximately forty-six thousand scattered.

Species A is taller and leaner than baseline humans, bone density reduced by thirty to forty percent, limbs slightly extended. A Species A individual weighs sixty to seventy percent of a baseline human of equivalent height. They move efficiently, require less food, sleep less, tolerate altitude better. Their species trait is temporal-spatial: most Species A individuals have an innate capacity to foresee immediate harm by fractions of a second, enough to flinch out of the way of a thrown stone or swung club before it connects.

The tradeoff is fragility. Species A bones break more easily. Fall damage is severe. A Species A in hand-to-hand combat against a Species F loses.

Species A in the mid-Atlantic operates as scattered family networks, often integrated into trade or scholarly roles. They are disproportionately found in the Philadelphia Bourse's merchant houses and in the scholarly institutions (Princeton, Rutgers).

## Species B — The Deep-Voiced

Primary cluster: South Asia, the Gangetic plain. In the mid-Atlantic: approximately forty-four thousand, mostly in immigrant-descended communities of metropolitan areas.

Species B is broader-framed than baseline humans, with an enlarged chest cavity and modified vocal apparatus. They produce sounds baseline humans cannot — voice that carries three to five times normal, sub-vocal infrasound used for disorientation, sensitive hearing. Species B orators are disproportionately effective at persuasion and command. Their speech carries weight; their communication at distance requires no radio.

Species B took the worst losses of any species in the Onset year — their home region's collapse conditions were acute. The diaspora in the mid-Atlantic is small and culturally marked. Species B communities maintain elaborate oral and musical traditions; they are known locally as the Singers.

## Species D — The Pale-Eyed

Primary cluster: Eastern Europe, the Russian plain and Baltic coast. In the mid-Atlantic: approximately ninety-six thousand, with a notable outer-Brooklyn enclave of three thousand in historically Eastern European neighborhoods.

Species D has pale skin and light eyes that reflect in low light. The species trait is perceptual — low-light vision, sensitivity to intent, and an auratic-style presence that causes baseline humans to trust or fear them in ways they themselves do not understand. Species D members are disproportionately found as mediators, judges, and confidence-intensive roles.

## Species E — The Slow-Breath

Primary cluster: South America, Amazon basin, Andean Chile and Peru. In the mid-Atlantic: approximately fifty-six thousand, scattered.

Species E has a slowed metabolism — six to eight breaths per minute at rest, caloric requirements at sixty percent of baseline, breath-hold capacity of six to ten minutes. They are more cold-tolerant than baseline humans, less heat-tolerant, resistant to toxins and pollutants. Most distinctively, they can enter conscious torpor — a state of reduced metabolism lasting days to weeks, from which they emerge functional. A Species E can survive a winter on supplies that would feed a baseline human for a week.

In the mid-Atlantic, Species E individuals are occasionally found as frontier scouts, long-haul couriers, and deep-wilderness specialists. The capacity to enter torpor makes them unusually suited to occupations others cannot sustain.

## Species F — The Broad-Shouldered

Primary cluster: Western Europe, France, Iberia, Italy. In the mid-Atlantic: approximately seventy-one thousand, minimal presence.

Species F is physically imposing. Fifteen to twenty-five percent larger than baseline humans by frame, with proportional muscle mass and bone density. An average Species F adult stands six-foot-one to six-foot-seven and weighs two hundred to two hundred and fifty pounds. The species trait is kinetic-adjacent: enhanced strength, endurance, durability. Their children often show low-tier kinetic capacity even before formal manifestation.

In the mid-Atlantic, Species F are valued as guardsmen and shock troops, often integrated into warlord retinues as bodyguards. The Iron Crown employs approximately two dozen Species F soldiers; the Federal Continuity Command perhaps sixty.

## Species J — The Stone-Silent

Primary cluster: sub-Saharan Africa, West African coast and Sahel. In the mid-Atlantic: approximately thirty-three thousand, minimal presence.

Species J is taller than baseline humans — two hundred centimeters average — with elevated bone density and muscle mass, and a characteristic stillness of demeanor. Species J individuals hold physical position with remarkable precision; they do not fidget. The species trait is auratic: a zone of steadiness, perhaps three to five meters around each individual, in which baseline humans find panic harder and careful decision-making easier. In crisis, a Species J's mere presence is stabilizing.

Species J took the second-worst losses of any species in the Onset year — their home region's collapse was severe. The mid-Atlantic diaspora is small and quietly respected, disproportionately found as crisis mediators, surgical assistants, and advisors in factions that value calm decision-making.

## Inter-Species Reproduction

A baseline human and a baseline human produce baseline children. A baseline and a metahuman produce children that are, seventy-five to eighty-five percent of the time, baseline, with the remainder matching the metahuman parent's species. Two metahumans of the same species produce that species. Two metahumans of different species — an unusual pairing — produce children that survive to birth only rarely, perhaps five percent of the time, and those who do survive show characteristics predominantly of one parent's species.

Intermarriage across species lines is legal in most factions and socially fraught to varying degrees. The Philadelphia Bourse treats it as unremarkable. Species I Flores-kin is internally divided — older generations resist, younger ones marry across. Species C Twelve Hands prefers endogamy without forbidding exogamy. Species H is quietly endogamous. Species G is the most assimilationist of the species and intermarries freely.

## The Minor Diasporas

Beyond the six species clusters described above, Species A, B, D, E, F, and J are present in the mid-Atlantic as family networks and neighborhood enclaves rather than as coherent communities. Individually they are below the threshold of organized political action. Collectively they represent approximately three hundred and forty-six thousand people — a meaningful population, but one that has not and probably will not form a unified voice. They are present. They are recognized. They are not, in any faction's strategic calculation, a variable.

# CHAPTER 4: THE FIRST YEAR

What follows is the history of the year between the Onset and the campaign's opening moment. It is the common knowledge of any adult character in the region — the shared memory of what has happened, told in the sequence it happened.

## Month One: The Collapse

**The first hour.** Every aircraft in the sky went down. Every car in motion stopped. The subway system trapped two hundred and ninety trains in tunnels with no power and no ventilation. Hospitals lost their life-support. The first-hour death toll in the mid-Atlantic alone is estimated at nine hundred thousand to one million four hundred thousand.

The manifestation wave struck simultaneously. Forty-eight million people in the region gained powers in a single afternoon. Most did not understand what had happened. A substantial fraction manifested destructively: kinetic force released into sidewalks, fire into crowded rooms, matter reshaped catastrophically. An estimated twenty-five thousand people died in the first three hours in Manhattan alone from accidental manifestation.

At the same time, roughly four point eight million people in the region underwent species transformation. Their bodies restructured overnight. Most did not know what they were until they found a mirror the next morning.

And roughly four hundred and eighty thousand people in the region transformed into something no longer human. Most died quickly — killed by panicked bystanders, or by self-recognition, or by the simple fact that their new bodies could not sustain life. The survivors fled into the wilderness and the ruins.

**The first week.** Water distribution failed. Manhattan's pumps stopped on day three; Brooklyn's the same week. Cholera cases began in Queens by day six. The Federal Continuity Command began to assemble at Andrews Air Force Base on day two, initially three thousand military personnel and eight thousand civilian government employees. The remaining federal executive was scattered or dead.

In Philadelphia, a group of newly-manifested biokinetic healers began operating out of the Inner Harbor Church — forty people treating the wave of refugees from the collapsing hospital system. This was the founding act of what would become the Bourse's medical infrastructure.

In Newark, a longshoreman named Antonín Volk took control of the central dock area with a core of loyalists. His first week of consolidation killed roughly three hundred people. It set the tone that persists today.

**The second week.** Starvation was not yet widespread, but food ran out in Manhattan apartment buildings. The first attempts at mass urban exodus began — people leaving on foot, on bicycles, in hand-drawn carts. Highway violence was catastrophic. An estimated eight thousand people died at the Goethals Bridge alone over four days.

In the Hudson Valley, the Peekskill reactor approached criticality. Its cooling systems had failed on Onset day; backup diesels failed within forty-eight hours. By day ten, containment was approaching structural compromise.

A former structural engineer named Dmitri Kostas — who had manifested that day as a low-tier matter-manipulator — arrived at the site with a small team and began forty hours of continuous containment work. During those forty hours, his tier rose from six to seven at hour twelve, from seven to eight at hour twenty-four. His body mineralized along his left side as defensive Physical-domain secondary emerged. He stabilized the containment. The reactor did not fail. He is the Bear-House of Peekskill, and that reactor is still, one year later, the only source of electrical power in the mid-Atlantic.

**The third week.** Cholera and typhoid became endemic across the region. Water-borne disease killed perhaps two and a half percent of the pre-Onset NYC metro population in that week alone. Biokinetic healers in the proto-Queens Commonage saved an estimated one hundred and twenty thousand lives.

In the Pine Barrens, the first reports of *something in the trees* reached small settlements. Seven refugees from Camden encountered a figure in the woods near Batsto who spoke languages none of them had ever heard. Two left. Five remained. The Listening cult was founded.

**The fourth week.** Approximately twenty to twenty-five percent of the pre-Onset NYC metro population was already dead. Antonín Volk had consolidated the Port of Newark and was extending claims over Jersey City. In Brooklyn, five candidate warlords had begun the territorial negotiations that would become the Tower Lords. In Washington, the Federal Continuity Command formalized its structure under General Sarah Alcott — a council of military and civilian officers, institutional authority without personal combat capacity.

## Month Two: The Thrones Begin

The Hudson Valley stabilized first. Kostas expanded outward from Peekskill through month two; his demesne held ninety thousand people by its end.

In the Catskills, a T9 manifested fully. Colonel James Preston, retired Special Forces, on vacation in the mountains on Onset day. He had manifested at T6 on day one — a narrow Physical kinetic specialist — and rose through three breakthroughs during the following month: defending refugees from a pack of wretches (T6 to T7), stopping a landslide above his camp (T7 to T8), surviving a direct confrontation with fifteen hunters (T8 to T9). Each breakthrough concentrated him further in kinetic specialization rather than broadening his profile. He is the classic narrow-path specialist.

By the end of month two he had issued a call to the Hudson passes: every T6+ kinetic user in the region who wished to serve should come to him. Forty-one came. The Catskill Throne was founded.

The urban exodus violence peaked in weeks seven and eight. Starvation peaked in the same period. In Lower Manhattan, the surviving population hunted, salvaged, and in some cases resorted to cannibalism. The Financial District emptied almost completely. Something began to make itself known there, though it was not yet called the Echo.

## Months Three through Six: Mapping the Factions

By the end of month three, most of the region's factions had named themselves and taken their approximate shapes. The Central Jersey League began under Dr. Maya Okafor of Rutgers, whose agricultural biokinetic expertise drew fifteen townships into the first formal multi-township federation. The Delmarva Harvest Lords established themselves independently, one lord per biokinetic-enhanced demesne. The Tower Lords of Brooklyn consolidated their five-faction arrangement. The Staten Citadel under Viktor Chen converted the harbor facilities into a functional port.

Starvation declined dramatically in month three as biokinetic agriculture began producing. The Delmarva Lords' first large-scale crops came in during October — biokinetic-accelerated corn, potatoes, and winter wheat. Food began flowing north.

The winter of the first year was the final test. Inadequate heating, inadequate water purification, inadequate food storage. Winter mortality across the region was twelve percent of the then-remaining population. The factions that survived the winter intact emerged into spring as the stable powers.

In month five, the Iron Crown made its first major salvage expedition into Manhattan — forty people operating out of Newark, crossing through the Staten ferry route with a negotiated toll. The expedition's success established the pattern that persists today: Manhattan as salvage frontier, accessible via Iron Crown's resources and the Staten Citadel's harbor, with a tense and escalating conflict between the two powers over the division of revenue.

By the end of month six, the collapse dynamics had exhausted themselves. The populations that were going to die from collapse had died. The survivors had organized. The regional population had stabilized at approximately seventeen to eighteen million — a sixty-five percent loss from pre-Onset. Further attrition would continue through the first year but at a much slower rate.

## Months Seven through Nine: The Market Emerges

Biokinetic agricultural techniques — which had emerged independently in multiple places — began to diffuse through the network of practitioners. By the end of month nine, approximately sixty percent of the region's biokinetic practitioners were using saturation-yield techniques. Food surplus began appearing in Delmarva, Central New Jersey, and Yonkers.

Month eight: the Philadelphia Bourse issued its first stamped copper coinage, backed by accumulated pre-Onset silver and copper from bank vaults and new copper from the Lehigh mines. The initial issue was fifty thousand coins in three denominations. Within six weeks, the Bourse copper was accepted across Philadelphia metro, Delmarva, and Washington. It is now the standard currency of the region.

Month nine saw the Iron Crown's first open confrontation with the Staten Citadel. Volk attempted to bypass the Staten harbor toll with his own small-boat fleet. Chen seized three boats and their crews. The Philadelphia Bourse mediated a stand-down. The tensions have continued to escalate since.

## Months Ten through Twelve: Approaching Game Start

Month ten: the Species I kin-networks held their fourth formal assembly. Tomás Reyes argued for polity declaration. Elder Flores argued for continued informal coordination. The vote was deferred.

Month eleven: the Federal Continuity Command's reach visibly contracted. A mission to re-establish authority in Baltimore failed — the Crabclaw Confederation successfully resisted. The failure damaged Alcott's credibility. The Potomac Irregulars under Colonel Harlan Reeve became visibly more independent.

Month twelve, days before game start: the Listening issued its first formal territorial claim. A transformed human messenger — visibly corrupted — delivered simultaneous statements to the Philadelphia Bourse, the Central Jersey League, and the Iron Crown, declaring the central Pine Barrens "theirs." The statements were delivered at the same moment in three places, impossible to explain by conventional means. The Pine Barrens are now formally off-limits to human travel.

This is where the campaign begins. The region has survived its first year. The equilibrium holds. Ten macro-clocks measure the tensions that will break it, and every one of them is advancing.

# CHAPTER 5: THE REGION

The mid-Atlantic is divided in this book into twelve zones. Each is described below in the form a traveler would need: what is there, who controls it, what a party can do there, what is dangerous about it. Populations are rounded.

## Washington and the Federal Core

**Population: four million eight hundred thousand.** The largest surviving concentrated population in the region, held together by the Federal Continuity Command under General Sarah Alcott.

The core is the old federal triangle — the Pentagon, Andrews Air Force Base, and Quantico to the south — extended outward through Arlington, Bethesda, and northern Virginia. Alcott's authority is strongest within fifty kilometers of the Pentagon. Beyond that, twenty regional deputies govern Districts with significant autonomy.

**Who rules.** General Alcott governs through a military council with a civilian shadow-government. The military is the senior partner. The civilian government exists and operates courts and records, but it does not overrule the general.

**What you can do.** Federal Continuity scrip is the only paper currency in the region; it trades against Bourse copper at roughly ten to one. Public civil courts operate — the only in the region — and accept cases from non-citizens if both parties consent. Pentagon stockpiles include functional smallarms, ammunition, and the last large reserve of pre-Onset medical supplies anywhere. Georgetown University's library is open to scholars with sponsorship.

**What is dangerous.** The southern District — Colonel Harlan Reeve's Virginia command — is nominally loyal and functionally independent. Reeve's Potomac Irregulars answer to his orders, not Alcott's, and a political crisis is approaching. Species I populations in Prince George's and Anacostia are formally protected and practically second-class. Travelers identified as Species I in the outer Districts can expect harassment from Irregular patrols.

**Notable sites.** The Pentagon, operational as the Command headquarters. Andrews Air Force Base, nominal air command with no functional aircraft. The Mall, partially restored, where civic ceremonies are still held. Georgetown University, active as archive and educational institution. Arlington National Cemetery, maintained as symbolic territory. Anacostia, holding the Species I Vasquez-kin concentration.

## The Outer Boroughs and Long Island

**Population: four million nine hundred thousand.** Manhattan's refugee population reconstituted across the boroughs.

This zone contains five political entities: the five districts of the Tower Lords of Brooklyn (roughly one million one hundred thousand combined), the Queens Commonage (four hundred and eighty thousand), the Staten Citadel (two hundred and sixty thousand), the Flushing-Edison Cluster of Species C (approximately forty thousand in the Flushing-Sunset Park concentrations, plus smaller enclaves), and scattered independent neighborhoods. Long Island beyond Queens is sparsely held — farming communities, militia-scale fortifications, no single authority.

**Who rules.** Five Tower Lords govern Brooklyn's districts, perpetually rearranging alliances. The Queens Commonage operates as a federation of neighborhoods around a perceptual council. The Staten Citadel is a single-lord polity. The outer areas are mosaic.

**What you can do.** Manhattan salvage is the region's largest extractive industry. Tower Lord salvage operations pay forty to eighty copper per day with share in recovered goods. Staten's harbor is the only functional port between Newark and Philadelphia; passage across is negotiated by the Bridgemaster. Flushing's central market trades the finest small-matter craft in the region. Jamaica Bay fishing is contested and profitable.

**What is dangerous.** Manhattan. Anything south of Canal Street is the Echo's territory, and time does not behave correctly there. The Bronx north of the old highway system is effectively abandoned to hunters and wretches. Inter-Tower-Lord violence is common and unpredictable. Staten's toll-collection is non-negotiable.

**Notable sites.** The 42nd Street Line, the informal boundary between intact Midtown and anomalous Lower Manhattan. Central Park, a biokinetically-altered wildland of fast-growing flora and altered fauna. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, largely intact and essentially untouched, no faction having the means to move or appraise the collection. The Brooklyn Navy Yard, operational under Staten sub-claim. Flushing Meadows, the Cluster heartland. Coney Island, holdout territory for a commune that refuses Tower Lord vassalage.

## Manhattan

**Population: two hundred and fifty thousand.** Scattered across the ruined borough in communities of one hundred to ten thousand.

Manhattan is technically claimed by no faction. The island is treated as a commons for salvage operations by several — Tower Lords, Iron Crown, Staten, and smaller independents — all operating under informal agreements to avoid each other.

The population here is some who refused to leave, some who could not, some who came back. They survive by trading salvage to outside factions for food and medicine. The communities are tough, insular, and not friendly to outsiders without business.

**What you can do.** Salvage, if you have the means and the stomach. The deep storage of pre-Onset warehouses occasionally yields intact pharmaceuticals — a single course of antibiotics trades for eight hundred copper in Philadelphia. Museum recovery is lucrative for specialists. Certain buildings hold technical equipment (medical imaging machines, specific tools) that matter-manipulators can sometimes restore.

**What is dangerous.** Everything south of Canal Street, which is the Echo's territory. Central Park, which is a wildland. The Financial District, where memory does not persist. The streets themselves, which in heavily-corrupted districts are physically warped — floors that are not level, corners that are not ninety degrees, dust that does not settle. Mound-builder rats in enormous numbers. Occasional altered fauna from the Park spilling into adjacent streets.

**Notable sites.** The Echo's territory, Financial District to roughly 14th Street, where time and memory fail. Central Park, biokinetic wildland. The Peekskill Ruin of the Lower East Side, heavily kinetic-corrupted from early warlord violence, streets physically reshaped. The Metropolitan Museum. The 42nd Street Line.

## The Hudson Valley and Westchester

**Population: two million eight hundred and seventy thousand.** The most stable polity zone in the region, governed by a ring of tributary relationships.

Three major polities hold the valley. The Bear-House of Peekskill, founded by Dmitri Kostas around the functioning reactor. The Yonkers Compact, the largest horizontal polity in the region, a rotating assembly governed by a council of three biokinetic healers. Scattered minor lords and tributary towns in between.

Everyone in the valley ultimately answers to the Catskill Throne. The tribute arrangement is ritualized — a wagon of grain per season, three cords of wood, one work-horse, and increasingly, one youth per tributary village to the Throne's training hall.

**Who rules.** Kostas at Peekskill. The Three Judges at Yonkers — Olamide Obi, Natasha Rubinstein, and Sonia Villanueva, all biokinetic and widely regarded as the finest healers in the mid-Atlantic. Preston at Mount Tremper, distant but real. Smaller lords locally.

**What you can do.** The Peekskill reactor provides limited electricity — the only electrical grid in the region. Buildings on it can operate pre-Onset equipment that is nowhere else functional. Yonkers operates the region's premier medical facility, and hires healers from across the region for difficult cases. The valley is safe for travel; Catskill Throne patrols maintain the passes.

**What is dangerous.** The Catskill wilderness proper, off the tributary roads, is dangerous. The Hollow Choir — a Warped pack in the eastern Catskill valleys — is effectively off-limits even to Throne patrols. Mourning bears range in the upper Poconos. The Ramapo Vine, the northern outpost of the Hive, closes certain mountain routes entirely.

**Notable sites.** The Peekskill Reactor, Kostas's seat, the only functional electricity. Yonkers Healing Hall, where the Three Judges preside. Mount Tremper, Preston's command site, hardened pre-Onset federal facility. Kingston, a major Yonkers trade town. West Point, ruined, briefly seized by a would-be warlord whom Preston destroyed in month two, now vacant.

## The Catskills

**Population: five hundred and thirty thousand.** Refugees from New York City absorbed into mountain villages, loosely held under the Throne.

The Catskill zone has grown during the post-Onset period — the only major zone in the region to gain population, as mountain villages that were nearly empty before the Onset have filled with refugees seeking defensible terrain.

**Who rules.** Preston, through approximately one hundred and sixty T5+ retinue officers who act as his regional lieutenants. Marshal Thomas Whelan commands military. Beatrice Amar administers the hall. The Shadow (identity undisclosed) handles intelligence.

**What you can do.** Serve Preston. The Throne accepts petitioners; Preston personally tests anyone who wishes to join his retinue. Mountain agriculture, hunting, and lumber work are the civilian economies. Kaaterskill Falls and other minor sites have small independent demesnes with specific specialties.

**What is dangerous.** Preston's Throne Hall contains a truth-field maintained by Beatrice Amar. Lies are very hard to tell there. The Hollow Choir territory in the eastern valleys is avoided. The Ramapo Vine blocks northern routes. Off the tributary roads, altered fauna — mourning bears, spike-antler elk, bonded wolf packs — are the norm rather than the exception.

**Notable sites.** Mount Tremper Command Site, the Throne. Kaaterskill Falls, held by Marla of the Falls (T5 biokinetic, independent). The Hollow Choir's range, ceded territory.

## Newark, Jersey City, and the Industrial Corridor

**Population: seven hundred and forty thousand.** Concentrated around Port Newark, held by the Iron Crown.

**Who rules.** Antonín Volk, T8, through approximately thirty captains, each controlling a neighborhood. There is no institutional structure; rule flows through personal violence and captains' loyalty backed by Volk's dread-field.

**What you can do.** Port Newark is the only deep-water industrial harbor in warlord hands. Labor is in demand — dock work, salvage crews, retinue soldiering. Wages are higher than average: a working laborer earns eighty to a hundred copper a month; a retinue soldier three hundred to four hundred. Iron Crown chits circulate within territory at a twenty-to-one discount to Bourse copper and are worthless outside.

**What is dangerous.** Everything. The Iron Crown's captains answer to Volk and to ambition; they enforce their neighborhoods with impunity. Species I and Species H travelers are at elevated risk — Volk's enmity with Species H is formal, and Species I are distrusted on principle. The refugee camps on the outer perimeter are effectively lawless; theft, extortion, and conscription into day-labor are routine.

**Notable sites.** Port Newark Dockyard, Iron Crown's capital. Jersey City Heights, Brick Stepanski's district. Liberty State Park, the staging ground for Manhattan salvage operations. The old railyard, where the region's homeless cluster in warm months.

## The Central New Jersey Corridor

**Population: one million six hundred and ninety thousand.** The Central Jersey League — fifteen townships from Princeton to Toms River, bound by the Princeton Accord.

The largest working polity in the region by population. Not a sovereignty; a federation. Each township has its own governance. The Accord binds mutual defense, agricultural coordination, shared currency (Bourse copper), and internal non-aggression.

**Who rules.** The Accord Council, one representative per township plus Dr. Maya Okafor of Rutgers as coordinator. Okafor is not a ruler; she is the recognized expert whose advice is regularly followed. The Council has no formal power to impose decisions on a township.

**What you can do.** The safest, most functional zone in the region. Agriculture, trade, scholarship. Princeton University is active; its library, partially preserved, is among the largest in the region. Rutgers hosts the biokinetic training center, where apprenticeships are competitive. The townships welcome skilled trades.

**What is dangerous.** Very little, within the Accord. The Pine Barrens to the south is a different matter. The northern townships feel Iron Crown pressure, and the edge-of-Accord territory is occasionally raided.

**Notable sites.** Princeton University, Accord Hall, the library. Rutgers New Brunswick, biokinetic training. Edison Township, Species C sub-cluster. Toms River, southern anchor. The Princeton Corvid Council — a formal alliance with seven Warped ravens that holds an advisory role in university matters.

## The Pine Barrens

**Population: seventy thousand.** Scattered across the wilderness; the vast majority are now Listening cult members.

The southernmost of the Pine Barrens is effectively closed to human passage. The Listening cult occupies the central and southern Barrens in coordination with the Batsto Sprawl, the largest Hive colony in the region.

**Who rules.** The One Who Waits, through the Listening. The cult has a visible leadership structure of transformed humans — the progressive corruption of the cult's members is one of its public marks — but the One Who Waits is the functional authority.

**What you can do.** Leave. Outsiders entering the Barrens with cult escort are tolerated; those entering without are not. A small trade in pre-Onset artifacts — the Barrens' pre-Onset villages were abandoned on Onset day and have been thoroughly looted by the cult — passes through intermediaries. The Philadelphia Bourse maintains a contact with the cult for exactly this purpose.

**What is dangerous.** The cult. The Hive. The Aberrant eldritch entities that range through the forest. The mourning bears. The bog witches (altered egrets, T4 auratic). The ghost-cougars, which displace themselves spatially when approached. The Listening messengers, who visit the perimeter settlements and return corrupted.

**Notable sites.** Batsto Village, the cult's founding site and primary settlement. Wharton Forest Reserve. The Black Pines Line, the informal boundary. Ong's Hat, abandoned even by the cult, anomalous.

## The Lehigh Valley and the Poconos

**Population: nine hundred and ninety thousand.** Twelve small principalities in coal-valley towns, each led by a T6 or T7 warlord.

**Who rules.** The twelve Principalities are independent lordships that cooperate loosely on trade matters. Allentown is the largest; Councilor Anna Veldt of Species G serves as its chief agricultural advisor and has influence across several Principalities. The Principalities operate the last functional small-scale coal mining in the region, biokinetic-supplemented.

**What you can do.** Labor in the mines pays reasonably — ninety to one hundred and fifty copper a month, with free room in the valley towns. Trade routes through the valley are safe by Principality agreement. The Species G agricultural consultancy is world-class for post-Onset farming.

**What is dangerous.** The Ramapo Mountains to the east hold the Ramapo Vine, the northern Hive outpost. Bonded wolf packs range in the higher elevations. The Old Badger (T8 Warped) holds territory south of Jim Thorpe and accepts tribute from the Principalities — travelers through his range are safe if they carry appropriate documentation.

**Notable sites.** Allentown, the largest Principality. Bethlehem steelworks ruin, source of recoverable industrial metal. Carbon County coal operations. Jim Thorpe, with Species G presence. The Old Badger's territory.

## The Philadelphia Metro

**Population: two million five hundred and twenty thousand.** The region's mercantile capital, governed by the Philadelphia Bourse.

**Who rules.** The Bourse — thirty-one merchant houses, governed by a Council of Thirty-One, with a Factor-General as rotating chief executive. The current Factor-General is Solomon Beltran of House Beltran, T6 perceptual, Species I. The city has no sovereignty in the warlord sense. It has physical enforcement through the South Philly Holding under Marcus Dreng (T7 kinetic), paid eight percent of trade revenue in exchange for harbor security and external defense.

**What you can do.** Commerce, of every kind that the region sustains. The Old Market at 30th Street is the regional hub. The Delaware wharves process trade from Delmarva and points south. House Pereira's Camden operations run Manhattan salvage imports. The Bourse Library is open to members and to outside scholars with sponsorship. The Bourse operates the region's only active printing press — a thousand pages a day, matter-manipulator-supported.

**What is dangerous.** Camden, across the river, is contested. The Schuylkill Grove — a small Hive colony in the upper watershed — requires annual burn-back; the Bourse funds this but the border is never entirely quiet. The Fairmount Park wildland is biokinetically altered and dangerous after dark. The Broker, an Aberrant eldritch entity in outer Camden, attempts occasional communication with salvagers.

**Notable sites.** The Old Market. The Delaware Wharves. Navy Yard, South Philly Holding's seat. The Bourse Library. Camden (Herrera-kin Species I concentration, House Pereira operations, The Broker). Fairmount Park.

## Baltimore and the Chesapeake North

**Population: one million eighty thousand.** The Crabclaw Confederation — three warlords in uneasy cooperation, each holding a district of greater Baltimore.

**Who rules.** Three T7 warlords: the Inner Harbor lord (largest), the Dundalk lord (industrial), the Towson lord (northern). They cooperate against Federal Continuity expansion but do not trust each other.

**What you can do.** The Inner Harbor fishery, operated by the Inner Harbor lord's biokinetic specialists, feeds much of the region. Bay trade — oysters, crabs, fish — moves north to Philadelphia and south through Delmarva. Dundalk salvage operations specialize in industrial metals. Towson's northern orientation makes it a gateway for Lehigh trade coming south.

**What is dangerous.** The Crabclaw-Federal Continuity conflict is active. The border south of Baltimore is periodically raided. Grandfather Silt — a T8 eldritch Sovereign in the southern bay — has sunk three salvage vessels and is the reason the Nanticoke river mouth is a no-go zone. Ironmouth crocodilians have established a presence in the bay tributaries; Delmarva fishing boats occasionally lose crew to them.

**Notable sites.** The Inner Harbor, confederation capital district. Dundalk, industrial partner. Fort McHenry, ruined but fortified as southern defensive line. Towson, northern anchor.

## Delmarva

**Population: one million one hundred and fifty thousand.** Six major biokinetic lords and approximately twelve lesser ones, holding biokinetic-enhanced agricultural demesnes.

**Who rules.** Each Lord independently. Lord Eleanor Marsden holds eastern Delmarva around Cambridge-Easton — the largest producer, famous for biokinetic-altered cattle. Lord Samuel Chen (Species C) holds central Delmarva around Denton-Greensboro and operates the regional seed bank. Lord "Old Hat" Archer holds the north, around Chestertown, known for the unusual calm of his lands. Lord Inez Velasquez (Species I) holds the south around Salisbury. Lord Peter Finch holds the coast at Rehoboth, specializing in fisheries. Brother Tam holds a non-familial monastic demesne north of Dover.

There is no unified Delmarva authority. The lords cooperate commercially and trade extensively; they do not govern jointly.

**What you can do.** Agricultural labor pays well on biokinetic demesnes — a T1 or T2 biokinetic laborer earns one hundred to three hundred copper a month with housing. The region's food exports fund its stability. Brother Tam's monastery accepts novices, particularly orphans. The Salt Judge (T9 Warped) patrols the bay; certain waters are forbidden to nets by his edict.

**What is dangerous.** Less than elsewhere. The Chesapeake Mangrove-Weep, a semi-aquatic Hive colony in the southern tidal interior, closes seasonal navigation. The Tidewater Cluster (eldritch-biological hive) maintains territory adjacent to Grandfather Silt; Lords Velasquez and Finch maintain buffer zones. The Nanticoke is a no-go zone.

**Notable sites.** Chestertown (Archer's seat). Easton (Marsden's seat). Salisbury (Velasquez's seat). Rehoboth Beach (Finch's fisheries). Brother Tam's Monastery, north of Dover.

# CHAPTER 6: THE FACTIONS

What follows is a faction-by-faction reference. Each entry is structured the same way: what the faction is, who leads it, what it wants, what it fears, how it treats outsiders, and the three or four named figures who matter most beyond the leader. Minor figures are indexed separately in the appendix.

The entries focus on what a game-master needs to know to run the faction — how it reacts, what motivates it, how it negotiates, where its limits are. Population figures and geographic holdings are in the gazetteer chapter; they are not repeated here.

## The Federal Continuity Command

**What it is.** A military-civilian hybrid, the largest organized polity in the region, claiming institutional succession from the United States federal government. It has the formal trappings: a uniformed military, paper currency, functioning courts, civil records, a standing bureaucracy. Under those trappings it is a warlord state with a flag.

**Who leads.** General Sarah Alcott, fifty-eight, formerly Deputy Commander of the United States Northern Command. She governs through a council of ten surviving flag-rank officers and a civilian shadow government. Her tier is T3, profile Perceptual-primary with Auratic trust-field secondary. She is not a combatant. Her authority is institutional and ceremonial, backed by the willingness of the military to continue to recognize the pre-Onset chain of command.

**What it wants.** Legitimacy. Alcott's faction — the continuity faction — believes that what is being defended is not a territory but the idea that the United States government never fell. Every ceremony, every uniform, every formal proclamation serves this purpose. The secondary goal is regional consolidation: bringing more of the pre-Onset federal map back under formal authority.

**What it fears.** Colonel Harlan Reeve. Reeve commands the Virginia District and the Potomac Irregulars, nominally a Fed Continuity formation, practically his personal army. He is T8, Physical primary with Auratic command-presence secondary. His troops follow him personally. He has not openly broken with Alcott, but he has stopped pretending his deference is genuine. The Erosion clock measures how long until he acts.

**How it treats outsiders.** Formally. Envoys are received with full diplomatic protocol. Non-citizens who enter Fed Continuity territory are subject to its law, which is broadly fair and slow. Species I is actively courted; Species H and Species C are treated as neutral foreign communities; Species G is welcomed. Commercial relationships with the Philadelphia Bourse, the Central Jersey League, and Delmarva are good. Relations with Crabclaw Confederation are hostile. Relations with the Catskill Throne are cool — Preston has not formally recognized Fed Continuity's authority over any territory, and the two powers studiously ignore each other.

**Named figures beyond Alcott.** Colonel Harlan Reeve — the succession threat. Major Ayelet Sternberg, T6, chief of intelligence, profile Perceptual-primary with Temporal spatial-awareness secondary; she is the competent center of Fed Continuity operations and probably the person most aware of how fragile the institution is. Dr. Reshma Iyer, T4, Biological, surgeon-general equivalent; her medical corps is the largest in the region and her knowledge of pre-Onset medicine is institutionally irreplaceable. Rabbi David Klein, T5 Auratic trust-field with Perceptual secondary; chief of civilian affairs and the reason refugee integration in Fed Continuity territory has gone as smoothly as it has.

## The Iron Crown of Newark

**What it is.** A warlord state in the classical sense: personal rule, backed by violence, expanding where it can and holding what it has through fear. Economically dependent on Port Newark and Manhattan salvage operations.

**Who leads.** Antonín Volk, forty-two, pre-Onset a longshoreman at the Port of Newark. Seized the port in his first week. T8, profile Physical kinetic primary with Auratic dread-field secondary and minor Biological durability tertiary. His dread-field extends roughly thirty meters around him and produces involuntary fear reactions in anyone who has not deliberately conditioned themselves against it. This is why his captains stay loyal — his presence is terrifying beyond what rational assessment can justify.

**What it wants.** More. More port. More salvage. More captains. More territory. Volk is ambitious without specific strategic vision — he takes what he can take, and what he takes he holds. His current practical goal is to break the Staten Citadel's toll-extraction on Manhattan salvage routes.

**What it fears.** Succession. Volk has no heir, no clear second, and no institutional structure to survive him. Any injury that takes him out of the field for more than a few days would trigger a succession war among his captains. He knows this. Every captain knows this. It is the single largest variable in his strategic calculations. The conflict with Staten is as much about demonstrating his continued capacity as about the substantive dispute.

**How it treats outsiders.** Poorly. Envoys are made to wait in cold rooms for hours before being received. Commercial agreements are backed by threat of retaliation rather than by institutional enforcement. Species H travelers are effectively banned — the blockade after Month Four's expropriation incident has not been formally lifted. Species I are distrusted. Philadelphia Bourse representatives are received with grudging courtesy because the Bourse is the only counterparty Volk cannot simply intimidate.

**Named figures beyond Volk.** Brick Stepanski, T7 Physical, captain of the Jersey City waterfront; brutal, competent, ambitious. Chenai Nkomo (Species H), called Razorgrin, T6 Matter/Energy (metal-edge), captain of the Elizabeth salvage yards — notable because most Species H do not serve Iron Crown; Nkomo's personal history is complicated. Petra Voss, called the Butcher, T7 Biological (pain induction), interrogator; her capacity to induce pain by touch makes her the Crown's primary coercive instrument. Henry Giancarlo, called Silent Henry, T6 Perceptual telepath with Auratic trust-presence secondary — the Crown's intelligence chief and the only major figure whose presence is not actively threatening, which is why he is the Crown's face to outside counterparties.

## The Catskill Throne

**What it is.** A personal dominion, the most militarily capable polity in the region, held together by the reputation of a single man.

**Who leads.** Colonel James Preston, fifty, retired Special Forces. T9, profile Physical kinetic narrow specialist with minor Perceptual battlefield awareness. The highest-tier human in the mid-Atlantic. Reclusive; governs through approximately one hundred and sixty T5+ lieutenants.

**What it wants.** Stability. Preston's ambition ended with the crisis that produced him. He does not want to expand. He wants the Hudson passes held, the tribute flowing, the valley below his mountains undisturbed. He is the rare warlord whose goals are conservative in the literal sense.

**What it fears.** His own mortality. Preston has lost the capacity to feel fear — this is the mark left by his breakthroughs — but he is not unaware of what his death would mean. The Throne has no succession structure. If he dies, the hundred and sixty lieutenants will not hold together. His personal safety is the Throne's primary strategic concern.

**How it treats outsiders.** Formally, through lieutenants. Preston himself is rarely seen. Envoys are received by Marshal Whelan or by Beatrice Amar in the Throne Hall. Lies are very hard to tell in the Throne Hall — Amar maintains an Auratic truth-field — and everyone who comes to treat with the Throne knows this. Tributary polities are treated correctly; their tribute is collected on schedule, their internal politics are not interfered with.

**Named figures beyond Preston.** Marshal Thomas Whelan, T7 Physical with Perceptual tactical-awareness; pre-Onset NYPD Emergency Service Unit commander; de facto military commander. Beatrice Amar, T6 Auratic truth-field with Perceptual emotion-reading; hall administrator; the source of the truth-zone effect. The Shadow, identity undisclosed, T6 Temporal/Spatial with remote-viewing secondary; commander of the courier corps, the Throne's intelligence chief, and the figure through whom most of Preston's external business actually flows.

## The Bear-House of Peekskill

**What it is.** A matter-specialist's demesne organized around a functioning nuclear reactor. The only source of electricity in the mid-Atlantic. Tributary to the Catskill Throne, internally independent.

**Who leads.** Dmitri Kostas, forty-three, pre-Onset structural engineer. T8, profile Matter/Energy structural primary with Physical mineralized durability secondary and minor Perceptual structural awareness. His left side is visibly mineralized from the containment crisis; the process continues slowly.

**What it wants.** To expand slowly and methodically. Kostas is an engineer, not a warlord. He holds what he has, improves it, and absorbs adjacent territory when the opportunity is sustainable. He has doubled his demesne's population in the past year without a single battle — refugees fleeing failing areas have come to him because his territory is competently governed and heated in winter.

**What it fears.** Reactor failure. The Peekskill containment is maintained by continuous matter-work. Kostas himself is the primary practitioner; two of his senior officers assist. If all three were incapacitated, the reactor would fail within weeks. This is not widely known. Kostas goes to some length to ensure it stays that way.

**How it treats outsiders.** Professionally. Engineers and scholars are welcomed. Traders are received. Petitioners seeking refuge are screened and often accepted. The Bear-House's relationship with Yonkers Compact is particularly close — biokinetic goods in exchange for electrical power on a small scale.

**Named figures beyond Kostas.** Engineer Kira Morales, T6 Matter/Energy, his chief deputy on reactor work; one of the two others who can maintain the containment. Captain Joseph Orlov, T6 Physical, the demesne's military commander. Dr. Ingrid Volken, T4 Perceptual, the demesne's senior intelligence officer and the reason the Bear-House is usually aware of threats before they arrive.

## The Central Jersey League

**What it is.** A federation of fifteen townships bound by the Princeton Accord. The largest working polity in the region by population. A counter-example to warlord rule.

**Who leads.** Dr. Maya Okafor, fifty-five, pre-Onset agricultural sciences professor at Rutgers. T7, profile Biological primary (agricultural biokinetic plus healing) with Perceptual emotional-reading and Auratic trust-field secondaries. She is the coordinator, not the ruler. The Accord Council has one representative per township; Okafor's authority comes from her expertise and her reputation, not from formal power.

**What it wants.** Agricultural surplus, mutual defense, and the maintenance of the Accord. The League's members do not share a political ideology. They share an arrangement that benefits them all. Preserving that arrangement is the League's consistent goal.

**What it fears.** Succession of its own. Okafor is aging, works constantly, and has no clear successor. Several townships would plausibly defect if Iron Crown attacked during a leadership transition. The Accord is durable but not indestructible.

**How it treats outsiders.** Warmly. The League welcomes trade, scholarship, and sanctuary. Species I is a close ally. Species G is institutionally integrated. Species C Cluster in Edison is a full participant. Bourse commercial relationships are deep. Even Fed Continuity is received cordially, though politically distant. The only faction the League treats with active caution is Iron Crown.

**Named figures beyond Okafor.** The Council rotates, but several figures recur. Tomás Reyes (Species I, Wilmington) participates through his own kin's holdings. Master Iwo Kuan represents Edison. Councilor Anna Veldt (Species G) advises on inter-League agricultural coordination through her Lehigh ties. Professor Idris Afolabi, T3 Perceptual (language and culture intuition), is the League's senior diplomat, handling most external negotiations.

## The Philadelphia Bourse

**What it is.** A mercantile consortium of thirty-one trading houses, not a sovereignty. Issues the region's accepted currency. Operates the region's printing press. Runs the region's most reliable commercial arbitration. Backed militarily by the South Philly Holding in a formal protection arrangement.

**Who leads.** The Council of Thirty-One — one seat per house, two-thirds majority required for decisions. A Factor-General as rotating two-year chief executive. The current Factor-General is Solomon Beltran (Species I), T6 Perceptual with Auratic trust-presence. The Council meets weekly.

**What it wants.** The expansion of commerce under Bourse-backed rules. The Bourse genuinely believes that trade is the foundation of peace, and its strategic choices reflect this — it backs polities that respect contracts, opposes those that do not, and works continuously to expand the territory in which Bourse copper is accepted as currency. Its unstated long-term goal is regional currency union.

**What it fears.** Major faction war that would damage the trade network. Specifically: the Iron Crown-Staten confrontation, the Fed Continuity-Crabclaw border tension, and any scenario in which the Listening breaches the Pine Barrens perimeter. The Bourse has quietly bankrolled mediation in all three.

**How it treats outsiders.** Transactionally. Everyone who comes to trade in good faith is received; everyone who comes to disrupt is refused. The Bourse maintains formal commercial treaties with every major polity in the region except the Listening. Its library is open to scholars with sponsorship. Its arbitration service is open to any party willing to accept binding ruling.

**Named figures beyond Beltran.** Mariam Okonkwo, T5 Perceptual with Auratic personal-magnetism, eldest of House Okonkwo (the largest trading house); the Bourse's senior political figure, consulted on every major decision whether Factor-General or not. Master Saburo Tanaka (Species C), T5 Matter/Energy, premier salvage-restorer; his work in restoring pre-Onset mechanical devices is unmatched. Lina Pereira, T4 Perceptual, House Pereira Camden operations chief; handles Manhattan salvage imports. Marcus Dreng, T7 Physical, lord of the South Philly Holding and the Bourse's military enforcement; contractual ally, not a Bourse member himself.

**What it is.** A mutual-aid commune organized around biokinetic healing capacity. The most equitable polity in the region. Tributary to the Catskill Throne in the minimal sense.

**What it fears.** Being forced to choose. The Compact's neutrality works because no faction has demanded it end. If the Iron Crown-Staten war produces significant casualties needing healing, if Preston demands tribute of healers rather than grain, if the Fed Continuity-Crabclaw conflict comes close enough to require passage — any of these could force the Compact into a position it does not want.

**How it treats outsiders.** Openly. The Compact does not refuse patients based on faction affiliation. An Iron Crown captain and a Potomac Irregular officer have been treated on adjacent beds at the healing hall without incident. The Three Judges will hear cases from non-Compact members if both parties consent.

**Named figures beyond the Three Judges.** Prime Voice Kenji Lee, T6 Perceptual broadband telepath with Auratic trust-field; coordinates the Compact's communication network and its dealings with adjacent polities. Abel, T7 Warped collie, permanent resident of the Compact and informal advisor to the Three Judges — the only Warped with formal standing in a human polity.

**Who leads.** Currently: Ronen "Crown" Marcus (Crown Heights, T7 Physical). Marius Tenno (Flatbush, T7 Physical with minor Auratic). Elena (Bay Ridge, T6 Matter/Energy — she uses no other name). Two others whose names change as Tower Lords are killed and replaced.

**What it is.** A single-lord polity organized around the Staten Island harbor facilities. The only functional spatial-teleportation-based port in the region.

**Named figures beyond Chen.** The Bridgemaster, T6 Temporal/Spatial, harbor security officer; manages the passage permission system and is the face of the Citadel to travelers.

## The Delmarva Harvest Lords

**What it is.** Six major and twelve minor biokinetic lordships across the Delmarva peninsula. The region's agricultural foundation. Not a unified polity.

**Who leads.** Six major lords, described in the gazetteer. The unification clock is at one of eight and advancing slowly — none of the lords has incentive to subordinate to another.

**What they want.** Each wants to hold and improve their demesne. Collectively they want stable trade arrangements and freedom from external pressure. They do not want to govern each other.

**What they fear.** Each other, slightly. External pressure more. If Fed Continuity expanded seriously into Delmarva, the Lords would likely coordinate defense for the first time. If Crabclaw made moves against their bay trade, similarly.

**How they treat outsiders.** Each differently. Lord Archer is the most welcoming; Lord Marsden the most formal; Lord Chen the most commercially sophisticated; Lord Velasquez maintains Species I ties; Lord Finch is focused on coastal matters; Brother Tam welcomes orphans and refugees to the monastery.

**What it is.** Three warlords holding Baltimore districts, cooperating against Federal Continuity expansion, distrusting each other.

The Crabclaw's intelligence on Fed Continuity interior politics is unusually good — they have cultivated informants within the Irregular staff.

## The Listening

**What it is.** A cult, and a polity, and something less classifiable. Organized around the eldritch Sovereign called the One Who Waits. Occupies the central Pine Barrens. Grows steadily.

**Who leads.** The One Who Waits, through a priesthood of progressively transformed humans. The oldest members of the priesthood — five or six individuals who have been with the cult since its founding — are no longer entirely human in appearance. What remains of their personalities is uncertain.

The Philadelphia Bourse's burn-crew operations at the Schuylkill Grove represent exactly the kind of pressure that, applied at scale, could threaten the cult. No faction has yet committed to this.

**How it treats outsiders.** With offers. The Listening's messengers — visibly corrupted cult members — occasionally visit perimeter settlements and speak to individuals they identify as sympathetic. The offers vary. Some accept. Most refuse.

**What it is.** Not yet a polity. Six major kin-networks in diaspora, coordinating through quarterly assemblies. Two million one hundred thousand people, informally governed, increasingly considered to be a collective political actor by outside factions.

**Who leads.** No single figure. Elder Josefina Flores of Prince George's holds the most informal influence. Tomás Reyes of Wilmington is the most powerful individual. The Consolidation clock is at two of eight.

**How they treat outsiders.** Individually. Each kin-network has its own relationships with external factions. The diaspora's collective positions are negotiated at the assemblies and communicated through the senior elders.

# CHAPTER 7: THREATS

Four categories of non-human threat operate in the mid-Atlantic. Each is described below with what it is, where it is, how it behaves, and how it dies.

## The Eldritch

Eldritch entities are the transformed survivors of the one-and-a-half percent of the human population who became, on Onset day, something no longer human. The transformation was not uniform. Different individuals became different things, classified below by their observable behavior.

Roughly two hundred and twenty-five thousand eldritch entities operate in the mid-Atlantic. The majority are individually weak; a small number are individually formidable; a very small number are not classifiable by any framework that applies to the rest.

### Wretches

The majority, roughly sixty percent. Low-function humanoid or partially-humanoid entities, the most common outcome of eldritch transformation. Wretches retain bilateral symmetry, recognizable limbs, occasionally fragments of clothing. They are T1 or T2 equivalent in combat — individually weak — but they hunt in groups and are resistant to conventional damage. Most survive at the margins of agriculture, preying on isolated livestock and occasional travelers.

Wretches do not reproduce in any observed manner. New wretches do not appear; the existing population ages and dies slowly.

### Hunters

Roughly twenty percent of eldritch. Faster, more intact, more capable. Bipedal or quadrupedal; often human-sized or larger. Predatory behavior: stalking, ambush, return to kill sites. T3 to T4 combat equivalent. Hunt in packs of two to six.

Unlike wretches, hunters appear to increase in number over time. Whether new hunters emerge from ambient eldritch-present territory or through some form of conversion from wretches is unclear. The Pine Barrens and the deeper Catskills are hunter-concentrated territory.

### Aberrants

Roughly ten percent. Entities that violate ordinary biology more dramatically — multi-limbed, tumorous, size-irregular, or exhibiting features not present in any terrestrial species. Rarer than hunters, more dangerous. T4 to T7 combat equivalent. Some aberrants exhibit rudimentary group coordination. The Hollow Choir in the eastern Catskills is an aberrant cluster operating with apparent collective cognition.

### Sovereigns

Roughly five percent of the eldritch population, perhaps three hundred total in the region. Entities with evident cognition, strategic behavior, and territorial memory. Most Sovereigns operate in specific territorial niches and maintain lesser eldritch as population networks — wretches and hunters under Sovereign direction cohere into something resembling an ecology.

About thirty Sovereigns are known and tracked by faction intelligence. The named ones include:

**The One Who Waits.** Pine Barrens. A humanoid figure approximately three and a half meters tall, robed in what appears to be bark and lichen. Speaks languages no human has ever spoken. The central entity of the Listening cult. Has never been directly observed to kill anyone, though those who "speak" with it undergo progressive transformation.

**The Echo in Lower Manhattan.** Financial District. Never directly observed. Presence manifests as lost time, confused memories, and occasional physical movement of objects. Anyone spending more than approximately two hours in its territory loses fragmentary memories and acquires confused temporal-spatial impressions. Territory has not expanded in over a year.

**Grandfather Silt.** Chesapeake eastern shore, near the Nanticoke river mouth. A submerged entity estimated at eight to twelve meters. Has sunk at least three salvage vessels. Crabclaw Confederation has declared a no-go zone of approximately four hundred square kilometers around the presumed territory.

**The Hollow Choir.** Eastern Catskill valleys. A cluster of fifteen to twenty aberrant entities operating with coordination that no single entity appears to direct. Produce synchronized sub-audible vocalizations that carry across valleys and cause acute unease in baseline humans.

**The Broker.** Outer Camden. An aberrant Sovereign that occasionally appears at specific salvage sites and attempts to communicate through crude written symbols, gesture, and offered objects. Not believed to be hostile. The offered objects have included pre-Onset mechanical items in various states of repair. What it wants is unknown.

### Shades

Roughly five percent. Incorporeal or semi-incorporeal entities. Manifest as presence without body — cold zones, wrong sounds, shadows that move incorrectly. Cannot be harmed by conventional means. Believed to be vulnerable only to auratic, perceptual, or eldritch capacities.

Most shades are non-aggressive. They haunt specific locations and ignore or mildly disturb visitors. A small minority actively target humans; these are the most feared eldritch relative to their frequency.

### Anomalous

Approximately fifteen percent of observed eldritch entities display behaviors or carry knowledge that cannot be explained by human origin. They reference entities, places, or events not in any human record. They speak languages that have never existed. Their stated purposes, when they articulate anything, refer to cosmologies that do not align with any scientific or religious human framework. Their statements contradict each other across different anomalous entities.

Four competing theories exist among surviving scholars, none privileged: that entities from elsewhere entered during the Onset; that the Onset attached fragments of other minds to human hosts; that the entities have confabulated mythologies their fragmented cognition believes; that the Onset itself was caused by something whose nature is not comprehensible in human terms.

The question is not resolved. It will not be resolved in this campaign.

## The Hive

Mobile, aggregating plant-organisms that have expanded across the mid-Atlantic since the Onset. Individual Hive nodes are plant-bodies of one to five meter scale. Some are rooted, some ambulatory, a minority are rapidly mobile. They operate as distributed colonies of twenty to two thousand nodes, coordinated through mechanisms not fully understood.

The relationship between Hive flora and the humanoid eldritch Clusters — the Batsto Cluster, the Hollow Choir, and similar — is debated. The Single-Entity Hypothesis holds that they are the same distributed organism in different forms. The Parallel-Emergence Hypothesis holds that they are biologically distinct but ecologically cooperating. The Symbiotic Hypothesis holds that the Clusters are the mobile component of an organism whose rooted component is the Hive flora. No hypothesis is confirmed.

The Hive at one year holds approximately twelve thousand square kilometers of territory — three percent of the region — and is expanding at roughly five percent per year in the base case.

### Major Colonies

**The Batsto Sprawl.** Inner Pine Barrens. Fifty thousand tons of biomass across twenty-four hundred square kilometers. Extreme aggression. Coordinates with the Listening cult. The largest Hive colony in the region. Expansion rate five percent per year.

**The Ramapo Vine.** Ramapo Mountains, northern New Jersey. Forty-five thousand nodes, high aggression. Ambushes Catskill Throne patrols; reclaiming abandoned suburban corridors. Preston has formally ceded certain mountain routes.

**The Passaic Thorn-Crown.** Upper Passaic corridor. Thirty thousand nodes, high aggression. Iron Crown treats it as a deliberate buffer zone and does not engage.

**The Jersey Barrens Satellites.** Multi-site across central-southern New Jersey. Fifteen thousand nodes combined. Moderate to high aggression. Central Jersey League townships contain locally; no coordinated response.

**The Chesapeake Mangrove-Weep.** Southern Delmarva tidal. Five thousand nodes, moderate aggression. Semi-aquatic. Closes navigation channels seasonally. Delmarva Lords tolerate as buffer against the Tidewater eldritch cluster.

**The Susquehanna Crown.** Central Pennsylvania floodplains. The newest major colony. Sixty thousand nodes, high aggression, expanding rapidly. Lehigh Principalities maintain active border.

**The Schuylkill Grove.** Philadelphia watershed upper reaches. Three thousand nodes, moderate aggression. The Bourse funds annual burn-crew operations that contain but cannot eradicate it.

**The Wissahickon Green.** Fairmount Park, Philadelphia proper. Minor, contained by Bourse operations. Present inside city limits.

### Node Behavior

Individual nodes are not classifiable as any pre-Onset plant species. Common features: rooted nodes can creep one to three meters per day through soil displacement; a minority of mobile nodes can relocate ten to one hundred meters per day; very few are rapidly ambulatory at human-running speeds over short bursts.

Nodes attack with thorn-tipped tendrils, constricting vines, or chemical sprays — contact-toxic, airborne-irritant, or paralytic. Mobile nodes can pursue prey into human-built structures through windows and doors. Colonies funnel prey toward concentration zones; nodes sacrifice themselves to protect colony cores.

Vulnerabilities: fire (the primary containment tool), salt, biokinetic-reversal work by skilled practitioners. The Bourse contracts three such practitioners for annual Wissahickon reduction; the Central Jersey League has two working with townships.

## Beasts and Monsters

The altered fauna of the region. Animals that manifested at the Onset and now operate at higher capacity than their baseline species. T1 to T5 altered fauna number in the tens of millions; they are the ambient ecology. What follows are the dangerous specimens — T4 and above, representing perhaps seven hundred thousand individuals.

### Notable Beasts

**Pack-alphas.** Bonded wolves, T4. Lead packs of eight to twenty. Coordinate kills on adult humans. Actively hunt livestock at night. Appalachian and Catskill regions.

**Horned boar patriarchs.** T4 to T5. Tusked males up to four hundred kilograms. Kinetic ram-charges break log barricades. Five to eight traveler deaths per year in Pine Barrens perimeter alone.

**River drakes.** T4 to T5. Mature specimens up to four and a half meters. Piscivorous but will drown humans and livestock. Each drake's territory covers approximately eight river-kilometers. Present in Hudson, Delaware, and Chesapeake systems.

**Mourning bears.** T5. Larger than pre-Onset grizzlies. Coordinated stalking in groups of two to three. Associated in rural folklore with infant deaths and missing children; the pattern is real though the mechanism (probably olfactory) is not deliberate predation. Catskills and Pocono interior.

**Dire deer rut-dominants.** T4. Actively territorial in late autumn rut. Will charge through occupied buildings. Approximately twenty human deaths per year.

**Glass-spined hogs.** T4. Feral pig descendants. Vitreous spines fire as projectiles in threat. Pine Barrens perimeter and abandoned suburbs.

**Ghost-cougars.** T4 to T5. Spatial-displacement manifestation; vanish at twenty to thirty meters when threatened or when hunting. Catskills, Poconos, Shenandoah.

**Hollow-eyed crows.** T4. Corvids with auratic dread-fields. Large flocks induce dread states in any humans in range. Suspected of coordination with Warped ravens. Region-wide, approximately forty-five thousand individuals.

**Ironmouth crocodilians.** T5. Pre-Onset alligators migrated northward. Bony facial armor resists smallarms. Chesapeake tributaries; approximately seventy-five individuals.

**Lake Kittatinny Snake-Mass.** T5, possibly T6. An aquatic serpentine entity approximately twelve meters long in a single lake in northern New Jersey. Eight sightings since the Onset. Ate a fisherman last autumn. Possibly sentient.

**Night-walker raccoons.** T4. Twenty-five to forty kilograms. Matter-manipulation opens any latch. Raid settlements in packs of five to fifteen. Urban ruins, region-wide.

**Bronze-shell snappers.** T4. Thirty-to-eighty-kilogram snapping turtles. Matter-hardened carapace. Pull children and livestock under freshwater. Freshwater systems throughout.

**Spike-antler elk.** T4 males. Kinetic-charged antlers break stockade walls during rut. Catskills, Poconos, Appalachians.

**Bog witches.** T4, altered egrets. Auratic fear-field extending thirty to fifty meters. Disorient hunters in wetlands. Pine Barrens and Delmarva wetlands.

### Encounter Rates

A traveler in the region encounters beasts at rates that vary dramatically by region.

Delmarva agricultural land: roughly one encounter per thirty travel-days. Fatal encounter: one per one thousand travel-days.

Central Jersey League township corridors: one encounter per fourteen days. Fatal: one per five hundred.

Catskill patrolled roads: one per ten days. Fatal: one per three hundred.

Catskill wilderness: one per three days. Fatal: one per eighty.

Pine Barrens perimeter: one per two days. Fatal: one per forty.

Pine Barrens interior: one per day. Fatal: one per fifteen.

Manhattan ruins: one per two days. Fatal: one per sixty.

Armed escort by T3+ kinetic guards reduces fatal encounter rates by sixty to eighty percent. This is why armed escorts cost fifteen copper per guard-day.

## The Warped

The Warped are animals that manifested at the Onset and gained progressively higher cognition at higher tiers. At Tier 3 they are noticeably smarter than baseline; at Tier 6 they achieve human-equivalent sentience; at Tier 7 and above they exceed it. They constitute a category of person that did not exist a year ago, and whose nature and rights are actively being worked out across the region.

The defining fact about the Warped is that they claim — with remarkable consistency across species, regions, and isolated contacts — to have existed before humanity, to have been dormant rather than absent, and to remember events predating human civilization. Whether these claims are true is the central open question of Warped studies.

Approximately thirty-seven thousand T6+ Warped are believed to operate in the mid-Atlantic. Higher tiers are progressively rarer — roughly twenty-three thousand T7, sixteen thousand T8, twelve thousand T9, and a contested count at T10 and above. Of these, only about twenty at the T8+ tier have been contacted or named by major factions.

### Cognitive Tiers

**T3.** Problem-solving, individual recognition, cause-effect reasoning over minutes to hours. Equivalent to the best pre-Onset ape or corvid cognition. Can learn twenty to fifty human words receptively.

**T4.** Multi-step planning, theory of mind about individuals, tool-making. Equivalent to a precocious four-year-old human. Can learn and use two hundred words productively.

**T5.** Abstract reasoning, basic literacy, conversation. Equivalent to a seven-year-old. Forms intentional relationships with humans.

**T6.** Full human-equivalent sentience. Can hold philosophical discourse, argue ethics, create art, plan across years. Many T6 Warped are literate in multiple languages.

**T7.** Superhuman pattern recognition, memory, integration. Can process information streams that overwhelm humans. Often seem to know things they should not.

**T8.** Profoundly non-human cognitive patterns. Reasoning may be comprehensible but priorities and attention structures are alien.

**T9 and above.** Cognition operating on scales that resist human mapping. Statements from T9+ Warped often seem true in multiple ways simultaneously.

### Hostility Distribution

Warped at T3+ divide roughly as follows toward humans. Ten to fifteen percent are actively hostile. Thirty-five to forty percent are wary and territorial — they defend their range but do not seek conflict. Thirty to thirty-five percent are neutral, indifferent to human activity. Ten to fifteen percent have formed selective alliances with specific human communities. Three to five percent actively aid human communities on a consistent basis.

The distribution correlates with species and tier. Corvids are disproportionately friendly. Large predators (wolves, bears, large cats) are disproportionately hostile. Cephalopods are highly aligned among themselves and alien to humans. Rodents are disproportionately hostile, particularly mound-builder rat Warped whose interests directly conflict with human settlement. Raptors vary widely.

### Named T8+ Warped

**Old Stone.** T9, red-tailed hawk origin. Hudson Highlands, western bank. Claims to have been born before the last glaciation. References specific dates in a "Third Cycle" approximately forty thousand years ago. Not hostile to humans unprovoked. Negotiated with Preston once; outcome undisclosed.

**The Five Sisters.** T8 each, gray wolves. A coordinated council in the Poconos. Unified memory stretching back "many thousands of moons." Their theology names a being called the First Mother from whom all canid sentience descends. Hostile to Catskill Throne expansion; have killed three of Preston's patrols. Will parley with those who approach alone and unarmed; their safety guarantees are reliable within their territory.

**Grey Walker.** T9, American black bear. Southern Catskills; wanders. Says he remembers a prior human civilization that "fell into the ice" before human history. Has attempted to teach pre-Onset-unknown architectural features and unnamed stars to human visitors. Non-hostile. Widely regarded by Warped-informed humans as the closest thing to a trustworthy informant.

**The Parliament of Crows.** Approximately forty T6-T7 plus three T8 ravens. Philadelphia metro, with satellite roosts. Informally allied with Philadelphia Bourse; trade information for carrion rights at slaughterhouses. The three T8 elders negotiate in clear English but will not write.

**The Drowned Mother.** T10, white-tailed deer. Eastern Catskills. A single immense doe, unnaturally large. Claims to have been "first green thing that remembered its own green-ness." Pacifistic but terrifying. Twelve sightings since the Onset; each produces spontaneous biokinetic enhancement in the surrounding forest lasting weeks.

**The Salt Judge.** T9, striped bass. Chesapeake Bay, primarily northern. Claims dominion over the bay's living things. References a "great poisoning" consistent with mid-twentieth-century pollution. Communicates through intermediary Warped, usually osprey or eagle. Has forbidden netting in certain bay regions; fleets that violate are found sunk within days.

**The Camden Alley-King.** T7, Norway rat. Camden ruins. Claims memory of pre-Onset shipping operations in specific detail. Openly hostile to human habitation. Commands enormous mound-builder rat populations. Has destroyed five Bourse salvage operations in Camden.

**The White Horse of Princeton.** T8, feral horse. Princeton region. Claims to remember "ten thousand generations of bit-and-bridle." Leads a herd of approximately sixty normal horses plus three T6 warped horses. Has entered formal treaty with Central Jersey League.

**The Lost Thing.** T10, unknown origin. Pine Barrens; moves unpredictably. Appearance shifts — wolfish, bear-like, long-limbed, many-eyed. Its own claims about itself are internally inconsistent. Some scholars believe it an eldritch-Warped hybrid; some believe it the referent of pre-Onset "Jersey Devil" folklore. Catskill Throne formally classes it as eldritch-adjacent.

**The Old Badger.** T8, American badger. Lehigh Valley interior. Coherent theology centered on subterranean knowledge and "the dark that is not empty." Lehigh Principalities maintain a formal arrangement: one slaughter-ready pig monthly in exchange for his acceptance of agricultural boundaries. Has honored the agreement eleven months running.

### What the Warped Want

No single answer. Observed patterns: territorial security most commonly; information exchange with human scholars; food provisions (the Old Badger model); preservation of their original species from human hunting; theological propagation (Grey Walker's method); strategic neutrality; in a minority of cases, active hostility. The Camden Alley-King and the Lost Thing represent the hostile minority at high tier.

No Warped polity exists. Despite T6+ cognition, the Warped have not formed governments of their own. The Princeton Corvid Council is the closest analogue; it functions as a council of elders rather than a governing body. Whether this will change as the post-Onset generation of Warped matures is unknown.

### Theological Claims

Beyond origin claims, T7+ Warped articulate coherent theological frameworks that recur across individuals with suggestive consistency. The world has been multiple things; the current form is one of several. Humans are a recent phenomenon, a brief loud event in a longer quiet. Something is returning; the Onset is part of a larger transition. The eldritch Sovereigns are older than the Warped themselves. Manifestation is not new; prior epochs of manifestation are remembered in animal-lineage tradition.

The consistency of these claims across isolated contacts is suggestive. The specificity of certain geological and ecological claims — the Salt Judge's pollution timeline, Grey Walker's glaciation memory, the Drowned Mother's forest descriptions — is harder to explain as confabulation. But the claims also diverge in specifics in ways that would not occur if they were drawn from a shared accurate memory. The ambiguity is deliberate in the fiction. Preserve it in play.

# CHAPTER 8: LIFE IN THE LORDLY EQUILIBRIUM

This chapter is texture. What people eat, what they earn, how they marry, what they believe, what they fear at night. A game-master uses this material to ground scenes that would otherwise be abstract — to turn "you arrive at the town" into a town you can describe. The numbers are not meant to be rolled against; they are meant to be consulted, so that when a player asks how much a horse costs or how often a peasant eats meat, an answer exists.

## The Shape of the Population

Sixteen million people live in the mid-Atlantic at game start. The demography is not the pre-Onset demography. The first-year collapse killed elders and infants disproportionately; combatant-age males died more than females; the surviving population is a concentrated workforce with unusual age and sex structure.

The overall sex ratio stands at seventy-nine men per hundred women. In the reproductive-age cohort — roughly twenty to forty-five — the ratio drops to seventy per hundred. This imbalance shapes marriage patterns and property transmission across the region.

The age structure is inverted. Elders — people over sixty — are about four percent of the population, down from eighteen percent before the Onset. Children under ten are about five percent. The working-age middle — twenty to sixty — makes up nearly eighty percent of the population, a concentration that is structurally productive and demographically fragile. The generation is a bottleneck. When it ages, if no new births fill the gap, the region will have a different problem.

Births are picking up. The fertility rate, which collapsed to below one during the first year, has risen to approximately two. It will rise further as the situation stabilizes. But infant mortality remains catastrophic — approximately fifteen percent of live births die in the first year without biokinetic intervention, four to five percent with it. Families that can afford biokinetic care for pregnancy and infancy do. Most cannot.

## Class

The post-Onset class structure combines pre-Onset occupational categories with manifestation tier, species membership, and faction legal status. It is not clean. What follows is a working description.

**The ruling class** — regional sovereigns, great lords, minor lords — is roughly three-tenths of one percent of the population. Perhaps twenty individuals hold regional-sovereign authority; a hundred to two hundred hold great-lord status; a larger but still small group holds minor lordships of town or estate scale.

**The administrative and skilled class** — retinue officers, artisan-masters, merchant-principals, biokinetic healers, clergy, bourse clerks, skilled artisans in rare trades — is about ten percent of the population. This is the class that maintains the functioning parts of the post-Onset world. Biokinetic healers are a distinct sub-class within this band, economically unique, often earning twenty to thirty times what a skilled artisan earns.

**The productive working class** — retinue soldiers, freeholder farmers, tenant farmers — is about thirty-five percent. Most of the settled countryside belongs here. Retinue soldiers are on retainer with warlord or federation militias. Freeholders own their land; tenants work land owned by lords or by the Bourse-adjacent free-association communes.

**The precarious class** — urban laborers, refugees, indentured and debt-bonded persons — is about twenty-four percent. These are the people whose lives have not yet stabilized, who work day-labor or whose debts have bound them to a specific employer. The Iron Crown's territory holds a disproportionate share.

**The excluded** — chattel slaves in the warlord territories that permit the practice, and the visibly corrupted eldritch-adjacent — make up less than two percent. Slavery is legal in the Chain-King's outer Brooklyn territory and in parts of the Tower Lords' domains. It is abolished in the Philadelphia Bourse, Central Jersey League, Fed Continuity, Yonkers Compact, and most Delmarva demesnes.

**The metahuman species**, nineteen percent of the regional population, operate in a parallel legal category that cuts across the class hierarchy. Their legal treatment varies by faction — equal under law in most polities, with practical discrimination in some; second-class or excluded in others. A Species C master artisan and a Species I kin-elder are not classed with tenant farmers regardless of what their class would otherwise be.

## What Things Cost

What follows is a reference, not a price list to be rolled against. Prices fluctuate by region, season, and faction; what is listed is the rough Philadelphia Bourse reference price in Bourse copper at game start.

**Food.** A kilogram loaf of bread: one to two copper. A bushel of wheat: eight. A pound of beef on the hoof: eighteen. A dozen eggs: three. A gallon of milk: two to three. A pound of good cheese: twelve. A pound of Chesapeake fish: six. Salt is expensive — four copper per pound — because most salt is now mined rather than refined. Sugar and honey are luxuries: twenty copper a pound.

**Services.** A day of unskilled labor: two copper. A day of skilled tradesmen's work: eight. A minor healing from a biokinetic: fifteen. A major healing: two hundred and fifty. A life-saving intervention: twenty-five hundred. An annual retainer from a senior biokinetic to a noble household: ten thousand. A Bourse-certified courier, hundred-kilometer route with guaranteed delivery: fifty. A Species H discreet courier for the same route: one hundred and twenty. Bourse arbitration fee for commercial dispute: two hundred.

**Goods.** A new pair of leather boots: forty. A winter wool coat: eighty. A working horse: eight hundred. A war-trained horse: twenty-five hundred. An ordinary matter-crafted knife: twenty-five. A Species C master-made knife: three hundred. A functional pre-Onset watch: five hundred. A functional pre-Onset radio, very rare: four thousand. A fifty-kilogram bag of high-grade Lehigh coal: thirty-five. A pound of raw unworked copper: eighty. A pound of salvaged forged iron: six. A single course of pre-Onset antibiotics, when they can be found: eight hundred.

**Housing.** A month's rent for a single room in Philadelphia: thirty copper. A month's rent for a family house in the Philadelphia suburbs: one hundred. A workshop purchase in Flushing-Edison Cluster territory: eight thousand. A small farm purchase, ten acres in Delmarva: fifteen thousand. A fortified manor suitable for a minor lordship: one hundred and twenty thousand.

**Transport.** A Chesapeake boat passage one-way to Philadelphia: twenty-five. A wagon charter for a day: forty. The Catskill Throne toll through the passes: twenty per person. An armed guard's day of escort service: fifteen.

## What People Do

**The laborer.** A baseline unskilled laborer earns sixty copper a month. If she has a T1 or T2 biokinetic manifestation suited to agricultural work, she earns one hundred and ten. If she is T3 or above and has found a demesne that values her, three hundred or more. The same pattern runs through every occupation — manifestation of the right kind multiplies earning power substantially, and this is why everyone in the region pays attention to manifestation tiers in the way they would have paid attention to credentials before the Onset.

**The artisan.** A baseline skilled artisan earns one hundred and eighty a month. A T3 to T5 matter-manipulator of genuine skill: five hundred. A Species C master working at the level that produced Master Iwo Kuan: nine hundred or more.

**The physician.** A pre-Onset-trained physician without manifestation: four hundred a month. A T3 biokinetic healer: eight hundred. A T5: twenty-five hundred. A T7 at Okafor's level: twelve thousand a month, and she could command more; the Central Jersey League pays her less than her market rate because she stays out of principle.

**The soldier.** A warlord retinue soldier in ordinary service: one hundred and fifty copper a month. A trusted captain at T6: two thousand. A Brick Stepanski — T7, senior captain, district commander — six thousand, plus his share of district revenue.

**The merchant.** A Bourse clerk: two hundred and fifty a month, plus rations and dormitory. A merchant principal running a middle-sized trading house: thirty-five hundred to five thousand, with equity in house profits. A House Okonkwo senior like Mariam: undisclosed, but the house is capitalized at probably two hundred thousand copper and she is majority owner.

**The courier.** A baseline courier running regional routes: one hundred and eighty a month. A Species H network courier, with the network discount: four hundred. A Temporal/Spatial teleportation specialist on exclusive contract: eight hundred or more, plus bonuses per route.

**The clergy.** A priest with a large congregation: three hundred a month, usually plus housing. A rabbi, similar. An imam, similar. A monk at Brother Tam's monastery: no wage; the monastery provides room, board, and training in exchange for labor.

**The scholar.** A scribe: two hundred and eighty. A Bourse library archivist: three hundred and fifty. A Rutgers-employed ethnographer like Professor Afolabi: four hundred and fifty, plus research budget.

## What People Eat

Diet varies dramatically by region and class.

**Delmarva** has abundance. Farm families eat meat three or four times a week, eggs daily, fresh dairy, seasonal vegetables, preserved foods through winter. A lord's household eats meat daily. The region exports its surplus.

**The Central Jersey League** is similar, with less dairy and more legumes. Meat twice a week is normal. Regional biokinetic root vegetables — post-Onset varieties developed at Rutgers — supplement grain.

**Philadelphia** imports food. The urban working class eats bread, preserved salt-cured pork and dried fish, occasional fresh vegetables in season. Meat once a week is typical. The Bourse merchants' tables are better — fresh everything, wine, cheese. The contrast between the Bourse dining halls and the dockside bread-lines is visible within a single block.

**The Tower Lords' territory in Brooklyn** relies on hunted altered fauna, foraged and rooftop-grown vegetables, and shrinking stockpiles of pre-Onset canned goods. Mound-builder rat, despite appearances, is edible and regularly eaten. Chronic protein deficiency is common for non-retinue populations. The Tower Lords themselves eat Delmarva imports; their captains eat better than their soldiers.

**The Catskill Throne** hunts. Deer, elk, boar. Distribution is ritualized — Preston hunts personally several times a year, and the kills are distributed to retinue and tributary towns in a formal ceremony. Mountain agriculture provides grain and potatoes. Trout from streams supplement.

**Fed Continuity** distributes military rations. Surviving pre-Onset MREs still appear occasionally as officer meals. Soldiers eat coarse grain from Delmarva, preserved meat, and seasonal supplements. Hunting in Rock Creek Park and the Potomac provides modest variety.

**Species I** maintains Central American foodways where possible. Corn, beans, chiles, pork preservation. Community feasts for kin-network gatherings are significant cultural events. The Flores-kin's feast hall in Prince George's seats four hundred.

**Species C** in the Flushing-Edison Cluster has the most varied diet in the city — surviving East Asian foodways, fish from the harbor, vegetables from rooftop and courtyard gardens. Tea is still available, imported through convoluted trade routes at considerable expense.

The morning meal across the region is now called "first-light." The evening meal is "late-food." These terms began in Federal Continuity refugee camps and have spread through couriers. Pre-Onset vocabulary survives but is increasingly archaic.

## Diseases and Medicine

Pre-Onset pharmaceuticals are running out. Antibiotic stockpiles are eighteen months old and increasingly unreliable. Insulin is gone. Most chronic-disease medications are gone. The pre-Onset generation of chronic patients — asthmatics, diabetics, cardiac patients — died in the first year unless they had biokinetic intervention.

What remains: biokinetic healers, herbal medicine, and the accumulating knowledge of the region's surviving physicians. The Yonkers Three Judges have made their healing hall the region's most respected medical center. Fed Continuity's military medical corps is the region's largest. Rutgers' biology department, now reorganized as a biokinetic training school, is producing the next generation of practitioners.

Endemic diseases at game start: cholera and typhoid in the urban cores; tuberculosis is spreading and rising; measles has returned; pertussis is taking infants; dysentery is ubiquitous; influenza is annual; rabies is increasing in the rural zones; hepatitis from unreliable water is widespread. Smallpox has not yet appeared in the region — a latent risk that everyone with institutional medical memory thinks about.

Power-burnout is a recognized medical condition, treatable with rest and in severe cases with biokinetic support. Eldritch wasting — the progressive decay afflicting those who have had direct eldritch contact — has nearly one hundred percent untreated mortality; even biokinetic treatment has mixed results.

Life expectancy varies dramatically by class. A regional sovereign can expect to live to sixty-two; a great lord to fifty-five. A Bourse merchant-principal to fifty-eight. A biokinetic healer who treats herself, sixty. A skilled artisan, forty-eight. A Delmarva freeholder farmer, fifty. An urban laborer, thirty-eight. A tenant farmer, forty-two. A refugee, thirty. A Manhattan salvager, thirty-two. A warlord retinue soldier, thirty-five. The regional average is approximately forty-two. The pre-Onset American average was seventy-eight.

## Literacy and Knowledge

Regional literacy stands at twenty-nine percent and is falling. The pre-Onset generation mostly retains literacy; the post-Onset children of tenant farmers and urban laborers are increasingly not being taught.

Schools operate in the Philadelphia Bourse (for merchant families and apprentices), in the Central Jersey League townships (community schools, free to residents), in Federal Continuity territory (government schools in Districts), in Rutgers and Princeton (scholarly institutions), in Species I Flores-kin territory (community schools, in Spanish and English), and in Brother Tam's monastery. Other communities rely on informal transmission — parent to child, priest to congregation — which is variable in quality.

The knowledge decaying fastest: modern medicine (the pre-Onset doctors are aging without successors), engineering in all its forms, higher mathematics, physics and chemistry as coherent disciplines, and digital technology entirely. The knowledge being preserved: biokinetic agricultural techniques, traditional and revived herbal medicine, literacy itself, smithing and metalwork, military training, commercial and legal practice.

The Princeton University Library is the largest surviving collection. Georgetown's partial library is second. The Philadelphia Bourse Library is the largest *growing* collection — the Bourse actively acquires books, accepts donations from estates, and has begun to commission new writing on post-Onset subjects. The Rutgers agricultural archive is the most specialized. The Catskill Throne's personal library holds five thousand volumes and is not open to outsiders.

Madame Pilar Reyes, a Bourse-adjacent archivist, maintains a specialized collection of tier-breakthrough accounts and power phenomenology. Her work is the region's closest approximation to post-Onset science.

## Religion

Pre-Onset religions survive in varying states.

**Catholicism** has lost its institutional hierarchy. Philadelphia's Archbishop died in the second year; no successor has been consecrated; local parishes operate independently. Father Marcus Rhodes of Queens is the highest-profile Catholic leader in the New York metropolitan area. Delmarva Catholic communities are stable. Brother Tam's monastery has emerged as a semi-autonomous institution, doing theological work alongside its agricultural and orphanage missions.

**Protestant denominations** are highly fragmented. Small congregations in every town; no regional organization. Strongest among surviving suburban populations.

**Judaism** survives strongly in specific communities. Rabbi David Klein in Federal Continuity civilian affairs is the highest-profile figure. Several communities in northeastern New Jersey maintain continuity. The Philadelphia Jewish community runs one of the region's functioning schools.

**Islam** survives in scattered communities. Species H in particular is substantially Muslim; several imams operate in the North Jersey enclaves.

**Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism** are present in small immigrant-descended communities. Species A Japanese and Korean populations maintain Buddhist practices.

**Santería, Vodou, and various African-diaspora traditions** have seen resurgence, particularly among Species I and Species J populations. Their pre-Onset adaptability to diaspora conditions makes them well-suited to the post-Onset world.

**New movements** have emerged. The Listening cult is the most visible. The Onsetist movement — a philosophical-religious position holding the Onset was a transformative event with theological meaning — has perhaps fifty to a hundred thousand adherents across the region, informally organized, crossing existing religious lines. The Quiet — an anti-manifestation movement holding that manifestation was a mistake or a curse — has perhaps twenty to thirty thousand adherents, mostly in Federal Continuity-adjacent territory. The Seven-Day Saints, a millenarian movement with possible eldritch influence, is smaller and harder to classify.

**Species-specific spirituality** is emerging. Species I Flores-kin have developed practices centered on ancestor veneration they call Ojos de Abuela, Grandmother's Eyes. Species C communities maintain Shinto-derived practices. Species G has revived pagan traditions — Druidic, Heathen — that their pre-Onset ancestors had mostly abandoned. Species H communities maintain South American Catholic traditions with species-specific modifications.

Religious leaders have higher-than-average survival and influence. Many clergy were auratic-secondary users whose presence aided community cohesion. Literacy among clergy is extremely high. Clergy are widely trusted across faction lines, and the region's most important informal diplomatic channels often run through religious figures.

## Law

Each major faction maintains its own legal practice. The short version of each:

**Federal Continuity** operates the only public civil courts in the region. The US Constitution and Federal Code apply in theory; military courts handle most matters in practice. Pre-Onset property titles are honored where documents survive. Punishment ranges from fines in scrip to exile to execution for capital crimes. The Continuity courts accept non-citizen cases if both parties consent, and Bourse merchants often route disputes through them when a formal ruling is worth more than speed.

**The Philadelphia Bourse** claims only commercial jurisdiction — contracts, trade disputes, debt. It does not claim criminal jurisdiction. Arbitration by the Factor-General or designated House judges is binding upon signatories. Fees are two hundred copper. Enforcement is through loss of Bourse recognition — commercial death for any merchant who defies a Bourse ruling.

**The Central Jersey League** handles most matters at the township level. Inter-township disputes go to the Accord Council. Restorative rather than punitive emphasis; exile is the severest routine sanction; capital punishment requires Council vote. The most deliberative legal system in the region.

**The Catskill Throne** is personal rule. Preston is the law; Marshal Whelan handles routine matters; Beatrice Amar's truth-field hall is used for cases requiring truth-determination. Efficient but arbitrary.

**The Iron Crown** is personal rule without institutions. Volk's word is law; captains handle district disputes; appeals to Volk are rarely granted. No concept of contract law in the Bourse sense.

**The Yonkers Compact** is council-based, restorative, with exile as severest routine sanction. The most equitable system in the region.

**Species I kin-networks** handle internal disputes through kin-council governance, with cross-network disputes mediated by senior elders. Expulsion from the kin-network is the severest sanction.

**Species C Twelve Hands** govern commercial matters; workshops handle internal matters. Trade disputes settle through mediation.

**Species H** has no formal law. Disputes resolve by rough consensus; sanction is loss of network access.

Cross-faction contract enforcement relies on Bourse-backed agreements (the regional gold standard), hostages (family members holding residence in counterparty territory), material collateral held by the Bourse in custody, and mutual deterrence. Eldritch binding — pact-based contracts offered by eldritch specialists — is effective but socially suspect and rare.

## Families and Children

Post-Onset family structure has returned toward multi-generational households. A typical settled household includes a married couple (first marriages typically at ages seventeen to twenty-three), two to five surviving children, one or more grandparents if any survived, occasionally a widowed sibling of spouse, occasionally orphaned nieces or nephews, sometimes an apprentice. Household size of six to twelve is normal.

Polygamous households exist in warlord territories, Species I kin-network leadership, and some Yonkers Compact informal arrangements. They are not the norm but are not rare.

Arranged marriages are common among warlord families (dynastic strategy), Species I kin-networks (traditional practice), and Bourse merchant families (commercial alliances). Among freeholders, townspeople, and refugees, free marriage dominates.

Children manifest at puberty. The event is the central moment of adolescence in the post-Onset world. Communities prepare. Pre-manifestation assessment by skilled biokinetics — accurate about sixty-five percent of the time — lets wealthy families prepare for likely domains. The manifestation celebration is a significant social event; among Species I it rises nearly to religious ritual. Children who fail to manifest — approximately one to two percent of post-Onset-born — have socially difficult positions, handled differently in different communities.

Roughly thirty to forty percent of children under ten have lost one or both parents. Orphan absorption works through kin (most common), institutions (Federal Continuity, Yonkers, Lehigh orphanages), religious communities (Father Rhodes's Queens community, Brother Tam's monastery), species networks, and occasionally warlord adoption as future retinue.

Elder care has mostly collapsed. Most pre-Onset retirees died in the first year. Surviving elders are cared for in multi-generational households, intentional elder-care communities (Yonkers, Delmarva, Central Jersey has one each), religious houses, and occasionally warlord courts for valued advisors.

## Language and Culture

English remains dominant. New vocabulary has emerged: *first-light* for morning meal, *late-food* for evening meal, *the Before* for the pre-Onset era, *the Shaking* as an alternative to the Onset, *manifest* as a verb meaning to first display powers, *the Line* for any border between factions, *charged* for corruption-zone-altered territory, *to tier* meaning to breakthrough ("he tiered last spring"), *to burn out* for power-overuse collapse, *chit* versus *copper* distinguishing Iron Crown chits from Bourse coins, with "chit" becoming general slang for untrustworthy money.

Species-specific language survives. Species B speaks with an infrasonic component baseline humans cannot fully hear. Species I has developed hand-signal communication for distances. Species C maintains some original-language usage for craft terminology. Species H is bilingual; couriers often work in both Spanish or Portuguese and English. Species G has revived heritage loanwords — Old English, German, Welsh — particularly in agricultural communities.

Music is central. Without electronic media, live music is how people experience music. Most communities have at least one musician. Philadelphia, Washington, and the Central Jersey League townships have active music scenes. Songs about the Before, about the Onset, about specific faction events have emerged as genres.

Storytelling has reemerged as a major art form. Regular storytellers operate at warlord courts, in Bourse reading halls, in Species kin-network gatherings, in town squares. The best are paid well — a Catskill Throne-court storyteller can earn two hundred copper for an evening's work.

Theater has reemerged. Princeton hosts a troupe. Philadelphia has two small theaters. The Yonkers Compact has begun community theater. Pre-Onset plays are popular; new writing is rare but valued.

The calendar uses "After Onset" — A.O. Year One began at the summer solstice following the Onset. Game start is early summer of Year Two A.O. Most communities retain pre-Onset month names, though the Yonkers Compact has renamed them after agricultural seasons.

Time of day is tracked by solar markers — first-light, mid-morning, noon, mid-afternoon, dusk, evening. Faction capitals operate mechanical clocks and publish schedules for their citizens.

# CHAPTER 9: RUNNING THE GAME

This chapter contains the procedural material: the oracles you consult when the fiction needs an answer you did not prepare, the encounter tables you roll against when characters travel, the macro-clocks that track what the world does while the players are not watching, and the campaign frames that structure extended play.

The assumption is that the game system you are using handles basic resolution — rolls, stats, initiative, whatever your table uses. What this chapter provides is world-specific procedural content: the kind of material that cannot be borrowed from a generic setting.

## Oracles

When the fiction needs a specific answer — what is the NPC's reaction, what is the nature of the rumor, what complication arises in this scene — and you do not have it prepared, consult an oracle. Roll 1d100 unless otherwise specified.

### Faction Reaction

When player characters approach a faction official, merchant, or retinue member in a formal or semi-formal context, roll 1d100. Modify by the faction's disposition toward the character's affiliations: +20 if the characters represent an allied faction, -20 if hostile, 0 if neutral.

- **01-10** Hostile. The NPC will not assist and actively obstructs. If a formal envoy, a diplomatic incident is in progress.
- **11-25** Cold. The NPC performs their role minimally. Every question is answered with the least information required.
- **26-45** Professional. The NPC handles the interaction correctly. No warmth, no obstruction.
- **46-65** Correct with interest. The NPC is engaged. Useful information emerges if asked.
- **66-80** Warm. The NPC goes beyond their duty. Offers unasked-for context or introductions.
- **81-95** Personally interested. The NPC wants something from this interaction beyond the formal business. May hint at personal circumstances.
- **96-100** Conspiratorial. The NPC offers an opportunity or asks for help outside the formal business, possibly off the record.

### Warped Disposition

When the characters encounter a Warped individual of T6 or above whose disposition has not been pre-established, roll 1d100.

- **01-10** Actively hostile. Will attack or attempt to remove the characters from its territory.
- **11-25** Territorial. Will demand characters leave; will enforce with violence only if ignored.
- **26-55** Wary neutral. Watches the characters. Will not engage unless provoked or approached.
- **56-75** Curious. Will initiate contact cautiously if the characters are calm and visibly open to communication.
- **76-90** Allied-adjacent. Has a relationship with a human community the characters might have leverage with; will parley if introduced correctly.
- **91-100** Actively helpful. Has reason to want to aid these specific characters, whether they know why or not.

### Eldritch Presence

When the characters enter an area with potential eldritch presence — the Pine Barrens, Lower Manhattan, the Chesapeake southern shore, deep Catskill wilderness — roll 1d100 on entry and at each day or significant location change.

- **01-40** No eldritch encounter. The territory is ambient only.
- **41-60** Indirect signs. Lost time, wrong sounds, tracks that do not match any known species. No entity encountered.
- **61-75** Wretch pack. One to six low-function entities, possibly hunting.
- **76-85** Hunter pair or trio. Coordinated predators, T3 to T4 equivalent, hunting.
- **86-92** Aberrant. A single entity of notable strangeness, T4 to T6 equivalent.
- **93-97** Sovereign presence. A named or unnamed Sovereign becomes aware of the characters. What follows depends on the Sovereign.
- **98-100** Anomalous contact. Something that cannot be classified makes itself known. Treat as narrative event rather than combat.

### Rumor

When characters seek rumors in a tavern, market, or public gathering, roll 1d100.

- **01-20** True and actionable. A specific piece of correct information the characters can verify and use.
- **21-40** True but incomplete. Correct in substance, missing context that would change how it is interpreted.
- **41-60** Partly true, partly false. Requires sifting; the false part is plausible enough to mislead.
- **61-75** False but widely believed. Consistent with what locals think they know; wrong.
- **76-85** Deliberately planted. Someone wants this story to spread. Whose interest it serves is the interesting question.
- **86-95** Fragmentary. A detail from something larger, not enough to act on without more.
- **96-100** Anomalous. The rumor concerns something that should not be possible, and treating it as normal information is dangerous.

### NPC Quick Manifestation

When you need an NPC manifestation profile you did not prepare, roll 1d100 for dominant domain, then 1d100 for tier.

**Dominant domain:**
- **01-28** Physical/Kinetic
- **29-42** Matter/Energy
- **43-57** Biological/Vital
- **58-72** Perceptual/Mental
- **73-85** Temporal/Spatial
- **86-95** Auratic/Presence
- **96-100** Eldritch/Unnatural

**Tier:**
- **01-40** T1
- **41-65** T2
- **66-82** T3
- **83-92** T4
- **93-97** T5
- **98-99** T6
- **100** T7 or higher — make a narrative decision

For profile complexity, roll 1d100: **01-45** single domain only; **46-73** secondary in another domain at one tier lower; **74-91** secondary of same domain; **92-100** three domains, with secondary and tertiary at one and two tiers lower.

### Scene Complication

When a scene needs a complication that was not planned, roll 1d100.

- **01-15** A third party arrives with their own agenda.
- **16-25** A mechanical or infrastructure failure (broken wheel, failing horse, collapsing structure) forces a delay.
- **26-35** Weather turns hostile — storm, heat, fog.
- **36-45** Someone in the scene is not who they claimed to be.
- **46-55** A faction's interest is revealed to run through this scene in a way no one knew.
- **56-65** The characters' past actions return — an NPC they wronged, a debt, a promise.
- **66-75** An altered fauna or eldritch entity disrupts the scene's priorities.
- **76-85** The scene's target or prize is not what was expected.
- **86-95** A character's manifestation or corruption becomes relevant at an awkward moment.
- **96-100** Something the players did not know about becomes suddenly, irreversibly true.

## Macro-Clocks

Ten clocks measure the region's evolving tensions. Each clock has a fill (where it is now), a max (where it fires), and an effect at firing. Advance clocks between sessions as the fiction warrants; a standard rule of thumb is one segment per major session or per in-fiction month of passing time, with fictional events accelerating or retarding particular clocks.

### Bourse Currency Stability (5/6)

The Bourse copper's adoption across the region is nearing formal regional standard. Each segment fills when a major polity formally adopts Bourse copper as its unit of account, or when a significant commercial counterparty capitulates to Bourse currency.

**At firing:** The Bourse copper becomes the formal regional standard. Federal Continuity scrip devalues sharply — holders of scrip lose half their savings. The Bourse's political leverage increases substantially; it can now pressure polities through currency access.

### Biokinetic Agricultural Saturation (5/8)

Biokinetic agricultural techniques diffuse through the practitioner network. Each segment fills when a new major region or township adopts saturation-yield methods.

**At firing:** Regional food surplus becomes reliable. Famine risk ends permanently. Food prices fall by roughly half over six months. Urban populations grow. Warlord states that depend on food scarcity to control their populations lose leverage.

### Federal Continuity Erosion (4/8)

The political erosion of Alcott's authority. Each segment fills when Reeve acts more independently, when a District stops remitting revenue, when a public ceremony is disrupted, when a major faction stops recognizing Continuity authority.

**At firing:** The Command fractures. Reeve's Potomac Irregulars declare independence (or he simply stops pretending). Virginia, Maryland outer, and possibly the Pentagon itself split into competing factions. Continuity scrip collapses. The largest organized polity in the region becomes two or three smaller ones.

### Iron Crown vs Staten (3/6)

Open war over Manhattan salvage. Each segment fills with tangible escalations — seized cargo, killed personnel, blocked passage, failed mediation.

**At firing:** Open war. Iron Crown attempts to force the harbor. Staten responds with spatial strike capacity. Civilian casualties in Newark and across the harbor. Manhattan salvage disrupted for the duration. Possible succession event on either side.

### Pine Barrens Expansion (3/12)

The Listening's territorial push beyond its Pine Barrens core. Each segment fills with each formal territorial claim, each successful expansion of cult membership into a new township, each Hive incursion into previously-cleared land.

**At firing:** The Listening breaches the Delaware line. Corruption zones appear in populated New Jersey. Central Jersey League must respond militarily or lose townships. Regional response coalition forms or fails to form.

### Crabclaw-Fed War (3/6)

Open war along the Chesapeake line. Each segment fills as border incidents escalate.

**At firing:** Open war. Federal Continuity attempts to expand north; Crabclaw defends. Baltimore-Washington corridor becomes a combat zone. The Bourse suspends trade through the corridor. The Potomac Irregulars are heavily engaged, which may precipitate the Erosion clock.

### Catskill Throne Tribute (2/6)

Formalization of Hudson Valley polities as Throne vassals rather than tributary states. Each segment fills as polities formally subordinate their legal authority.

**At firing:** Hudson polities become formal vassals rather than tributaries. Bear-House, Yonkers Compact, and smaller polities lose independent legal authority. The character of the Hudson region shifts from commune-federation to lord-vassal hierarchy.

### Species I Consolidation (2/8)

The Species I diaspora's move toward formal polity declaration. Each segment fills when an assembly takes formal consolidation steps, when a kin-network declares autonomy, when a confrontation with another faction forces diaspora unity.

**At firing:** Species I declares formal polity. Flores-kin or Reyes-kin (depending on which faction won the internal debate) becomes the new polity's capital. Regional politics resets — a new major power is named. Other factions must decide whether to recognize, ally with, or oppose.

### Manhattan Eldritch Breach (2/8)

The Echo or related anomalous entities push beyond their current Financial District confinement. Each segment fills when anomalous events occur outside the Financial District, when salvage teams lose members to anomaly, when Echo-zone phenomena spread.

**At firing:** Anomalous entities push into salvage zones. Manhattan becomes uninhabitable beyond a shrinking northern zone. Tower Lords lose territory; Iron Crown salvage operations collapse. The Philadelphia Bourse's Bureau of Manhattan Interests moves to contain.

### Harvest Lords Unification (1/8)

Delmarva's move toward unified polity. Each segment fills when a major external threat forces coordination, when a Lord's internal succession creates a unification opportunity, when Bourse pressure aligns interests.

**At firing:** Delmarva consolidates into a unified polity. A new great power emerges — agriculturally dominant, commercially coordinated, potentially exceeding any other regional faction in material capacity. The regional balance shifts in ways that take years to reveal.

## Campaign Frames

Three frames for extended play, each drawing on the setting's major tensions. A game-master can run one as presented or combine elements.

### Frame One: The Bourse Agents

The characters are agents of the Philadelphia Bourse. Not merchants — operatives. The Bourse's interests extend across the region, and the Council of Thirty-One maintains a small group of skilled, trusted individuals for work that cannot be done through ordinary commercial channels. Investigations, recoveries, mediations, occasional direct action.

**Opening situation.** House Okonkwo has lost a caravan on the route from Delmarva to Philadelphia. Three wagons, eight hundred copper of goods, twelve personnel including a House factor. The last confirmed sighting was at Smyrna. The characters are dispatched to recover the caravan if possible and to determine what happened.

**Core tensions.** The Bourse is a merchant faction in a region of warlords. It cannot match Iron Crown or Catskill Throne in direct violence, and its political position depends on maintaining commercial neutrality while covertly backing outcomes that serve its long-term interests. The characters operate in this space — able to act where the Bourse cannot act openly.

**Expected macro-clocks.** Currency Stability (likely to fire in the first year of play, reshaping what the Bourse is and what it can do). Pine Barrens Expansion (threatens the region's stability). Species I Consolidation (the Bourse has investment in the outcome).

**Likely endgame triggers.** Currency Stability firing combined with Federal Continuity Erosion would give the Bourse unprecedented regional leverage. Characters navigating this transition — which counterparties to favor, which to isolate, which factions to strengthen for the post-Continuity world — are the campaign's central question.

### Frame Two: The Throne's Shadow

The characters are members of the Catskill Throne's intelligence corps, working under or adjacent to the individual called the Shadow. Their work takes them across the mid-Atlantic — any threat to Preston, any opportunity to strengthen the Throne, any tributary polity that needs observation.

**Opening situation.** The Hollow Choir has ventured beyond its eastern Catskill range. Three tributary villages report vocalizations heard in their valleys. If the pattern continues, Preston will need to decide whether to engage — a decision that would be costly regardless of outcome. The characters are dispatched to determine what is happening and, if possible, to resolve it without requiring Preston's direct involvement.

**Core tensions.** The Throne is one man. His reputation does work his reputation alone cannot sustain. The Shadow's corps exists to maintain the appearance that Preston is everywhere; characters serve this appearance by resolving problems that would otherwise reach him. Over time, the characters become the Throne's functioning nervous system — and the question of what happens when Preston dies becomes personal rather than abstract.

**Expected macro-clocks.** Catskill Throne Tribute (the characters enforce and advance it). Pine Barrens Expansion (a regional threat the Throne must position against). Federal Continuity Erosion (reshapes Preston's strategic environment).

**Likely endgame triggers.** Preston's death — from age, injury, or eldritch exposure — forces the succession question the Throne has not answered. The characters become either kingmakers, contenders, or the people who try to hold the Throne together through a transition that was not planned.

### Frame Three: The Kin Before the Nation

The characters are Species I, members of one or more kin-networks, at a moment when their diaspora is deciding whether to become a polity. Their work is internal politics, external negotiation, and the slow daily labor of holding a community together across two million people and six major kin-networks.

**Opening situation.** Tomás Reyes has called for a vote. The quarterly assembly next month will debate whether to begin formal polity-declaration proceedings. Elder Flores opposes. Three kin-networks are undecided. The characters are dispatched — some by Reyes, some by Flores, some by undecided networks — to shape the vote. Their success or failure determines whether a new regional polity emerges in the next year or the next decade.

**Core tensions.** The Species I diaspora has held together because it is not a polity. Becoming a polity changes everything — it creates a target for hostile factions, requires formal institutions that currently do not exist, forces decisions about territory and sovereignty that most kin-members do not want to make. The characters work in the space where internal disagreement meets external pressure, where the kin's survival is at stake in ways the kin itself cannot quite articulate.

**Expected macro-clocks.** Species I Consolidation (the central clock). Federal Continuity Erosion (affects what polity-declaration would mean). Iron Crown vs Staten (resolves hostile-faction capacity to oppose consolidation). Currency Stability (determines commercial context for new polity).

**Likely endgame triggers.** Consolidation firing creates the new polity — and the characters become its founding generation, for good and ill. Consolidation failing, after prolonged effort, produces a different kind of story: the diaspora preserved, but at a cost, and with the knowledge that the question will return.

## Session Zero Procedure

Before the first session, resolve the following with the players:

**What faction affiliations do the characters hold?** This determines much of what follows. Allied affiliations make starting relationships easier; mixed or opposed affiliations create interesting tensions but require careful handling.

**What are the characters' manifestation profiles?** Use the power system in Chapter 2. Confirm each character's dominant domain, secondary (if any), tertiary (if any), and overall tier. Most starting characters should be T3 to T5 — competent but not regionally significant. A single T6 character in a starting party is acceptable; multiple T6s or a T7 shifts the scale of threats upward.

**What are the characters' species?** Roughly one in ten characters in a realistic party will be a metahuman species member. The specific species matters mechanically (species traits) and narratively (diaspora ties, prejudice, community obligations).

**What did the characters lose in the Onset?** Every character old enough to have lived through the Onset lost something or someone. This is shared background and it shapes who the character is now. For characters too young to have lived through the Onset, the question becomes: what did their family lose, and how does that family's loss shape them?

**What does each character want?** The short-term goal — what the character is trying to do in the first several sessions — grounds play. Long-term ambitions can develop through play. Short-term goals should be specific enough that the game-master can introduce opportunities and obstacles.

**What are the campaign's opening stakes?** Choose one of the three frames, or a custom frame, and describe the immediate situation. Make sure every character has a reason to be present and a reason to care about the outcome.

Set the region, set the clocks (use the default values in this chapter or adjust based on preceding fiction), and begin.

# APPENDIX B: TIMELINE

A condensed chronology from the Onset through game start, with projected horizon events for game-master reference.

## Pre-Onset

The world as it was. Not the subject of this book, but assumed: industrial civilization, electronics, global trade, none of the species, none of the Warped, no manifestation. Most adult characters remember it. Many grieve it.

## Year One A.O. — The Collapse and the Settling

**Day 1 (late May, the Onset).** Every electronic device in the region fails. Approximately one million people die in the first three hours. Forty-eight million people manifest powers; four point eight million transform into one of the ten species; four hundred and eighty thousand transform into eldritch entities.

**Week 1.** Water distribution fails in the major cities. Federal Continuity Command begins assembly at Andrews. Cholera begins in Queens. The Inner Harbor Church healers begin operating in Philadelphia. Antonín Volk seizes Port Newark.

**Week 2.** Mass urban exodus begins. Highway violence at the bridges. The Peekskill reactor approaches criticality; Dmitri Kostas arrives and works forty hours, two breakthroughs during the work.

**Week 3.** Cholera and typhoid become endemic. The first reports of *something in the trees* reach Pine Barrens settlements. The Listening cult is founded.

**Week 4.** Twenty to twenty-five percent of the pre-Onset NYC metro is dead. The Iron Crown consolidates. Tower Lord territorial negotiations begin. Federal Continuity formalizes under Alcott.

**Month 2.** Hudson Valley stabilizes. Preston manifests fully and rises through three breakthroughs to T9, founding the Catskill Throne. Manhattan exodus violence peaks. The Echo begins to be noticed in the Financial District.

**Month 3.** Most factions name themselves and take their shapes. The Central Jersey League founds under Okafor. Tower Lord arrangement consolidates. Staten Citadel converts the harbor.

**Months 4-6.** Starvation declines as biokinetic agriculture begins. The Iron Crown's first major Manhattan salvage expedition. The Iron Crown attempts to seize a Species H workshop in Newark; the courier network blockade follows. Winter arrives. Twelve percent winter mortality across the region. Factions surviving winter intact emerge as the stable powers.

**Months 7-9.** Biokinetic agricultural techniques diffuse. Bourse copper currency issues for the first time. First Iron Crown-Staten confrontation. Bourse mediation.

**Months 10-12.** Species I quarterly assembly; Reyes argues for polity declaration; Flores opposes; vote deferred. Federal Continuity reach contracts; Baltimore consolidation mission fails. Reeve becomes more independent. The Listening's first formal territorial claim through three simultaneous transformed-messenger deliveries. Pine Barrens declared off-limits.

## Year Two A.O. — Game Start

The early summer. Sixteen million people in the region, distributed among approximately 290 polities. The Lordly Equilibrium holds. Ten clocks measure the tensions that will break it. Every clock has at least one segment filled.

## Projected Horizon Events

The following are likely or possible regional events on a multi-year horizon. Use them as the long shadow against which player action takes place.

**Years 2-3.** Iron Crown-Staten war probable. Currency Stability and Biokinetic Agricultural Saturation likely to fire. Federal Continuity Erosion likely to begin its terminal phase.

**Years 3-5.** Federal Continuity fractures. Reeve declares independence or de facto operates as such. New regional polity formation likely — Species I consolidation, Delmarva consolidation, or both. Catskill Throne tribute consolidation probable.

**Years 5-10.** Pine Barrens expansion crisis. The Listening either breaches the Delaware line and provokes coordinated regional response, or expands more slowly and is negotiated with. Manhattan eldritch breach possible.

**Years 10-20.** First post-Onset-born manifestation cohort reaches adulthood. Population recovery becomes structural. Literacy collapse stabilizes or continues; the answer determines what the next century looks like. Preston dies; the Throne fractures or transitions.

**Years 20-30.** Regional political map fully reformed. Most pre-Onset institutional memory has died with its bearers. The new polities, the new currencies, the new religions are the established order. The post-Onset world has become the world.

# APPENDIX C: GLOSSARY

Terms in current use in the region.

**A.O.** *After Onset.* The dating system in regional use. Year One A.O. began at the summer solstice following the Onset.

**Aberrant.** An eldritch entity exhibiting biology that violates ordinary terrestrial form — multi-limbed, tumorous, irregular in size or feature. Tier 4 to 7 equivalent.

**Anomalous.** An eldritch entity displaying behavior or knowledge that cannot be explained by human origin. References cosmologies that do not align with any human framework.

**Auratic.** The continuous domain of manifestation — fear-fields, calm-fields, trust-fields. Effects that emanate from the user as a state rather than as an action.

**The Before.** Pre-Onset era. Common usage across all factions.

**Biokinetic.** Common name for Biological/Vital domain practitioners, particularly healers and agricultural specialists.

**Bourse copper.** The accepted regional currency, issued by the Philadelphia Bourse from accumulated pre-Onset metal and Lehigh mine output.

**Burnout.** Collapse state from sustained power overuse. Varies by domain. Hours to days of incapacity, occasionally permanent.

**Charged.** Of territory: significantly altered by sustained manifestation use, displaying physical or perceptual anomalies. *Kinetic-charged* zones have warped geometries; *biological-charged* zones have altered fauna and flora; *eldritch-charged* zones are dangerous to enter.

**Chit.** Iron Crown scrip, accepted only within Crown territory and at significant discount. By extension, slang for any untrustworthy money.

**Cluster (Hive).** A coordinated colony of Hive nodes, twenty to two thousand, operating as a distributed organism.

**Cluster (Eldritch).** A coordinated grouping of aberrant eldritch entities, sometimes operating with apparent collective cognition. The Hollow Choir is the regional archetype.

**Continuity scrip.** Federal Continuity Command paper currency. Trades against Bourse copper at roughly ten to one.

**Corruption.** Permanent personal alteration from sustained heavy power use. Differs by domain.

**Demesne.** A lord's holding, particularly a biokinetic agricultural one.

**The Echo.** The eldritch Sovereign in the Lower Manhattan Financial District.

**Eldritch.** The transformed survivors of the one and a half percent of the population who became no longer human at the Onset. Subdivided into wretches, hunters, aberrants, sovereigns, shades, and anomalous.

**Federation.** Term for the Central Jersey League's structure — fifteen townships under the Princeton Accord. Used as a model in informal political discussion.

**First-light.** Morning meal. Term emerged in Federal Continuity refugee camps; now general.

**The Hive.** Mobile, aggregating plant-organisms expanding across the region. Major colonies named individually.

**Hunter.** An eldritch entity of T3 to T4 combat capacity; predatory, pack-hunting.

**Iron Crown.** The warlord state of Newark, ruled by Antonín Volk.

**Kin.** A Species I extended family network, named for its eldest elder or originating family line. Six major kin-networks in the regional diaspora.

**Late-food.** Evening meal.

**The Line.** Any border between factions. The *Black Pines Line* is the Pine Barrens perimeter; the *42nd Street Line* is the boundary between intact Midtown and anomalous Lower Manhattan.

**The Listening.** The cult organized around the One Who Waits.

**Lordly Equilibrium.** The current regional political condition. No single power dominates; none is collapsing.

**Manifest.** Verb. To first display powers. *He manifested at puberty; she manifested at the Onset; she has not yet manifested.*

**Manifestation.** The general phenomenon of post-Onset powers.

**Onset.** The day every electronic device in the region failed and ninety-five percent of humans gained powers. Sometimes called the Shaking, particularly in religious contexts.

**Onsetist.** Adherent of the philosophical-religious position that the Onset was a transformative event with theological meaning.

**Perceptual.** The mental domain of manifestation — telepathy, empathy, illusion, perception enhancement.

**Profile.** A character's complete manifestation description: dominant domain, secondary, tertiary, and overall tier.

**Quiet, the.** Anti-manifestation movement holding that manifestation was a mistake or curse. Concentrated in Federal Continuity-adjacent territory.

**Retinue.** A warlord or lord's standing armed force; usually T3+ manifested individuals on personal retainer.

**Salvage.** Recovery of pre-Onset goods from ruins. Manhattan is the largest source; the Iron Crown and Tower Lords compete for primary access.

**Sojourn.** Term of Catskill Throne service. Petitioners may apply for sojourn — a fixed-term residency at the Throne, often as test of fitness for retinue.

**Sovereign.** An eldritch entity with cognition and territorial behavior. About three hundred in the region; thirty named.

**Species (capitalized).** A metahuman species. *Species I, Species C,* etc. The ten clusters established by the Onset.

**Tier.** Single-number assessment of overall manifestation capacity, T1 through T10+.

**To tier.** To breakthrough — to advance to the next tier through depth, breadth, or integration. *He tiered last spring.*

**To burn out.** To suffer power-overuse collapse. *She burned out at the Hudson crossing.*

**Tower Lords.** The five warlords of outer Brooklyn districts. Collective name for a political situation.

**Tributary.** Of polities: paying formal tribute (typically grain, work-horses, conscripts) to a sovereign without formal subordination of legal authority.

**Vassal.** Of polities: formally subordinated under a sovereign, with ceded legal authority. The Catskill Throne Tribute clock measures the transition from tributary to vassal status in the Hudson Valley.

**Warped.** An animal manifested at sufficient tier to display elevated cognition. T6 and above are sentient; T7 and above are superhuman. Approximately thirty-seven thousand T6+ in the mid-Atlantic.

**Wretch.** The most common eldritch type. Low-function humanoid entities, T1 to T2 combat equivalent.

# APPENDIX E: CORRUPTION AESTHETICS BY DOMAIN

Quick reference for what *charged* zones — territory significantly altered by sustained manifestation use — look and feel like. Useful for descriptions of warlord seats, demesnes worked by lifelong specialists, and the residue of major confrontations.

## Kinetic-Charged Zones

The geometry is wrong. Walls are not quite plumb. Corners are not quite ninety degrees. Stones in the road have been stepped on so many times by something heavy that the road itself has memory of the steps. Dust does not settle the way it should — it rests in patterns suggestive of motion that has already happened. Sounds carry strangely; a footstep in one room is sometimes audible in another, then absent, then audible again from a different direction.

The Lower East Side ruin in Manhattan is the regional archetype. Volk's audience hall in Newark is a smaller, controlled version; Preston's training yard at Mount Tremper is the largest non-ruined example.

## Matter-Charged Zones

Surfaces are too smooth or too rough. Iron in the area sings faintly at certain frequencies. Pre-existing manufactured objects have begun to take on slight asymmetries that match the affinity of the working practitioner — stone-shaper zones have rounded edges everywhere; metal-shaper zones have unexpected grooves and ridges. Weather behaves oddly: lightning preferentially strikes in metal-shaper territory, fire is harder to extinguish in pyrokinetic zones.

Kostas's containment chamber at Peekskill is the regional archetype. The Species C Flushing master workshops have a milder version; Master Iwo Kuan's bench is centered in a faint zone of perfect smoothness.

## Biokinetic-Charged Zones

Plants thrive disproportionately. Small animals are calm and unusually willing to approach humans. Wounds heal faster, even in non-biokinetics passing through. Biological time runs slightly faster — fruit ripens quicker, hair grows faster, scars fade. The smell is different: vegetal, slightly sweet, the air seems wetter.

Yonkers Healing Hall is the regional archetype. The Delmarva demesnes operate in milder versions across thousands of acres; "Old Hat" Archer's lands in northern Delmarva are notable for atmospheric calm extending several kilometers from his residence.

## Temporal-Spatial-Charged Zones

Distances do not match measurement. A walk that should be one hundred steps takes ninety, or one hundred and ten. Doors lead to rooms slightly displaced from their architectural drawings. People entering and exiting feel mild disorientation that fades in minutes. Time runs perceptibly differently — meetings that should take an hour take forty-five minutes by the clock but feel like ninety.

The Staten Citadel's harbor staging area is the regional archetype, though it is a controlled and useful version. The Fairmount Park wildland in Philadelphia has an uncontrolled version produced by an unknown long-departed practitioner. The Echo's territory in Lower Manhattan is the extreme uncontrolled case; do not go there.

## Perceptual-Charged Zones

Thoughts do not stay where they are placed. The user feels her own internal monologue echo as a faint external whisper. Strong emotions of others nearby are felt as her own. Group decisions made in such zones tend to converge more readily than they should. The character of a perceptual-charged zone matches the character of the practitioner — Beatrice Amar's Throne Hall is calm and clarifying; the Iron Crown's interrogation rooms are dread-saturated and produce confessions readily.

The Catskill Throne Hall is the regional archetype of the benign version. Petra Voss's interrogation chamber in Newark is the malign version.

## Eldritch-Charged Zones

The descriptions begin to break down here. Light comes from the wrong angle. Objects seen in peripheral vision are not what they appear when looked at directly. Sounds suggest words in languages no one in the area speaks. People develop coherent, vivid memories of events that did not happen. Time passes strangely and not always in the same direction. Sleep in such zones produces dreams that are shared among multiple sleepers without any apparent communication.

The Pine Barrens core is the regional archetype. The Echo's Manhattan territory is a different but equally dangerous variant. The Camden Alley-King's range carries a partial eldritch-adjacent character despite being Warped rather than eldritch in origin.

## Auratic-Charged Zones

The mood of the place persists when the practitioner is absent. A fear-warlord's residence remains uncomfortable to be in even when he is far away. A trust-merchant's hall continues to make truthful negotiation easier even at night when she sleeps. A calm-mediator's home cannot sustain a real argument; visiting families resolve their conflicts on the porch and then come inside.

Preston's Mount Tremper command site, the Philadelphia Bourse great chamber, and "Old Hat" Archer's residence are three regional archetypes of three different auratic charges.

# THE ELDRITCH

The eldritch is the part of the post-Onset world that does not cohere with the rest.

## The Question

Roughly 1.5% of the manifested population transformed into non-humanoid forms at Onset — approximately 480,000 in the mid-Atlantic originally, reduced through attrition and death to about 225,000 at T+1. These are the eldritch.

The central question about them is whether they are human.

The majority answer is yes: they are the products of Onset transformation acting on humans in the same way that the metahuman species transformation did, producing outcomes that happened to be non-humanoid and often cognitively diminished. These are the **ordinary eldritch**. Their biology is weird but traceable to human origin. They die. They can be killed. They do not reproduce. The survivors of the first years are the first years' survivors — the eldritch population is aging slowly and not replacing itself.

The minority answer is that some of them are not human.

Approximately 15% of observed eldritch entities — roughly 33,700 of the 225,000 — display behaviors and carry knowledge that cannot be explained by human origin. They reference entities, places, or events not in any human record. They speak languages that have never been spoken by any human population. Their stated purposes refer to structures, histories, or cosmologies that do not align with any scientific or religious framework of human origin. And crucially, **the referenced content is mutually contradictory across different anomalous entities.** Their statements do not cohere into a single hidden picture. If they were speaking the truth, there are many truths, and they do not agree.

These are the **anomalous eldritch**. What they actually are is unresolved.

Four theories compete, none privileged:

**The Intrusion Theory.** Entities from elsewhere entered during the Onset event. A small number of "originals" and their products are genuinely non-human; the manifestation wave was the mechanism or the side effect of their arrival. The contradictions between their accounts reflect multiple factions of arrivals with different origins.

**The Contamination Theory.** Human origin is real but the Onset attached fragments of other minds, other knowledge, to transforming humans. The anomalous eldritch are humans who have been partially replaced. The contradictions reflect the fragmentation — no single anomalous mind arrived, only pieces that recombined in each host differently.

**The Confabulation Theory.** The entities are genuinely human-origin but have manufactured mythologies their fragmented cognition believes. The contradictions prove that none of it is real. What looks like pre-human knowledge is post-Onset invention that the eldritch host cannot distinguish from memory.

**The Recursive Theory.** The Onset itself was caused by something, and the anomalous entities carry fragmentary data about its nature. Their accounts are incoherent because the cause is not comprehensible in human terms. The anomalous eldritch are messengers without a message — or with a message the messenger cannot pronounce.

No theory is privileged. The simulation engine treats the anomaly as permanently unresolved. Surviving scientific and religious communities are divided across all four positions.

## Ordinary Eldritch

**Wretches (majority, ~60%).** Low-function humanoid or partially-humanoid entities, the most common result of Onset-transformation. Retain some physical characteristics of human origin — bilateral symmetry, recognizable limbs, occasionally fragments of clothing still adhered. Individually weak (T1–T2 combat equivalent, often less), but group-hunting and resistance to conventional harm make them dangerous in numbers. Most exist at the human-agriculture border, preying on livestock and isolated travelers. They do not reproduce. The regional Wretch population is declining.

**Hunters (~20%).** Faster, more intact, more capable. Bipedal or quadrupedal; often human-sized or larger. Recognizable predatory behavior — stalking, ambushing, returning to kill sites. Hunter packs in Pine Barrens and the Catskills are the primary threat to frontier settlements. T3–T4 combat equivalent; hunt in small packs of 2–6. Hunters do not reproduce conventionally, but new Hunters appear in regions of heavy eldritch presence. The mechanism is not understood.

**Aberrants (~10%).** Entities that violate ordinary biology more dramatically — multi-limbed, tumorous, size-irregular, or exhibiting visibly non-terrestrial features. Rarer but more dangerous than Hunters. Pine Barrens hosts the highest concentration. Individual combat capability varies (T4–T7 equivalent). Some Aberrants exhibit rudimentary group coordination or territorial behavior.

**Shades (~5%).** Incorporeal or semi-incorporeal. Manifest as presence-without-body — cold zones, wrong-sounds, shadows that move incorrectly. Cannot be harmed by conventional means; believed to be harmed only by auratic, perceptual, or eldritch effects. Most Shades are non-aggressive and haunt specific locations. A small minority actively target humans; these are the most feared eldritch relative to their frequency.

## Anomalous Eldritch

**Sovereigns (~5% of all eldritch; effectively all anomalous).** Rare entities with evident cognition and strategic behavior. Territorial niches, population networks of lesser entities, consistent long-term behavior patterns. Roughly 150–300 Sovereigns exist in the mid-Atlantic; about 30 are individually known to faction intelligence.

Named Sovereigns:

**The One Who Waits.** Pine Barrens. Appears intermittently as a humanoid figure ~3.5 m tall, robed in what appears to be bark and lichen. Speaks languages unknown. Central to The Listening cult. Has not been observed to kill anyone directly in three years of contact; cultists who have spoken with it undergo progressive transformation. Claims knowledge of pre-Onset events it should not have. One of the most clearly anomalous entities in the region.

**The Echo.** Financial District, Lower Manhattan. Never directly observed. Presence manifests as lost time, confused memories, and occasional physical movement of objects near the Federal Reserve area. Anyone who spends more than ~2 hours in its presence zone loses fragmentary memories and acquires confused temporal-spatial impressions. Has not been observed to expand its territory in four years.

**Grandfather Silt.** Chesapeake eastern shore, Nanticoke River mouth. Estimated 8–12 meters; inhabits submerged waters. Has sunk at least three salvage vessels. The Crabclaw Confederation maintains a no-go zone of ~400 km² around the presumed territory.

**The Hollow Choir.** Outer Catskills. A cluster of 15–20 Aberrant entities operating with apparent coordination, moving together across the eastern Catskill range. No single leader. Produces coordinated sub-audible vocalizations that carry across valleys and cause acute unease in baseline humans who hear them.

**The Broker.** Outer Philadelphia (Camden side). An Aberrant Sovereign that occasionally appears at specific salvage sites and attempts to communicate — crude written symbols, gesture, offered objects. It wants something from human salvagers, not yet understood. Not believed to be hostile; offered objects include pre-Onset mechanical items in various states of repair.

Other unnamed Sovereigns operate in specific territorial niches throughout the region and are tracked informally by faction intelligence.

## Encounter Types

Encounters with the eldritch fall into recognizable types:

**Wretch ambush.** A pack of 4–8 Wretches swarms a small group — isolated travelers, a farmhouse at night, a salvage crew working late. Combat is close-range, messy, and the Wretches die easily but take a toll through sheer numbers. Most faction-adjacent settlements have local knowledge about which approach routes are Wretch-prone.

**Hunter stalking.** A Hunter pack tracks a target for days across wilderness. The target notices they are being watched; if they stop or hole up, the pack attacks; if they move efficiently, the pack sometimes disengages. Hunter engagements are closer to conventional combat than Wretch encounters — recognizable predator-prey dynamics.

**Aberrant contact.** An Aberrant emerges at a specific location and is either territorial (defending a zone) or opportunistic (attacking passing targets). Combat is highly variable depending on the Aberrant's form and capabilities. Most Aberrants can be killed by T5+ combatants with sufficient numerical advantage, but some are individually equivalent to T7 opposition.

**Shade haunting.** A location becomes associated with a Shade presence. Travelers in the location feel cold, hear wrong sounds, see shadows that move incorrectly. Most Shades are not combat encounters — they are ambient unease. Active Shades target specific individuals, usually through dreams or induced illness.

**Sovereign encounter.** Contact with a named Sovereign. Most Sovereign encounters are not combat. They are communicative — the Sovereign wants something, offers something, warns something. The Listening messengers arriving at a township to deliver a phrase; The Broker offering a mechanism in exchange for unknown consideration; a Sovereign's territorial warning to an encroaching faction. Violence with Sovereigns is rare and usually catastrophic — most are effectively T7–T8 equivalent and some are outside the tier framework entirely.

**Anomalous event.** Something happens that cannot be reduced to entity action — a building in Lower Manhattan shifts overnight without witness, a salvage expedition returns one day early (or one day late) without anyone noticing the discrepancy, a memory that three witnesses share does not match the physical record. These are the Echo-class phenomena generalized.

## Aesthetic Register

The eldritch in description should be wrong, not spectacular.

A Wretch is not a monster. It is a shape that used to be a person, shaped now into something smaller and hungrier. When described, the fragments that survive transformation are the handle — a shred of shirt, a hand still recognizable, a piece of a shoe. The horror is the intimation of what was lost, not the description of what remains.

A Hunter moves like an animal that learned to move like a predator. It is quiet. Its posture is low. When it runs, the gait is efficient and not human. The fear it produces is specific — the sense that something is hunting you and is better at hunting than you are at noticing.

An Aberrant is physically non-standard. The description should be sparing — a detail or two that do not match (too many joints; a mouth where a mouth should not be; symmetry that is subtly broken). Elaboration reduces horror. Restraint intensifies it.

A Shade's phenomenology is atmospheric. Cold that has no source. A sound that might have been a voice but was not quite a voice. A shadow on a wall when no one is standing to cast it. The body reports these things; the mind finds them disturbing and cannot quite articulate why.

A Sovereign is more specific and therefore more dangerous to describe well. The One Who Waits is tall and robed in organic matter; its face is not clearly seen by anyone who has been close enough to report; it speaks a language nobody else speaks. The Broker is quadrupedal and asymmetric; its written symbols are consistent across appearances but incomprehensible. Grandfather Silt is immense and submerged; what the sinking ships saw before they went down is not recorded.

The register that works is **grounded**. The eldritch is a fact of the world. The people who encounter it do not scream and faint. They go still. They try to leave. They remember specific sensory details and cannot reconstruct the whole. The horror is proprioceptive — the body knowing something is wrong before the mind can name what.

## Narrative Deployment (GM-facing)

The eldritch is load-bearing for the setting's tone but should be used sparingly at the encounter level. A campaign that features weekly eldritch encounters will make the eldritch ordinary; a campaign that features it once every several sessions maintains its weight.

Deployment guidelines:

**Wretches are common in specific regions.** Pine Barrens perimeter, Catskill wilderness, ruined urban zones at night. A journey through those regions should produce Wretch encounters naturally. A journey through the Delmarva agricultural belt should not.

**Hunters are rarer and more personal.** A Hunter pack tracking the party is a multi-session arc, not a random encounter. Hunters escalate; the party either resolves them or the pack grows.

**Aberrants are individual encounters with individual character.** Each named Aberrant in the mid-Atlantic is a specific entity; an encounter with the Parliament of Crows is not the same as an encounter with the Camden Alley-King. Aberrants should have motivations, habits, and recognizable behaviors across encounters.

**Shades are atmosphere first, threat second.** Most Shade encounters are descriptive — the party passes through a haunted zone, notices the phenomena, and either leaves or is marked. Combat with Shades is rare and usually requires specific preparation (a biokinetic with auratic capacity, an Eldritch-domain user, or a fully-transformed cultist).

**Sovereign encounters are campaign events.** Meeting The One Who Waits is not an encounter; it is a chapter. Meeting The Broker is not a transaction; it is a recurring relationship. Sovereigns should appear rarely and consequentially.

**Anomalous events should not be explained.** The party notices the discrepancy; they are left with it. Explanation is the death of the anomalous. A session where the party realizes they remember the wrong date and the calendar and physical evidence disagree is a session that stays with the players — if no explanation is provided.

The Listening is the exception that proves several rules at once. It is the region's most accessible ongoing eldritch conflict; its messengers appear regularly at the Pine Barrens perimeter; its cult absorbs the unwary; its territorial expansion is a regional problem factions discuss. A campaign can reasonably feature The Listening as a recurring element. Other Sovereigns should appear less often.

## Corruption Pathways

Four pathways are observed through which non-transformed humans come into the eldritch:

**Voluntary pact.** The user contacts an entity (usually a Sovereign or an unnamed lesser-Aberrant with cognition) and accepts a bargain. Effects are granted in exchange for obligations. Known pact-effects include: Eldritch-domain capacities (usually T3–T5 in primary-Eldritch form), marks that allow perception of related entities, projection of cursed or anomalous effects on others. The cost is cumulative. Each use deepens the obligation. Extended pact-users transform progressively; the transformation is usually irreversible once it crosses a threshold.

**Listening absorption.** A human joins The Listening cult. The cult provides shelter, food, community. In exchange, the convert accepts exposure to One Who Waits' presence and gradual transformation. Early-stage converts can sometimes defect (Seer Willem is the known example); later-stage converts cannot. The transformation endpoint is a fully eldritch form aligned with the cult's purposes.

**Exposure corruption.** Prolonged presence in eldritch territory (Pine Barrens interior, the Financial District, Grandfather Silt's zone) accumulates effects. The user begins to display minor eldritch traits — unusual sensitivities, fragmentary memories not their own, subtle physical changes. Short exposure is reversible; long exposure is not.

**Direct contact with an anomalous object.** A small number of objects recovered from eldritch territories carry residual anomalous-subset character. Sustained possession produces gradual corruption. Species C Master Kuan holds one such object and studies it on specific days; the long-term effect on him is not yet visible but is being watched.

Corruption is never presented as a power upgrade in setting-appropriate terms. It is a cost that is paid for specific effects. The effects may be useful; the user who pays the cost is trading selfhood for capacity. Long-term users do not return. Short-term users sometimes do.

What is not observed: reliable "curing" of eldritch corruption once it has progressed past the early stages. Several attempts have been made. Results have been mixed and occasionally catastrophic.
